The dark matter / dark energy problem is one of the most literature-rich zones in modern physics. Orthodox ΛCDM invokes two unexplained components to fit observations. A long list of alternative programs attempts to dispense with at least one. Where does τ's "no dark sectors" claim sit in this landscape?

## What the prior art claims

Alternative-gravity programs for the dark-matter problem fall into several groups:

- **Milgrom's MOND (Modified Newtonian Dynamics)**, 1983 onward. For accelerations below a characteristic scale a₀ ≈ 1.2 × 10⁻¹⁰ m/s², Newton's second law is modified: F = m·μ(a/a₀)·a with μ(x) → 1 for x ≫ 1 and μ(x) → x for x ≪ 1. This reproduces flat galactic rotation curves and the Tully-Fisher relation without dark matter. MOND's successes at galactic scales are striking; its failures at cluster scales are widely acknowledged and drive most modern critiques.

- **Bekenstein's TeVeS (Tensor-Vector-Scalar gravity)**, 2004. A fully relativistic extension of MOND that preserves general covariance and addresses gravitational lensing. TeVeS is mathematically developed but has been tightly constrained by post-2015 gravitational wave observations (GW170817 in particular).

- **Verlinde's entropic gravity**, 2010/2016. Gravity as an emergent entropic force derived from holographic arguments. Verlinde's 2016 "Emergent Gravity and the Dark Universe" paper attempts to derive a MOND-like regime from holography, with explicit formulae connecting dark-matter-like phenomenology to quantum-information bounds on the cosmological horizon.

- **Moffat's MOG (Modified Gravity)**, 1990s onward. A scalar-tensor-vector theory that mimics dark matter at galactic and cluster scales.

- **Khoury-Weltman chameleon fields** and similar screening-mechanism modified-gravity programs.

- **Non-gravity approaches to dark energy.** Quintessence, f(R) gravity, DGP branes, dark energy as a modification of the Einstein-Hilbert action.

- **Dark-matter-particle programs.** WIMPs, axions, sterile neutrinos, primordial black holes, fuzzy dark matter. These are not alternative-gravity; they accept ΛCDM's ontology and propose candidates for the dark-matter particle.

The common shape of alternative-gravity programs: **modify the gravitational dynamics (Newton's law, the Einstein equation, or their equivalent) so that observed phenomena classified as "dark matter" or "dark energy" arise from the modified dynamics applied to visible matter**.

## What is structurally the same

A specialist in MOND, TeVeS, or Verlinde's program will recognize some shared territory:

- **Rejection of the dark-matter particle.** Both τ and the alternative-gravity family deny that dark matter is a new particle. This is the core shared commitment.
- **Galactic rotation curves are not fitted parameter-by-parameter.** MOND, MOG, and Verlinde's program all claim a single extra-dynamics feature that reproduces the observed Tully-Fisher relation across galaxies. τ's Wilson-loop holonomy claim is structurally analogous: one framework feature should reproduce the galaxy-scale phenomenology for all galaxies, not galaxy-by-galaxy.
- **Attempt at a unified account.** All these programs aspire to a single theoretical move that resolves dark matter, dark energy, or both together — rather than postulating separate fixes for each. τ is a member of this family.

## What is structurally different

1. **τ does not modify gravity; it reinterprets what the existing fields mean.** This is the most important structural difference. MOND modifies F = ma at low accelerations. TeVeS adds scalar and vector fields to the Einstein-Hilbert action. Verlinde modifies the gravitational force via entropic arguments. **τ leaves the gravitational dynamics untouched.** What τ changes is the *interpretation* of what the gravitational readout of τ³ corresponds to — the observed gravitational phenomena are real; τ claims the underlying accounting is via Wilson holonomy structure of τ³'s boundary, not via a dark-matter halo or modified law. This is a categorical ontological move, not a dynamical one.

2. **The Sector Exhaustion Theorem is not a MOND-type fit.** Alternative-gravity programs are typically judged by their ability to fit observed rotation curves, clusters, and cosmological observations. τ's claim is prior to fitting: **no fifth primary sector exists** (gravitational + electromagnetic + strong + weak + Higgs-crossing is exhaustive), so dark matter as a new *thing* is structurally forbidden. This is an existence-structural claim, not a dynamics-fitting claim.

3. **Cluster-scale problems do not return.** MOND is empirically famous for succeeding at galaxy-rotation-curve scale but failing at cluster-scale (Bullet Cluster, X-ray mass vs weak-lensing mass). τ's account, being categorical rather than dynamical, does not inherit MOND's cluster problem by construction — but **τ must still make cluster-scale predictions** and be judged by them. The cosmology briefing documents specific τ predictions at cluster and large-scale-structure scales (cosmic magnetic fields, JWST early SMBHs, S8 tension) that are live empirical tests.

4. **Cosmological constant explicitly dissolved, not mimicked.** MOND does not by itself address dark energy; TeVeS requires separate machinery. Verlinde's program addresses both. τ's Cosmological Constant Problem Dissolved (V.T139) is a direct structural theorem: vacuum energy is zero because τ-vacuum is the empty set of unoccupied sectors. This is neither a fit nor a modification of the gravitational equations — it is a statement about what "vacuum" *means* in τ's categorical setup.

5. **Relation to holography is opposite in direction.** Verlinde derives gravity-as-emergent from holographic arguments. τ derives holography (II.C01) from its kernel structure, and derives the "no dark sectors" consequence from Sector Exhaustion and boundary-character structure. Verlinde: gravity emerges from information-theoretic holography → dark-like phenomena follow. τ: categorical kernel produces both holography and sector exhaustion as separate consequences. The directionality and the route through holography are different.

6. **GR is preserved as the correct readout at the relevant scales.** τ does not predict deviations from GR in the regimes where GR has been tested (solar system, binary pulsars, gravitational waves). MOND, TeVeS, and Moffat's MOG all must carefully tune their modifications to avoid conflict with GR in tested regimes; τ avoids this by not modifying GR at all.

## What a specialist would want to see

A cosmologist, general relativist, or specialist in alternative-gravity programs should ask:

1. **Galaxy rotation curves from Wilson holonomy — explicit computation.** What is the Wilson-loop holonomy structure in τ, and how does it reproduce the Tully-Fisher relation quantitatively across galaxies? A rotation-curve comparison to MOND and to dark-matter-halo fits across a sample of galaxies is the standard phenomenological test.
2. **Bullet Cluster.** The Bullet Cluster is MOND's classical empirical problem: weak lensing and X-ray gas separate, and the lensing peak tracks collisionless rather than baryonic mass. What is τ's account of this observation? Does the categorical boundary-reading interpretation give the right spatial distribution?
3. **CMB peak structure.** ΛCDM fits the CMB acoustic peaks with standard cosmological parameters. τ claims the first peak at +69 ppm from ι_τ. Does τ reproduce the full peak structure (peaks 1, 2, 3, ...) with zero free parameters, or only peak 1?
4. **S8 and H0 tensions.** Both are claimed as resolved within τ's orbit-depth framework. What is the precise mechanism, and does it give numerical agreement with current data to within stated σ agreement?
5. **Sector Exhaustion Theorem.** What is the formal statement of Sector Exhaustion? The claim "no additional sector is structurally available" is very strong; the proof structure should be inspected at the Lean level. Is the argument a genuine impossibility result or a choice made at kernel construction?
6. **Vacuum energy = 0.** V.T139 says vacuum energy is structurally zero. What is the τ vacuum state, and how does "empty set of unoccupied sectors" translate into a concrete field-theoretic statement that a QFT specialist would recognize?
7. **Gravitational-wave observations.** GW170817 and its electromagnetic counterpart constrained the speed of gravitational waves to c within one part in 10¹⁵. TeVeS was severely constrained by this. τ claims no modification to gravitational dynamics — so the GW speed is c by construction. But does τ's boundary-reading make any specific prediction for (say) stochastic gravitational wave background, or primordial gravitational waves?
