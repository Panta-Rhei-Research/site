All three frontier-LLM first-pass assessments of the Panta Rhei framework converged on the same concern: **relabeling risk**. Many of the τ framework's load-bearing claims sit in literature-rich zones — quaternionic analysis, spectral approaches to the Riemann Hypothesis, octonionic Standard-Model constructions, formal definitions of life, alternative-gravity cosmologies. The honest first-contact question in each of these zones is not "is τ right?" but "is τ genuinely new, or is it a restatement of existing work in a novel vocabulary?"

This lane answers that question directly, one prior-art zone at a time. Each comparison page follows the same structure:

1. **What the prior art claims** — a specialist-level summary of the existing body of work, naming key authors and results.
2. **What τ claims in this territory** — the τ framework's corresponding claim, with registry IDs and canonical-book references.
3. **What is structurally the same** — explicit acknowledgment of overlap. Where prior art and τ agree on the shape of the answer or the method.
4. **What is structurally different** — specific, concrete differences. Not marketing differentiation; technical separations.
5. **What is genuinely new (claimed)** — the content that would justify a novelty claim if the framework holds, stated carefully.
6. **What a specialist would want to see** — concrete asks a domain expert should make before adjudicating the comparison. This is where the framework acknowledges that it cannot settle its own priority questions.

The pages are written in the spirit of honest audit, not marketing. Where τ genuinely overlaps with prior art, this is stated directly. Where τ claims genuine novelty, the burden of proof is named. No specialist in these fields would find the comparisons *themselves* dispositive — they are first-contact artifacts, designed to help a specialist decide whether a deeper engagement is warranted.

## The five comparison pages

### [τ-Holomorphy vs Fueter-Regular Analysis]({{ '/framework/prior-art/tau-holomorphy-vs-fueter/' | relative_url }})

The Central Theorem 𝒪(τ³) ≅ A_spec(𝕃) lives in territory historically occupied by **Fueter-regular quaternionic analysis** and its many successors (slice regularity, discrete Cauchy-Fueter theory, quaternionic and octonionic holomorphy). Is the τ-bulk-to-boundary correspondence a new theorem or a reading of something already known?

### [Spectral ζ vs Hilbert-Pólya / Connes / Berry-Keating]({{ '/framework/prior-art/spectral-zeta-vs-hilbert-polya/' | relative_url }})

Book III's spectral approach to the Riemann Hypothesis sits in the territory of the **Hilbert-Pólya conjecture**, **Connes' adelic spectral interpretation** (1996), **Berry-Keating's H = xp heuristic**, and **de Branges spaces**. Is τ's Critical Line Theorem a new path to RH or a recasting of one of these programs?

### [Three Generations vs Octonionic / Twistor / Preon]({{ '/framework/prior-art/three-generations-vs-octonionic/' | relative_url }})

τ derives three fermion generations from H₁(τ³;ℤ) ≅ ℤ³. This competes with **Cohl Furey's octonionic Standard-Model constructions**, **Dixon's ℝ⊗ℂ⊗ℍ⊗𝕆 triality**, twistor approaches, and preon models. Is the three-generation claim structurally distinct from these or is it an isomorphic relabeling?

### [τ-Life Predicate vs Autopoiesis / IIT / FEP]({{ '/framework/prior-art/tau-life-vs-autopoiesis-iit-fep/' | relative_url }})

τ defines life as τ-Distinction + SelfDesc (VI.D04 + VI.D06). The field already contains **Maturana-Varela autopoiesis**, **Integrated Information Theory**, **Friston's Free Energy Principle**, **Kauffman's autocatalytic sets**, and **Rosen's (M,R)-systems**. Is the τ predicate a genuinely new formal criterion or another entry in a crowded taxonomy?

### [No Dark Sectors vs MOND / Verlinde / Entropic Gravity]({{ '/framework/prior-art/no-dark-sectors-vs-mond-verlinde/' | relative_url }})

The τ-cosmological claim that dark matter and dark energy are structural readout artifacts, not new particles, sits in the space of **Milgrom's MOND**, **TeVeS**, **Verlinde's entropic gravity**, and other modified-gravity programs. Is τ's "no dark sectors" a variant of these or a structurally different move?

## How to use these pages

- **If you are a specialist in one of the five domains:** treat the comparison page as a first-contact artifact. It will not convince you of anything. It should tell you whether the framework has at least engaged your literature seriously, and whether the claimed differences are technical or rhetorical.
- **If you are a non-specialist reader:** the pages are designed to be legible without domain expertise, but the "what a specialist would want to see" sections point at exactly the questions you should bring to a domain expert if you want to pursue the comparison further.
- **If you are a first-pass assessor (human or LLM):** these pages are the framework's response to the "relabeling risk" concern raised in the frontier-LLM assessments of April 2026. They do not settle the concern; they provide the material required to evaluate it.

## What these pages do NOT claim

- They do not claim τ is novel *because* it differs from prior art. Novelty is a content claim that specialists must adjudicate.
- They do not claim τ's differences from prior art are *improvements*. Different is not better.
- They do not cover every prior-art zone τ touches. Five of the most literature-rich zones were selected; other domains (topos-theoretic foundations, homotopy type theory, structuralist philosophy of mathematics) deserve their own pages and are on the engagement-lane backlog.
- They do not replace peer review. A prior-art comparison written by the framework's authors cannot establish priority. External specialist engagement remains the required next step.
