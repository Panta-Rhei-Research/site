## Publication Ontology

The Panta Rhei Research Program publishes through several complementary surfaces. Each serves a different purpose in the program's commitment to public inspectability.

### The Seven Books

The canonical monograph series — organized in proof-order, with each book earning its content from what came before:

| Book | Title | Subtitle |
|:----:|-------|----------|
| I | Categorical Foundations | *How Mathematics Is Earned* |
| II | Categorical Holomorphy | *Finite Readouts of Infinity* |
| III | Categorical Spectrum | *Where Physics Lives* |
| IV | Categorical Microcosm | *The Self-Describing Universe* |
| V | Categorical Macrocosm | *The Biography of the Universe* |
| VI | Categorical Life | *Life as Self-Decoding Distinctions* |
| VII | Categorical Metaphysics | *The Final Self-Enrichment* |

Available as hardcover, paperback, and Kindle on Amazon KDP.

### Guided Tour Whitepapers

Seven structural falsification guides (one per book) that identify the 5-8 load-bearing hinges of each volume and show exactly how to attack them. Available at [panta-rhei.site](https://panta-rhei.site).

### TauLib Formalization

The Lean 4 formalization — 450 modules, 125,771 lines, zero sorry in Books I-VI. Available at:
- Active development: [github.com/Panta-Rhei-Framework/taulib](https://github.com/Panta-Rhei-Framework/taulib)
- Frozen verification: [github.com/Panta-Rhei-Framework/formalization](https://github.com/Panta-Rhei-Framework/formalization)

### Companion Papers

44 public-good deployment papers across 11 impact portfolios. Available in the [Impact lane](/impact/).

## How Publications Relate to the Site

The books carry the canonical argument. The Atlas (this site) provides a navigable public interface organized by understanding-order. TauLib provides machine-checked verification. The guided tours and companion papers lower the threshold for structured engagement.

These are not separate projects. They are different public surfaces of the same research program.
