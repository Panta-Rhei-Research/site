Part IV proved that every object in $τ$-Idx
has an ABCD encoding:
$X = ((\underlineA ↑↑ \underlineC)^{\underlineB})
· \underlineD$.
But existence alone is not enough.
If two different tuples
$(\underlineA, \underlineB, \underlineC, \underlineD)$
and
$(A', B', C', D')$
could yield the same $X$,
then the ABCD chart would not be a faithful coordinate system:
it would be a *surjection*,
not a *bijection*.
This chapter frames the uniqueness question precisely,
states the Hyperfactorization Theorem,
and outlines the three-lemma proof strategy
that the following chapters will execute.
