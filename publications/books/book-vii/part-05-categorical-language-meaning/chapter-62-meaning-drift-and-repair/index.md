Meanings are not fixed. They shift, narrow, broaden, elevate, and
degrade over time. This chapter formalizes semantic drift as
perturbation of the natural transformation between syntax and
subsymbolic pattern: the readout functor is not rigid, and small
perturbations accumulate into significant shifts. Repair
mechanisms—dictionaries, institutions, normative practices—are
modeled as gluing-condition enforcement that stabilizes the readout
functor against perturbation. Some drift is irreversible: language
evolves because the readout functor is a living map, not a frozen one.
