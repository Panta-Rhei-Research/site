Cities enable large-scale coordination when direct mutual recognition
cannot scale. They trade interpersonal depth for breadth of access,
using institutions, roles, and infrastructure to regulate interaction
among strangers. This chapter frames common urban phenomena —
anonymity, formality, neighbourhoods, weak ties, and cosmopolitan
coexistence — as structural consequences of thin overlaps and
mediated gluing in a social topology whose carrier set far exceeds
the Dunbar threshold of the relevant chapter.
