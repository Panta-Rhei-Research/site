Why does experience feel unified? This chapter answers with a single
structural thesis: consciousness is a global section of the mind sheaf.
Local neural processes yield local sections — percepts, affects,
motor readiness — and conscious experience arises when those local
sections glue into a single, coherent global section over the
integration domain. The Consciousness as Global Section theorem
(VII.T41) formalises this claim, and the Binding as Gluing lemma
(VII.L14) dissolves the classical binding problem by identifying
binding with the sheaf-theoretic gluing condition.
