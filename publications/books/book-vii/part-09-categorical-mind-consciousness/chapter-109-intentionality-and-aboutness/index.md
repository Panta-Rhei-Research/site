Mental states are directed: beliefs are about facts, desires are about
goals, fears are about threats. This chapter formalises intentionality
as morphism structure within the mind-topos. The Intentionality as
Morphism definition (VII.D84) models aboutness as an internal map from
a representation to its intentional object, anchored to the external
world by a structure-preserving translation. The framework separates
content from reference, clarifies misrepresentation and fictional
discourse, and reconnects Brentano's classical thesis to the
categorical architecture of $τ$.
