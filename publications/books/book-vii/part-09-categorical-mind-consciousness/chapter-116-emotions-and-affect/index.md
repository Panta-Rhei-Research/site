Mind is not purely cognitive — it is suffused with feeling. This
chapter develops the categorical account of emotions: emotions arise
when content crosses between registers, and affect is the subsymbolic
pressure that motivates such transitions. The Emotions as
Register-Crossings Theorem (VII.T44) identifies six canonical
crossings, each generating a recognisable emotional family. The
Affect Lemma (VII.L15) characterises affect as a pre-symbolic force
that orients the system toward register transitions before any
explicit evaluation occurs. The chapter connects to appraisal theory,
emotional intelligence, and the embodied dimension of feeling.
