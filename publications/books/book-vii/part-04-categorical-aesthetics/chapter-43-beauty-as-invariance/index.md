the relevant chapter established the structural
identification: a motif is beautiful if and only if the aesthetic
functional vanishes, i.e., the motif is invariant under all admissible
transformations. This chapter tests the identification against three
domains—mathematics, nature, and art—showing that in each case the
patterns universally recognised as beautiful are precisely those that
exhibit maximal or near-maximal invariance. The cross-domain convergence
is not coincidental: it is the aesthetic shadow of kernel invariants
projected through different readout functors.
