The bi-square of Book I (the Bi-Square Characterization, Book I, Theorem I.T41) is the algebraic seed of the entire series: a $2 × 3$ pasted diagram whose left square encodes tower coherence and whose right square encodes spectral naturality. Book II's Central Theorem (II.T40) lifted the bi-square from algebraic to topological, replacing presheaf values with holomorphic sections and spectral naturality with boundary-value duality on the lemniscate $\Lemniscate$. This chapter constructs the third bi-square—the *enriched bi-square* at $E_1+$—and proves two theorems. First, the Finite Factorization Pasting (Theorem [thm:finite-factorization-pasting]): pasting the two squares forces every $E_1$ datum to factor through finitely many primitive sector components. Second, the Enriched Bi-Square Comparison (Theorem [thm:enriched-bi-square-comparison]): the three bi-squares are structurally identical—same shape, same structural maps, richer objects—and the comparison extends forward to the computational bi-square of Part VII (Definition [def:computational-bi-square], Ch. 58). The enriched bi-square is the single diagram that unifies all of Part VI: BSD coherence (Theorem [thm:bsd-coherence-theorem], Ch. 47), Langlands functoriality (Theorem [thm:functoriality-theorem], Ch. 49), and the physics layer readings (NS regularity, YM mass gap, Hodge addressability) are all encoded in its structure.
