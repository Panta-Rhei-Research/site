The preceding chapters diagnosed ZFC as an $E_2$ virtual machine
(Ch. 64), catalogued the five forbidden moves (Ch. 65),
and identified incompleteness as an $E_2$ boundary marker (Ch. 66).
This chapter formalises the bridge itself.
Section 1 defines the Bridge Axiom (III.D71):
a structure-preserving functor satisfying four properties.
Section 2 introduces shadow diagrams (III.D72):
images of $\T$-internal diagrams under the bridge,
preserving commutativity but incurring degeneracies.
Section 3 develops the three-layer template
that every bridge claim must satisfy.
Section 4 applies it to the Riemann Hypothesis as model case (III.T45).
Section 5 previews the extension to the remaining seven problems.
