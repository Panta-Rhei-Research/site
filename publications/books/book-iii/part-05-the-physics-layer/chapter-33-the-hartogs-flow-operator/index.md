We construct the central operator of the Navier–Stokes treatment: the Hartogs flow operator $H_{flow}$, which extends $τ$-admissible initial data into the $τ^3$ bulk via Local Hartogs continuation. The operator is linear, tower-coherent, and sector-preserving. A structural surprise emerges: the split-complex codomain $H_τ$ forces the natural differential operator to be the *wave operator*, not the Laplacian. This operator polarity swap—from elliptic to hyperbolic—is architecturally forced by the bipolar structure of the lemniscate boundary, and it explains why $τ$-internal Navier–Stokes has hyperbolic character while the classical formulation is parabolic.
