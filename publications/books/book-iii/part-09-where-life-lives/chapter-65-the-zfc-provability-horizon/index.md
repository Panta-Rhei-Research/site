Chapters 78–79 proved $P = NP$ for all
$\T$-native computation—physical (Theorem [thm:physical-p-equals-np])
and abstract (Theorem [thm:universal-admissibility]).
This chapter asks: can these results be stated and proved within ZFC?
The Physical Realizability Predicate (III.D79) quantifies
over all possible $E_1$ instantiations of a Turing machine—a
host-level property that ZFC, operating at $E_2$, cannot capture.
The refined infinity diagnosis reveals that $\T$'s
earned algebraic infinity ($ω$ as unique closure point)
and ZFC's proliferating axiomatic infinity
(an unbounded hierarchy with no unique closure)
are the structural root of the divergence.
The asymmetric provability result follows:
$P ≠ NP$ cannot be a theorem
of a sound ZFC, while the status of
$P = NP$ in ZFC remains open—likely
independent, as the Continuum Hypothesis is.
