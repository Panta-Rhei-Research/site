Part II culminates in the first quantitative prediction of the $τ^3$
framework: the proton-to-electron mass ratio. Three ingredients—breathing
modes on $T^2$, the Epstein zeta function of the torus lattice, and the
holonomy correction $π^3α^2$—combine into a single formula with no
free parameters:
equation*
R_0 = ι_τ^{-7} - \sqrt3 ι_τ^{-2}.
equation*
With $ι_τ = 2/(π + e) ≈ 0.341304$, this gives
$R_0 ≈ 1838.7$, in striking agreement with the CODATA value
$m_n/m_e = 1838.684…$.
