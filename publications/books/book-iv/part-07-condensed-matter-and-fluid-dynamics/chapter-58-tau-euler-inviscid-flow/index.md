The Euler equations of classical fluid dynamics
describe the motion of an inviscid fluid:
no friction, no dissipation, no viscosity.
In τ³, the Euler equations are not postulated.
They *emerge* from defect budget conservation on T²
in the regime where vorticity dissipation and compression
are negligible: d₂ ≈ d₃ ≈ 0.
This chapter derives the τ-Euler equation,
establishes the Kelvin circulation theorem
as a topological invariant of T²,
and shows how Bernoulli's principle
is a consequence of defect energy conservation.
