Part VI began with the Big Bang
and ends here,
with the universe's biography complete.
Twelve chapters have traced an arc
from origin to fate:
the Big Bang as regime,
inflation without an inflaton,
the threshold ladder,
the birth and topology of black holes,
bipolarity and fusion,
the No-Shrink Theorem,
merger normal forms,
global finiteness,
eternal circulation,
a falsification pack,
and boundary unification.

This chapter does four things.
First, it assembles the eleven structural results
of Part VI into a single inventory
(§).
Second, it states honestly what remains open
(§).
Third, it summarizes the export contracts
to Books VI and VII
(§).
Fourth, it builds the bridge to Part VII:
the systematic comparison
with orthodox physics
(§).

When this chapter is complete,
the physics of the universe —
from quantum mechanics on the fiber
(Book IV)
to cosmology and black holes on the base
(Book V) —
has been derived from seven axioms
on five generators.
What remains is to ask:
*How does this compare
to what physics has built
over the past four centuries?*
