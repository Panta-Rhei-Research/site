of energy transport:
radiation, conduction, and convection.
Radiation is electromagnetic (Maxwell's equations).
Conduction is “molecular vibration transfer.”
Convection is “bulk fluid motion.”
The three modes appear to have different physical origins:
radiation is a field phenomenon,
conduction is a microscopic collision chain,
and convection is a macroscopic flow.

in the $B$-sector ($γ$-generator, EM)
of the boundary holonomy algebra.

it is near-field EM transport
through the lattice.
Convection is not kinetic mass transfer;
it is EM-driven coherent displacement
of the defect-functional profile.
