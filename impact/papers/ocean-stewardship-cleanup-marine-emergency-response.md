## Overview

This paper examines how a physically faithful τ ocean-transport twin could transform marine debris trajectory modeling, oil-spill containment and cleanup coordination, search-and-rescue asset deployment, and ecological early-warning systems for ocean health emergencies.

## Detail

This companion paper develops the ocean stewardship and emergency response tier of the Ocean portfolio. It covers τ's potential contribution to marine debris tracking and cleanup prioritization, oil-spill response optimization, search-and-rescue mission support, and ecological early-warning for ocean health events. It argues that these applications share a common requirement for accurate Lagrangian transport modeling in complex ocean-atmosphere conditions, making them a natural cluster for τ's physically faithful, bounded-error ocean twin.

## Paper Thesis

A τ ocean-transport twin that models surface and subsurface drift with physical fidelity could provide the trajectory intelligence that marine debris cleanup, oil-spill response, search-and-rescue operations, and ecological early warning currently lack.
