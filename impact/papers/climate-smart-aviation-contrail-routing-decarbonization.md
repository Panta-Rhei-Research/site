## Overview

This paper examines how a physically faithful τ atmosphere-trajectory twin could transform aviation's climate impact by enabling contrail-aware flight planning, climate-optimal trajectory optimization, and tighter integration of weather intelligence into ATM modernization — addressing both CO2 and non-CO2 aviation climate effects.

## Detail

This companion paper develops the climate-smart aviation tier of the Weather portfolio. It covers τ's potential contribution to contrail-aware routing, climate-optimal trajectory selection, and the integration of high-fidelity atmospheric modeling into flight planning and air traffic management. The paper draws on EUROCONTROL's AVENIR work, EASA ANCEN notes, FAA/NASA/NOAA contrail research, and DLR analysis showing that contrail climate impact could be reduced by 73% with less than 1% extra CO2 emissions. Aviation emitted almost 950 Mt CO2 in 2023, and contrail radiative forcing may exceed CO2 warming effects over policy-relevant timescales.

## Paper Thesis

A τ atmosphere-trajectory twin that predicts ice-supersaturated regions, contrail persistence, and wind-optimal routes with physical fidelity could provide aviation with a major operational decarbonization lever that neither CO2-only emissions pricing nor conventional weather forecasting can deliver.
