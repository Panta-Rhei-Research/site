## Overview

This paper examines how a physically faithful τ low-altitude weather twin could transform the operational viability of advanced air mobility, heavy-lift airships, humanitarian air logistics, and remote-community access — addressing weather-fragility as the primary barrier to new aerial logistics becoming a reliable public-good infrastructure.

## Detail

This companion paper develops the new aerial logistics tier of the Weather portfolio. It covers τ's potential contribution to the operational reliability of advanced air mobility (AAM/UAM/RAM), heavy-lift airship operations, humanitarian air logistics corridors, and remote-community aerial access. FAA has identified 60 explicit and implicit weather-related needs in the UAS/AAM integrated research plan, NASA has added 41 unique requirements with more than 300 dependencies, and Transport Canada has assessed 182 remote communities as accessible only by air for most of the year. WFP's humanitarian air service transported over 355,000 passengers and 4,925 metric tons of cargo to almost 400 remote destinations in 2024.

## Paper Thesis

A τ low-altitude weather twin that models wind fields, turbulence, boundary-layer dynamics, and weather windows with physical fidelity could provide the operational reliability that advanced air mobility, heavy-lift airships, and humanitarian aerial logistics need to serve as dependable public-good infrastructure.
