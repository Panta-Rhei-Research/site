## Overview

This paper examines how a physically faithful τ ocean-weather-logistics twin could transform maritime routing decisions, port operational efficiency, and the viability of green shipping corridors by providing bounded-error predictions of ocean conditions, weather windows, and vessel performance.

## Detail

This companion paper develops the foundation tier of the Ocean portfolio. It covers τ's potential contribution to maritime routing optimization, port flow management, and the operational backbone of green shipping corridors. It argues that global maritime trade's dependence on accurate ocean and weather prediction makes it a natural first application for τ's physically faithful, bounded-error ocean-atmosphere twin, with both commercial efficiency and decarbonization implications.

## Paper Thesis

A τ ocean-weather-logistics twin that models sea state, currents, and weather windows with physical fidelity could unlock major efficiency gains in maritime routing, port flow coordination, and the operational credibility of green shipping corridors.
