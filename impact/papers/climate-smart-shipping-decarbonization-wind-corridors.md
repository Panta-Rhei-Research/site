## Overview

This paper examines how a physically faithful τ atmosphere-ocean twin could transform shipping decarbonization by enabling climate-optimal routing, improving wind-assisted propulsion economics, and providing the weather intelligence backbone for operationally credible wind-powered cargo corridors.

## Detail

This companion paper develops the decarbonization tier of the Ocean portfolio. It covers τ's potential contribution to climate-smart shipping through improved routing for emissions reduction, wind-assisted propulsion system optimization, and the operational backbone of wind-powered cargo corridors. It argues that shipping's dependence on accurate wind and sea-state prediction makes τ's ocean-atmosphere capabilities a natural accelerant for IMO decarbonization mandates.

## Paper Thesis

A τ atmosphere-ocean twin that models wind fields, sea state, and vessel-weather interactions with physical fidelity could provide the weather intelligence backbone that wind-assisted propulsion and wind-powered cargo corridors need to become operationally credible and economically competitive.
