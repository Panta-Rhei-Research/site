## Overview

This paper examines how a physically faithful τ energy-system twin could strengthen long-run capacity planning, market design for high-renewable grids, cross-border coordination, and the investment prioritization decisions that will determine decarbonization pathways at national and regional scales.

## Detail

This companion paper develops the system-integration and policy tier of the Energy portfolio. It covers τ's potential contribution to integrated energy-system planning, electricity market design under high-renewable penetration, cross-border grid coordination, and strategic investment prioritization for clean energy transitions. It argues that the complexity and long time horizons of energy system transitions make them a natural target for τ's ability to provide physically grounded, bounded-error system twins at multiple scales.

## Paper Thesis

A τ energy-system twin that captures the physical dynamics of integrated power systems with bounded error could improve long-run planning, market design, cross-border coordination, and investment prioritization for clean-energy transitions in ways that current soft-linked modeling approaches cannot.
