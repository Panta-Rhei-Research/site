## Overview

Biodiversity and ecosystem restoration is one of the most important remaining public-good domains in the broader τ meta-portfolio. The portfolio organizes five mission layers under one shared ecological twin, from landscape prioritization and wildlife corridors through blue-green habitats, stress early warning, and biodiversity finance verification.

## Detail

The Kunming–Montreal Global Biodiversity Framework calls for at least 30% of degraded terrestrial, inland water, and marine/coastal ecosystems to be under effective restoration by 2030. UNEP says around 3.2 billion people are adversely affected by land degradation and IPBES says around 1 million species are threatened with extinction. The Global Wetland Outlook 2025 reports 22% of wetlands lost since 1970. UNEP/CMS reported in 2024 that 44% of CMS-listed migratory species show population decline. Invasive alien species cost above USD 423 billion in 2019. UNEP's State of Finance for Nature 2026 says the world spends about 30 dollars on nature-degrading activities for every 1 dollar invested in protecting or restoring nature. The portfolio organizes five linked papers sharing one τ ecological substrate — hydrology, soil, vegetation, habitat, species movement, disturbance, and restoration dynamics.
