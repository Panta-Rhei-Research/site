## Overview

Climate is one of the highest-leverage τ deployment fields — not centered on fractional accuracy gains but on making the climate system's causal tree more legible for decision quality. The portfolio organizes five mission layers under one shared Earth-system causal twin, from driver attribution to long-range ocean and cryosphere resilience.

## Detail

The Climate portfolio frames climate as one of the most important τ deployment domains because decision quality is at least as important as additional scientific output volume. WMO reports 2024 was likely the first calendar year more than 1.5°C above the 1850–1900 average, while UNEP says current policies still point to about 2.8°C warming this century. The portfolio organizes five linked papers under one shared τ Earth-system causal twin, covering driver attribution and sink intelligence, regional adaptation planning, ocean and cryosphere resilience, and climate policy optimization. Institutions such as Destination Earth, NOAA/NESDIS, WMO G3W, UNEP MARS/IMEO, World Bank CCDRs, and the UN Ocean Decade/DITTO are already building pieces of the architecture this portfolio would strengthen.
