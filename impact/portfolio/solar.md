## Overview

Solar is one of the clearest first-wave τ deployment fields because weather and cloud physics write the solar production curve directly. The portfolio organizes five mission layers under one shared weather–irradiance–PV–grid twin, from bulk-grid dispatch and distributed PV visibility through critical-load resilience, asset protection, and flexible demand.

## Detail

Solar PV reached 2,000 TWh and 7% of global electricity in 2024, with over 2.2 TW of cumulative capacity and over 600 GW newly commissioned in 2024. Solar is no longer marginal — forecast error and reserve conservatism now impose real costs at ISO scale. NREL found that day-ahead and four-hour-ahead forecasts in a 25% solar ISO-NE system reduced net generation costs by 22.9%. DOE, NREL, FEMP, and major market operators are already working on solar forecasting, distributed PV visibility, DER interconnection, dynamic hosting capacity, solar-plus-storage resilience, storm-hardening, and flexible demand. The portfolio organizes five linked papers sharing one atmosphere–clouds–irradiance–PV–storage–grid substrate.
