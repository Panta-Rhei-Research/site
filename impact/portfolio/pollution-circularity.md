## Overview

Pollution and circularity is one of the largest remaining high-fit clusters in the broader τ meta-portfolio. The portfolio organizes five mission layers under one shared τ pollution–materials–exposure twin, from clean-air digital twins and emissions targeting through toxic-pathway intelligence, waste and plastics operations, and circular-system redesign.

## Detail

WHO says 99% of the global population breathes air above WHO guideline levels, and combined ambient and household air pollution causes about 7 million premature deaths per year. UNEP frames pollution as the largest environmental cause of disease and premature death worldwide, responsible for at least 9 million deaths each year. Air pollution costs about USD 8.1 trillion per year in welfare losses. Municipal solid waste generation could rise from 2.1 billion tonnes in 2023 to 3.8 billion tonnes by 2050. UNEP says 19–23 million tonnes of plastic leak into aquatic ecosystems annually. WHO attributes around 1.5 million premature deaths in 2021 to lead exposure. Resource extraction could rise 60% by 2060 without urgent action. The portfolio organizes five linked papers sharing one τ pollution–materials substrate: emissions, atmospheric transport, chemical contamination, waste flows, plastics leakage, material degradation, recovery, and circular-system dynamics.
