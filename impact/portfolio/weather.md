## Overview

Weather, climate, and disaster intelligence is one of the clearest first public-good deployment domains for τ. The portfolio covers seven core use cases under one shared τ atmosphere-physics substrate, organized across three deployment horizons from extreme-event forecasting to climate adaptation planning and global warning access.

## Detail

The WMO Atlas of 1970–2021 reports nearly 12,000 weather-, climate-, and water-related disasters, about USD 4.3 trillion in reported losses, and about 2 million deaths — roughly USD 82.7 billion in losses and 38,000 deaths per year. WMO states that a warning issued within 24 hours of a hazardous event can reduce damage by up to 30%, and the world could avoid USD 3–16 billion per year through stronger early warning systems. One-third of the world's population is still not covered by early warning systems. NOAA reports that in 2024 the United States experienced 27 billion-dollar weather/climate disasters with USD 182.7 billion in losses. Destination Earth's Extremes Digital Twin already produces daily global simulations at about 4.4 km resolution, ECMWF's AIFS reports up to 20% gains on tropical-cyclone track measures, and WMO/UN Early Warnings for All aims to protect everyone on Earth by 2027. The portfolio organizes seven core use cases under one τ atmosphere-physics substrate across three time horizons: 2–5 years, 5–10 years, and 10–20 years.
