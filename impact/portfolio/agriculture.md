## Overview

Agriculture is one of the strongest first-wave τ deployment fields: the chain from better physics and biology to better public outcomes is unusually short. The portfolio organizes five mission layers under one shared agro–climate–water–biology twin, from operational agro-weather intelligence to crop biology and breeding for long-run resilience.

## Detail

FAO puts agricultural losses from disasters at USD 3.26 trillion over 1991–2023 (roughly USD 99 billion per year), agriculture accounts for 72% of global freshwater withdrawals, and more than 295 million people faced acute hunger in 2024. The portfolio organizes five linked papers sharing one agro–climate–water–biology substrate: atmosphere and local weather, soil moisture and hydrology, crop growth and stress, pest and disease dynamics, livestock stress, and seasonal risk. Official institutions including WMO, FAO, WFP, CGIAR, USDA, and RIPE are already building surrounding infrastructure that a τ capability could strengthen.
