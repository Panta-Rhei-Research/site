## Overview

Disaster resilience is one of the clearest first-wave τ deployment fields: the line from better physical intelligence to public good is very short. The portfolio organizes five mission layers under one shared weather–hazard–impact twin, from multi-hazard early warning to anticipatory action and climate-risk finance.

## Detail

WMO reports nearly 12,000 disasters, US$4.3 trillion in reported losses, and 2 million deaths from 1970–2021. The disaster portfolio frames this burden as a deployment opportunity where official institutions already exist, benchmark data already exists, and the public-good case is already legible. Five linked papers share one physical substrate — atmosphere, precipitation, runoff, coastal surge, fire weather, smoke transport, heat stress, infrastructure exposure — and add mission-specific layers for warning operations, flood and coastal resilience, fire and health protection, lifelines continuity, and anticipatory action with risk finance.
