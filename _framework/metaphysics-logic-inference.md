## Overview

Logic is not a fixed system. It is **scale-dependent**: Boolean at the micro-level of single [ABCD addresses]({{ '/framework/mathematics-abcd-chart/' | relative_url }}), Bayesian at the meso/macro-level of populations and statistical ensembles. The framework unifies classical and probabilistic logic through the sheaf condition, earns modal semantics from the <math><mi>&tau;</mi></math>-kernel's own structure, and handles contradictions at register boundaries through the [paraconsistent]({{ '/framework/mathematics-earned-topos/' | relative_url }}) <math><msub><mtext>Truth</mtext><mn>4</mn></msub></math> logic earned in Book I.

## The Core Idea

**Truth-bearers as sections** (VII.D57): a proposition is a section of the truth sheaf over the agent's experiential base. A proposition is *true* when its section glues coherently -- when the evidence from all accessible perspectives is compatible. **Truth-makers as structures** (VII.D58): what *makes* a proposition true is not a corresponding "fact" in a separate realm, but the structural feature of <math><msup><mi>&tau;</mi><mn>3</mn></msup></math> that the section tracks. Correspondence theory (truth = matching a fact) and coherence theory (truth = fitting a system) **unify** in the sheaf condition: correspondence is the local matching of section to structure, coherence is the global gluing condition.

**Scale-dependent logic**: at a single ABCD address, the [subobject classifier]({{ '/framework/mathematics-earned-topos/' | relative_url }}) is <math><mrow><msub><mi>&Omega;</mi><mi>&tau;</mi></msub><mo>=</mo><mo>{</mo><mi>T</mi><mo>,</mo><mi>F</mi><mo>,</mo><mi>B</mi><mo>,</mo><mi>N</mi><mo>}</mo></mrow></math> -- four-valued but locally Boolean (each address sees only T or F in each sector). At the population level, where many addresses contribute to a single observable, the effective logic becomes Bayesian: probabilities emerge as relative frequencies of T-sections across the population.

**Modal logic** (VII.D62) is earned from the kernel: possible worlds are <math><mi>&tau;</mi></math>-admissible configurations (those satisfying K0-K6), and the accessibility relation between worlds is determined by which configurations are reachable by morphism composition. Necessity = truth in all accessible configurations. Possibility = truth in some. The Kripke semantics is not imported -- it falls out of the [earned topos]({{ '/framework/mathematics-earned-topos/' | relative_url }}) structure.

**Paraconsistency at register boundaries**: the [four registers]({{ '/framework/metaphysics-four-registers/' | relative_url }}) can make locally inconsistent claims (an empirical finding may conflict with a practical obligation). The <math><msub><mtext>Truth</mtext><mn>4</mn></msub></math> logic handles this: the B (both) truth value captures propositions that are true in one register and false in another without the system exploding. The [explosion barrier]({{ '/framework/mathematics-split-complex-holomorphy/' | relative_url }}) (I.T13) prevents contradictions from propagating across registers.

**Internal randomness** exists (quantum measurement outcomes are genuinely non-deterministic within the framework). External randomness does not (the [categoricity]({{ '/framework/mathematics-categoricity/' | relative_url }}) theorem means the universe has zero free parameters at the structural level).

## Why This Matters

The logic module completes the diagrammatic register and sets up the practical register ([ethics]({{ '/framework/metaphysics-categorical-ethics/' | relative_url }})): ethical reasoning requires a logic, and the framework provides one that handles the inter-register tensions that ethical dilemmas create.
