## Overview

The same [4+1 sector template]({{ '/framework/mathematics-sector-template/' | relative_url }}) that produces four forces at <math><msub><mi>E</mi><mn>1</mn></msub></math> produces **five life sectors** at <math><msub><mi>E</mi><mn>2</mn></msub></math>, each with a biological archetype. The sectors are not a classification imposed on biology from outside -- they are derived from the generator structure of Category <math><mi>&tau;</mi></math>.

## The Core Idea

The five generators map to five life sectors (VI.T07):

- **Persistence** (<math><mi>&alpha;</mi></math>-sector, VI.D13): temporal stability, homeostasis, deep time. Archetype: **Archaea** -- Earth's oldest lineage, surviving extremes of temperature, salinity, and acidity for billions of years. The origin of life is recast as the first persistence event.

- **Agency** (<math><mi>&pi;</mi></math>-sector, VI.D14): spatial motility, environmental interaction. Archetype: **Bacteria** -- the most abundant organisms on Earth, colonizing every environment via flagellar motility and chemotaxis. ATP as universal energy currency.

- **Source/Producer** (<math><mi>&gamma;</mi></math>-sector, VI.D15): structure generation, photosynthesis, carbon fixation. Archetype: **Plants** -- sessile autotrophs converting inorganic precursors into organic structure. The [genetic code]({{ '/framework/life-genetic-code/' | relative_url }}) lives here.

- **Closure/Recycler** (<math><mi>&eta;</mi></math>-sector, VI.D16): decomposition, nutrient cycling, death. Archetype: **Fungi** -- mycelial networks that decompose organic matter and return nutrients to the biosphere. Death and aging complete the closure loop.

- **Consumer** (<math><mrow><mo>(</mo><mi>&gamma;</mi><mo>,</mo><mi>&eta;</mi><mo>)</mo></mrow></math> mixed sector, VI.D17-D18): acquires structure and energy from other life forms. Archetype: **Animals** -- coupling source and closure channels. The most complex sector: [neural architecture]({{ '/framework/life-neural-architecture/' | relative_url }}), immune systems, and consciousness emerge here.

## Why This Matters

The sector classification organizes all of biology into a structurally derived taxonomy. It explains why these five functional roles recur across all ecosystems and why the three-domain taxonomy (Archaea, Bacteria, Eukarya) maps onto the sector structure. The sectors are not merely descriptive -- they generate testable predictions about which biological functions must coexist and which are structurally independent.
