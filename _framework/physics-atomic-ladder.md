## Overview

Hydrogen is not "a proton plus an electron." In Category <math><mi>&tau;</mi></math>, the hydrogen atom is a single co-rotor system on <math><msup><mi>T</mi><mn>2</mn></msup></math>: two coupled bulk modes (proton core and electron orbit) of the same torus, linked by the fibration map. From hydrogen, the entire complexity ladder ascends: the Bohr radius, the Rydberg constant, shell structure, chemical bonding, molecular geometry, organic chemistry, and the biochemistry that bridges to [Book VI]({{ '/publications/books/book-vi/' | relative_url }}).
