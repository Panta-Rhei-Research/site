The τ framework approaches philosophy not as an afterthought to the physical sciences but as the terminal enrichment layer (E₃) where the system becomes maximally formal about reflection itself. Ethics is derived, not postulated. Epistemology is grounded in the structure of sections over experience. And the framework explicitly marks its own limit — the point at which proof gives way to commitment.

## Key claims

### [Categorical Imperative as Fixed Point]({{ '/results/problem/categorical-imperative-fixed-point/' | relative_url }}) · Resolved

Kant's Categorical Imperative is the unique j-closed fixed point of the τ-digestion operator (VII.T35). Ethics is derived from the kernel's structure, not imported as a philosophical axiom.

### [Categorical Imperative Derivation]({{ '/results/problem/categorical-imperative-derivation/' | relative_url }}) · Resolved

The CI is not merely claimed to be a fixed point but is explicitly derived as structurally necessary at E₃. Stage CI (VII.P21) establishes that the ethical constraint is unavoidable for any sufficiently reflective system.

### [Gettier Problem]({{ '/results/problem/gettier-problem/' | relative_url }}) · Resolved

The Gettier Problem is resolved by defining knowledge as a global section of a presheaf over an observation cover. Gettier cases are identified as cover failures — justified true belief that fails the gluing condition.

### [Problem of Universals]({{ '/results/problem/problem-of-universals/' | relative_url }}) · Resolved

The problem of universals receives a structural resolution: universals are NF addresses in the kernel. Not a separate realm (realism) nor mere names (nominalism) but structural positions.

### [No Forced Stance]({{ '/results/problem/no-forced-stance/' | relative_url }}) · Resolved

The No Forced Stance theorem (VII.T47): the framework cannot force a stance on the ω-germ question. Any stance belongs to the commitment register. This is the program's own acknowledgment of where proof ends.

### [Substance vs Process Ontology]({{ '/results/problem/substance-vs-process-ontology/' | relative_url }}) · Resolved

The substance/process debate is dissolved: τ's relational ontology is neither pure substance nor pure process but a structurally articulated world in which both being and becoming are real.

### [Why Something Rather Than Nothing]({{ '/results/problem/why-something-rather-than-nothing/' | relative_url }}) · Resolved

The question "Why is there something rather than nothing?" receives a structural answer: the kernel's existence is the condition for anything to be thinkable at all. Nothing is not a coherent alternative.

### [Definition of Beauty]({{ '/results/problem/definition-of-beauty/' | relative_url }}) · Resolved

Beauty is defined as structural invariance (VII.T19): what survives all admissible transformations is beautiful. Not subjective preference but a structural property.

### [Beauty and Truth]({{ '/results/problem/beauty-and-truth/' | relative_url }}) · Resolved

Beauty and truth are structurally related at E₃: both are expressions of the same coherence structure, readable from different registers.

### [Animal Rights and Dignity]({{ '/results/problem/animal-rights-and-dignity/' | relative_url }}) · Resolved

Dignity is a structural status (VII.D65) belonging to any entity occupying E₃ reflective capacity. Animal rights and dignity receive a graded structural treatment rather than an all-or-nothing verdict.

### [Gödel Avoidance]({{ '/results/problem/goedel-avoidance/' | relative_url }}) · Resolved

Listed here because Gödel's theorems are as much a philosophical question as a mathematical one: the framework claims to show that incompleteness is a feature of a particular kind of formal world, not an absolute barrier.
