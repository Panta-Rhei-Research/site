## Overview

Magnetic monopoles — hypothetical particles carrying isolated magnetic charge — have been predicted by Dirac, by 't Hooft and Polyakov in GUT constructions, and have been searched for in cosmic-ray experiments and accelerator data for over fifty years. No monopoles have been detected. In τ, this absence is not an experimental puzzle but a structural theorem: IV.T42 (Homogeneous Maxwell Equations, Book IV ch21 "Gauge Invariance & Maxwell") proves that div B = 0 follows directly from d² = 0 — the Bianchi identity on the EM gauge bundle. Monopoles are forbidden by the bundle structure itself, not by contingent observation.

## Detail

The Dirac argument famously shows that if magnetic monopoles existed, the electric charge must be quantized; this has motivated monopole searches for nearly a century, reinforced by 't Hooft-Polyakov solutions in GUT models predicting super-heavy monopoles from early-universe phase transitions. In Category τ, the electromagnetic sector B/γ is carried by a gauge bundle whose field strength F = dA satisfies dF = d²A = 0 by the nilpotency of the exterior derivative. IV.T42 (Homogeneous Maxwell Equations, located in `books/IV-CategoricalMicrocosm/latex/sections/part03/ch21-gauge-invariance-maxwell.tex` line 204) formalizes this: the Bianchi identity dF = 0 yields the two homogeneous Maxwell equations — div B = 0 and ∇ × E + ∂B/∂t = 0 — automatically from d² = 0. A magnetic monopole would require div B = ρ_m ≠ 0 for some source distribution, which would contradict IV.T42 globally. The result is that monopoles are structurally forbidden in the τ-framework — the bundle structure does not admit them at all, independent of mass scale or experimental reach. The seventy-year null experimental record is the predicted outcome.
