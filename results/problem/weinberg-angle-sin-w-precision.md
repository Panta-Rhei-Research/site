## Overview

The weak mixing angle sin²θ_W ≈ 0.23122 is one of the most precisely measured parameters in particle physics, governing the strength of the electroweak mixing between the electromagnetic and weak forces. In the τ-framework, sin²θ_W is *not* a free parameter: it arises from the A–B sector mixing angle (the inter-sector coupling of the holonomy algebra). The LO formula sin²θ_W = ι<sub>τ</sub>(1 − ι<sub>τ</sub>) gives ≈ 0.2249 from the master constant ι<sub>τ</sub> alone.

At NNLO, the τ-framework derivation (IV.D337) uses Window Universality — the identity W₃(4) = 5 — to generate successive correction terms. The NLO correction (1 − α W₃(4) ι<sub>τ</sub>²) and the NNLO correction (1 + α² W₃(3) W₃(4) ι<sub>τ</sub>⁴) combine to yield sin²θ_W at **−0.7 ppm** from the PDG value. This makes sin²θ_W the most precisely derived electroweak parameter in the τ-framework, alongside the W boson mass at −0.5 ppm (IV.T177).
