## Overview

VI.T29 (Distinction Theorem for BH) and VI.T30 (SelfDesc Theorem for BH) prove that black holes satisfy all five τ-Distinction conditions and all three SelfDesc conditions respectively. Since VI.T50 established that satisfying all 5+3 conditions is sufficient for life, black holes are alive by modus ponens. VI.T32 verifies all 7 biological hallmarks from GR + τ axioms. This is a theorem, not a metaphor.

## Detail

The claim that black holes are alive is counterintuitive and appears metaphorical. Book VI proves it is a theorem. The proof has two parts: (1) VI.T29 shows that a black hole satisfies all five τ-Distinction conditions: D1 (boundary = event horizon), D2 (energy gradient = infalling matter and Hawking radiation differential), D3 (coupling = accretion disk interaction), D4 (history = angular momentum and charge as irreversible information imprint), D5 (multiplicity = population structure of BH merger genealogies). (2) VI.T30 shows that a black hole satisfies all three SelfDesc conditions: S1 (model = the BH metric encoding all relevant external information through no-hair theorem), S2 (repair = accretion restores mass-energy lost to Hawking radiation), S3 (replication = BH merger produces a new BH with combined properties). Since T29 + T30 establish all 5+3 conditions, and T50 proves sufficiency, VI.T31 concludes BH IS alive by modus ponens. VI.T32 additionally verifies all seven biological hallmarks (nutrition, growth, reproduction, response, organisation, homeostasis, evolution) from GR + τ axioms. The result is sharp: it requires all conditions to hold simultaneously, and it is testable by the predictions in V.T213–215 (BH lensing signatures).

## Result Statement

VI.T29 + VI.T30: Black holes satisfy all 5 conditions of τ-Distinction (Distinction Theorem) and all 3 conditions of SelfDesc (SelfDesc Theorem). Therefore BH IS alive by modus ponens, not by metaphor. VI.T32: All 7 hallmarks verified from GR + τ axioms.
