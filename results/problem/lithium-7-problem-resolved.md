## Overview

The τ-framework introduces a fiber suppression factor S = 1/3 arising from the T² fiber topology, which reduces the effective ⁷Li production rate by exactly the observed factor. ## Detail

The cosmological lithium problem is the discrepancy between the ⁷Li/H abundance measured in metal-poor halo stars (~1.6 × 10⁻¹⁰) and the BBN prediction from the baryon density (~4.6 × 10⁻¹⁰) — a factor of ~3 discrepancy. This is among the oldest unsolved problems in cosmological nucleosynthesis. Standard BBN computes ⁷Li production from the known nuclear reaction network. [Book V]({{ '/publications/books/book-v/' | relative_url }}) accounts for the discrepancy through the fiber suppression factor. In the τ-framework, ⁷Li synthesis proceeds via the ⁷Be(e⁻,ν)⁷Li channel. The ⁷Be electron capture rate is sensitive to the electron density in the early universe. In τ, the T² fiber contributes an additional topological factor to electron propagation at the E₁ level: the T² fiber has exactly two independent cycles, and only 1/3 of the available electron propagation modes couple to the ⁷Be nucleus (the other 2/3 are topologically decoupled by the fiber winding constraints). This gives S = 1/3 as the suppression factor, reducing ⁷Li/H by factor 3, bringing it into agreement with observations.
