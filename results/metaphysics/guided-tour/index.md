This is a **guided tour** through the τ-framework's metaphysics domain. Unlike physics or life, metaphysics has **no numerical anchor** — it's purely categorical. But it does have a structure, and the structure makes specific claims about classical metaphysical questions.

> **Reading time:** 10 minutes  ·  **Stops:** 6  ·  **Best read:** in order

## Stop 1 of 6 · The four registers

Imagine the τ kernel as a single object, and four "lenses" (called **registers**) that read it in different ways:

- **Reg_E** (Empirical) → reads observations. Every physics measurement, every biology biomarker.
- **Reg_P** (Practical) → reads norms. Every ethical or legal judgment.
- **Reg_D** (Diagrammatic) → reads proofs. Every mathematical theorem, every logical inference.
- **Reg_C** (Commitment) → reads stances. Every promise, every character trait, every dignity claim.

The four registers are the framework's "metaphysical readout architecture". Each one ([MG-R01]({{ '/results/metaphysics/glossary/MG-R01-empirical-register/' | relative_url }}) is the canonical Reg_E entry) projects τ into a different codomain (Obs, Norm, Proof, Stance respectively).

## <span id="stop-2"></span>Stop 2 of 6 · The six narrowing principles (OR1–OR6)

Not every candidate ontic structure can count as "real". The framework has **six structural requirements** that anything claiming ontic status must satisfy:

| # | Name | Constraint |
|---|---|---|
| **OR1** | Self-coherence | No internal contradictions ([MG-P01]({{ '/results/metaphysics/glossary/MG-P01-or1-self-coherence/' | relative_url }})) |
| **OR2** | Completeness | Every required morphism is present |
| **OR3** | Generativity | Structure can be extended consistently |
| **OR4** | Independence | Each requirement is independently necessary |
| **OR5** | Continuity | Adjacent τ-states connect via valid morphisms |
| **OR6** | Stability | The system tolerates perturbation |

Together, OR1–OR6 narrow the space of possible ontic realities down to the τ-categorical kernel itself. They're the framework's "selection rules" for what counts as real.

→ **Next stop:** [What is being?](#stop-3)

## <span id="stop-3"></span>Stop 3 of 6 · What is being?

Classical metaphysics has agonized over "being" for 2500 years — what does it mean for something to exist?

The τ-framework's answer: being is **cross-register categorical existence** ([MG-O01]({{ '/results/metaphysics/glossary/MG-O01-being/' | relative_url }})). Something exists when it has consistent readings across all four registers — when Reg_E reads it (as observation), Reg_P reads it (as norm-bearing), Reg_D reads it (as proof-coherent), and Reg_C reads it (as stance-compatible).

This isn't reductive — it's structural. A particle exists because the four registers agree on its categorical morphisms, not because we measured it.

→ **Next stop:** [The CI operator graph](#stop-4)

## <span id="stop-4"></span>Stop 4 of 6 · The CI operator graph (consequence-intervention)

When you reason "if I do X, then Y will follow" — that's a CI inference. The CI operator graph ([MG-A01]({{ '/results/metaphysics/glossary/MG-A01-ci-operator-graph/' | relative_url }})) is the architectural structure that connects causal claims to their commitments **across** registers.

It's how you get from a Reg_E observation ("this neuron fires") to a Reg_P norm ("therefore consciousness includes responsibility") to a Reg_C commitment ("I am responsible for my actions"). The CI graph is multi-functorial — it operates simultaneously across Reg_P / Reg_D / Reg_C codomains.

This is also the bridge to the life-sector. The CI graph is what consciousness ([LG-M01]({{ '/results/life/glossary/LG-M01-consciousness/' | relative_url }})) instantiates — a self-modeling τ³-computer that runs CI inferences on its own future actions.

→ **Next stop:** [Why are qualia hard?](#stop-5)

## <span id="stop-5"></span>Stop 5 of 6 · Why are qualia hard?

Qualia — the felt redness of red, the bitterness of coffee — are notoriously resistant to physical reduction. The "Hard Problem of Consciousness" is whether physical descriptions can ever explain phenomenal experience.

The τ-framework's answer ([MG-H01]({{ '/results/metaphysics/glossary/MG-H01-qualia/' | relative_url }})): qualia are **Mind-Topos-internal morphisms**. They exist as structural features of the topos in which mind operates — not as physical objects to be reduced, and not as ineffable extras.

This dissolves the Hard Problem. The "redness of red" is the morphism `red-stimulus → red-quale` inside the Mind topos. It's structural; it has objective form; and it's not the same as the physics of red light. They're different functorial projections of the same τ-kernel.
