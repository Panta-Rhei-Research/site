## Why answer-shapes matter

A large framework can fail even when individual sentences sound plausible.

It can predict without explaining, formalize without bridging, simulate without substrate, classify without recovery, or make metaphysical claims without public failure conditions. Answer-Shape Requirements prevent those shortcuts from being mistaken for success.

## Core requirements

<div class="v2-grid">
  <div class="v2-tile">
    <strong>Problem recovery</strong>
    <span>The answer must say which problem or burden it addresses and what is actually recovered.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="v2-tile">
    <strong>Layer typing</strong>
    <span>The answer must locate itself: mathematical, physical, life, metaphysical, bridge, verification, or status claim.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="v2-tile">
    <strong>Recovery path</strong>
    <span>The answer must show how the relevant structure arises, rather than merely naming it.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="v2-tile">
    <strong>No hidden externality</strong>
    <span>Dependencies must be internalized, derived, typed, factored out, bridged, or marked unresolved.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="v2-tile">
    <strong>Diagrammatic inspectability</strong>
    <span>The answer must expose enough structure to be followed in the public corpus or verification surface.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="v2-tile">
    <strong>Model / phenomenon / ontic distinction</strong>
    <span>The answer must not silently turn usefulness into ontic status.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="v2-tile">
    <strong>Verification mode</strong>
    <span>The answer must identify how it can be checked, challenged, formalized, compared, or falsified.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="v2-tile">
    <strong>Failure conditions</strong>
    <span>The answer must say what would weaken, refute, or reclassify it.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="v2-tile">
    <strong>Status and scope</strong>
    <span>The answer must carry a status label that does not exceed its evidence.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="v2-tile">
    <strong>Residual boundary disclosure</strong>
    <span>Unresolved boundaries must be disclosed instead of hidden inside confident prose.</span>
  </div>
</div>

## By question class

Mathematical answers may require definitions, theorems, proofs, formal dependencies, Lean status, and bridge criteria.

Physics answers may require quantity typing, dimensional consistency, regimes, measurement bridges, empirical contact, and falsification pressure.

Life answers may require organizational closure, boundary conditions, heredity, evolution, development, ecology, and mind/life transitions.

Metaphysical answers may require explicit handling of being, relation, identity, causality, modality, truth, language, value, and the ultimate boundary.

## Insufficient answer-shapes

The following do not count as complete answers by themselves:

- "The model fits."
- "The formalism is elegant."
- "The simulation produces the pattern."
- "The analogy is suggestive."
- "The concept can be rephrased."
- "The boundary is mysterious, therefore solved."

## Relation to Results and Verify

The [Results lane]({{ '/results/' | relative_url }}) records current classified claims. The [Verify lane]({{ '/verify/' | relative_url }}) records inspection routes.
