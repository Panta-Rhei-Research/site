## Why this is separate

Open problems test what the kernel can illuminate where current knowledge does not yet close. Recovery requirements test whether the kernel preserves established structures that must not be broken.

## Initial recovery families

<div class="v2-grid">
  <div class="v2-tile"><strong>Einstein equations</strong><span>Relativistic gravity as a required recovery target.</span></div>
  <div class="v2-tile"><strong>Maxwell equations</strong><span>Classical electromagnetism as a required field-structure recovery target.</span></div>
  <div class="v2-tile"><strong>Quantum formalism</strong><span>Measurement, state, operator, and probability structure as recovery pressure.</span></div>
  <div class="v2-tile"><strong>Thermodynamics</strong><span>Entropy, irreversibility, and energy accounting as non-negotiable constraints.</span></div>
  <div class="v2-tile"><strong>Standard number systems</strong><span>Natural, integer, rational, real, and complex structures as mathematical recovery targets.</span></div>
  <div class="v2-tile"><strong>Ring and field structures</strong><span>Algebraic structures that must be recovered or carefully re-grounded.</span></div>
</div>
