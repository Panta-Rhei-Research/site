This chapter assembles the three results into a single structural characterisation of the E₁ enrichment layer — the physics layer — under the metaphor of mortar, bricks, and blueprint. NS regularity is the mortar: it fills the seams between clopen patches, guaranteeing smooth gluing. The YM gap is the bricks: it provides discrete granularity, ensuring configuration space comes in irreducible minimum-separation units. Hodge addressability is the blueprint: it maps out spectral visibility, guaranteeing every room in the architecture is reachable.

## What this chapter contributes

The Physics Layer Assembly Theorem (*III.T29*) proves that all three Part V results are instances of Mutual Determination (*III.D25*) at the E₀ → E₁ interface. The proof constructs a unified three-row, three-column table: the rows are NS (fluid regularity), YM (gauge gap), and Hodge (NF-addressability); the columns are boundary data, spectral structure, and interior consequence. Each cell is filled with content already established in Parts IV–V. The forward direction of each row — boundary data determines interior consequence — is what the individual theorems prove. The backward direction of each row is then verified in this chapter: stabilised germs reconstruct canonical boundary assignment; gap existence implies NF lattice structure; NF-addressability determines balanced spectral content. The backward verification is not new mathematics but a re-reading of the same proofs in the opposite direction, enabled by the Mutual Determination framework.

The common mechanism is made explicit: a single enrichment step of the spectral algebra from E₀ to E₁ activates all three results. The 4+1 sector template distributes spectral content spatially, and the three results are three consequences of this single step applied to three distinct admissibility classes (τ-admissible fluid data, τ-admissible gauge data, σ-fixed characters). The unification is not a coincidence but a structural feature of the E₁ enrichment layer.

The chapter closes with five Part V export contracts (*III.R20*) and the Part V Coherence Summary (*III.R21*): regularity mechanism, gap existence, sector addressability, defect functional framework, and K5 sector isolation — each stated with explicit preconditions and postconditions for Part VI's BSD and Langlands blocks.
