This chapter opens Part IX by establishing that the question "Can search be efficient?" is *native* to the enrichment level E₂ — and meaningless below it. At E₀, objects have relations but no processes: there are no agents, no steps, no execution. At E₁, dynamics exist but no self-referential codes: a rock falling under gravity is not computing. The qualitative leap to E₂ occurs when objects contain their own decoders — when data carries the instructions for reading itself. This leap is formalised as the Code–Execution–Code cycle, defined through the E₂ Computational Agent (*III.D49*) and Operational Closure (*III.D50*). Life is the physical instantiation of this cycle running on E₁ hardware. The chapter closes by proving that every ingredient of the P vs NP problem — agents, steps, efficiency — presupposes E₂ structure.

## What this chapter contributes

Section 1 proves why computation cannot live below E₂. At E₀, proofs are static chains of implications; nothing happens. The Riemann Hypothesis is comfortable at E₀ — it is a question about spectral purity of NF addresses, not a procedural search. At E₁, fields evolve and fluids flow, but no E₁-object contains a representation of its own decoder: the decoder (spectral projection) acts from outside. Without self-referential codes there are no programs; without programs, no agents; without agents, no search; without search, no efficiency measure; and without efficiency, no P vs NP.

Section 2 makes the E₂ structure precise. The *E₂ Computational Agent* (*III.D49*) is a triple (C, D, φ) where C is an E₁-admissible code, D is a decoder that is itself representable as a code, and φ: C → C' is the execution map D(C) = C'. The self-referentiality is constitutive: the decoder is both an operator *on* codes and a code *itself* — exactly as a universal Turing machine is a program that reads programs, or a ribosome is a machine encoded by the DNA it translates. *Operational Closure* (*III.D50*) requires that the Code–Execution–Code cycle C₀ → C₁ → C₂ → ⋯ remain within E₂ at every step: no escape to E₃, no collapse to E₁. The cycle is self-sustaining. The spectral mechanism underlying this is the Parity Bridge Theorem (*III.T07*): balanced spectral polarity (pol(A-sector) = 1, equal χ⁺ and χ⁻ content) is the unique prerequisite for operational closure. Computation enters through the weak sector.

Section 3 identifies life as computation physically instantiated. DNA is a code; the ribosome is a decoder (itself specified by the DNA it reads); proteins are outputs that maintain the code. The correspondence maps precisely onto the E₂ template: code = DNA, decoder = ribosome, output = protein, closure = DNA repair/replication. The claim is not that life is *like* a computer. The claim is the reverse: computation is the ontological primitive, and biology is its physical instantiation on E₁ hardware.

Section 4 formalises *Computation Cannot Live Below E₂* (*III.R22*): the three presuppositions of P vs NP — agents, steps, efficiency — all require E₂. Asking "Is there a polynomial-time algorithm for SAT?" at E₀ is asking whether a search can be efficient in a universe where search does not exist.
