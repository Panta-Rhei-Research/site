The Hinge Theorem (*III.T41*, Ch. 50) proved that the seven-book architecture is derived. The export contracts (*III.R28*, Ch. 51) formalised what each downstream book receives. This capstone chapter closes Part VII with four instruments that together compress the entire series to a single view: the No Knobs Ledger, the Master Schema, the Results Inventory, and one commutative diagram. The series architecture is not a table of contents. It is a theorem. One constant, one diagram, no knobs.

## What this chapter contributes

Section 1 presents the **No Knobs Ledger** and proves *III.T42*, the No Knobs Theorem: every inter-sector and self-coupling in Category τ is a rational function of ι_τ = 2/(π + e), with integer coefficients and denominator non-vanishing on (0,1). The primorial depth at which each coupling first appears is uniquely determined by the spectral trichotomy. The ten couplings — four self-couplings and six inter-sector — are exhibited explicitly: κ(A;1) = ι_τ (weak self-coupling), κ(D;1) = 1 − ι_τ (gravitational complement), κ(B;2) = ι_τ² (electromagnetic), κ(C;3) = ι_τ³/(1 − ι_τ) (strong, with confinement). Three structural patterns emerge: self-couplings form a primorial-depth hierarchy; the D-sector self-coupling is the complement of the A-sector, reflecting fiber-base duality; every inter-sector coupling is a product of constituent self-couplings modulated by the parity structure of the cross-sector boundary characters. No Weinberg angle, no Yukawa matrix, no cosmological constant is a free parameter — each is a sector coupling, a rational function of ι_τ at a determined depth.

Section 2 presents the **Master Schema** (*III.R30*): all eight Millennium Problems placed on a single enriched bi-square. The distribution is 2–5–1: Riemann Hypothesis and Poincaré at E₀ (spectral purity and geometric realisation respectively); Navier–Stokes, Yang–Mills, Hodge, BSD, and Langlands at E₁ (dynamics and cross-sector coherence); P vs NP at E₂ (operational closure). The E₁ row inherits constraints from E₀; the E₂ row inherits from the entire E₁ row. The schema is a partially ordered dependency set, not a flat list. Enrichment level E₃ hosts no Millennium Problem because saturation renders its questions internal self-modelling facts rather than open conjectures.

Section 3 compiles the **Results Inventory** (*III.R31*): seventeen τ-effective theorems proved in Book III, listed in dependency order. The critical path through the inventory runs *III.T01* → *III.T04* → *III.T19* → *III.T40* → *III.T41* → *III.T42*: functor existence, ladder population, spectral separation, tower assembly, Hinge, parameter elimination. Every theorem carries τ-effective scope.

Section 4 displays the **One Diagram** — a commutative square with the enrichment tower on the vertical axis (E₀ to E₃) and the 4+1 sector decomposition (A, B, C, D, mix) on the horizontal. Each book occupies a determined cell; no alternative presentation exists. Every arrow in the diagram carries a coupling that is an entry in the No Knobs Ledger.

## Lean coverage

The Enrichment Tower Assembly (*III.T40*) guarantees the diagram's rows are coherent: the bi-square scales consistently across levels, making the One Diagram a canonical, not schematic, object. The dependency skeleton in the Results Inventory shows that the Millennium Problem theorems branch off the enrichment spine rather than sitting independently, confirming the unified nature of Book III's programme.
