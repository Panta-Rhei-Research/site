The Hinge Theorem (*III.T41*, Ch. 50) proves that Books IV–VII are sector instantiations of Book III's enrichment structure. This chapter translates that architectural theorem into four binding export contracts — one per downstream book — each specifying preconditions that Book III supplies, postconditions the downstream book must discharge as labeled theorems, a scope boundary delimiting what the book may not claim, and a verification protocol confirming that every theorem in the downstream book is an instance of the appropriate sector restriction. The contracts serve as a testable interface between the mathematical core and its applications, analogous to type signatures in programming.

## What this chapter contributes

Section 1 defines the contract format: a quadruple (Pre, Post, Scope, Ver). Preconditions are structures Book III provides, citeable without re-derivation, each carrying a registry identifier. Postconditions are binding obligations — not suggestions — that the downstream book must discharge as proved, labeled theorems. Scope boundaries prevent inter-book territory violations. Verification checks are algorithmic: audit citations against preconditions, match postconditions against labeled theorems, confirm scope respect by absence of out-of-scope claims.

The four contracts, formalised as *III.R28*, specify:

**Book IV** (fiber T² at E₁, D-sector): preconditions include the fibration τ³ = τ¹ ×_f T², D-sector boundary characters, Enr¹|_D, and split-complex scalars. Postconditions are the particle spectrum from irreducible D-sector characters (P1), quantum amplitudes from spectral characters on T²-morphisms (P2), and gravitational dynamics from D-sector Hartogs flow (P3). The scope boundary excludes the base τ¹ and sectors A, B, C. Cross-sector citations are permitted when D-sector phenomena require cross-sector input — such as electroweak vertex corrections — but such citations carry the same status as imported preconditions.

**Book V** (base τ¹ at E₁, A∪B∪C sectors): preconditions include the base τ¹, sector characters, Enr¹|_{A∪B∪C}, and inter-sector coupling via the Parity Bridge (*III.T07*). Postconditions assign the gauge forces to sectors: weak = A (balanced χ± polarity, SU(2)), EM = B (χ+-dominant, U(1)), strong = C (confinement at high depth, SU(3)), plus cosmological evolution from base Hartogs flow.

**Book VI** (E₂ instantiation): preconditions include the E₂ layer template, proto-codes (*III.D61*), the Parity Bridge as E₁→E₂ enabler, and operational closure (*III.D50*). Postconditions establish that biological codes are E₂ operationally closed objects, that the origin of life is the E₁→E₂ transition, and explicitly exclude consciousness as an E₃ matter.

**Book VII** (E₃ instantiation): preconditions include the E₃ layer template and Saturation (*III.T03*). Postconditions establish observer self-models, ethical structure as diagrammatic-sector morphisms, and that no E₄ exists.

Section 5 defines T-compliance (*III.R29*): a downstream book is T-compliant if and only if it satisfies all preconditions, discharges all postconditions, and respects its scope boundary. The Hinge Theorem guarantees that the four contracts jointly exhaust Books IV–VII without overlap.

## Lean coverage

The Parity Bridge (*III.T12*) appears as a precondition for both Books V and VI, making its role explicit across the two enrichment-level transitions it enables. The scope boundary for Book VI explicitly excludes E₃ claims — consciousness and self-modelling are assigned to Book VII — preventing enrichment-level leakage in the biological volume.

## Where this leads

Chapter 52 closes Part VII with the No Knobs Theorem (*III.T42*), the Master Schema, and one diagram. The export contracts established here are the formal commitments that Books IV–VII must honour; the contracts and the Hinge Theorem together guarantee the entire series is coherent.
