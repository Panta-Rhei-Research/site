Sixty chapters converge into a single directed path. This chapter displays the *complete dependency chain* from the five generators (α, π, γ, η, ω) to the Hinge Theorem of Chapter 61: fourteen links, each earned by a named theorem or definition, no link postulated. The chain serves simultaneously as a diagram of logical dependence, a contrast with axiomatic systems that begin by fiat, and the proof strategy for the Hinge Theorem itself — making this the architectural spine on which Part VII rests.

## What this chapter contributes

The central result is *III.D66*, the Complete Dependency Chain: a formal definition of the fourteen-link directed path from generators to Hinge. Each link maps to a named earning event — the Saturation Theorem (*III.T03*) earns the enrichment ladder's terminal step; the 4+1 Sector Template (*III.D10*) earns the sector partition; BSD Coherence (*III.T35*) and Functoriality (*III.T36*) earn the Millennium cluster readings. No link is postulated or imported by assumption. The master constant ι_τ = 2/(π + e) ≈ 0.341304 is derived rather than measured: π and e are the unique transcendental invariants of the solenoidal triple, and the factor 2 is the order of j ↦ −j on the boundary ring H_τ. Every numerical value in the chain — coupling constants, spectral gaps, enrichment dimensions — is a rational function of ι_τ.

Section 1 exhibits the chain in full, with each link's earning theorem or definition listed explicitly. Section 2 contrasts the chain with ZFC's axiomatic method: in ZFC, membership is postulated and free parameters (continuum size, large cardinals) remain undetermined; in Category τ, the five generators entail the axioms, which entail the orbits, and so on — no parameter is free. Section 3 maps the fourteen links to seven proof steps for the Hinge Theorem, showing the chain is not merely a logical diagram but the actual proof outline. Section 4 provides the Chain Verification Protocol (*III.P29*): a five-step algorithm that checks each link at every primorial depth k, confirms compatibility between adjacent levels via the Mittag-Leffler condition, and verifies τ-effective scope.

## Lean coverage

The Riemann Hypothesis reading (*III.T19*), BSD Coherence (*III.T35*), and the Enrichment Tower Assembly (*III.T40*) all appear in the chain as named links, giving readers a map back to their originating chapters. The chain is acyclic — every arrow points forward, with a unique source in the generators and a unique sink in the Hinge — and this acyclicity is itself recorded as a remark, confirming the absence of circular dependence in Book III's logical structure.

## Where this leads

Chapter 50 draws the immediate consequence: the Hinge Theorem (*III.T41*) follows by diagram chase through the assembled tower once every link in this chain is in place. Chapters 51 and 52 then use the chain to generate the export contracts for Books IV–VII and the No Knobs Theorem (*III.T42*), respectively. The chain is the logical foundation on which the entire downstream architecture rests.
