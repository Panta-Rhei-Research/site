The canonical force mapping (Chapter 10) assigns each of the four primitive forces to a generator and a sector: A = π (weak), B = γ (EM), C = η (strong), D = α (gravity). This chapter observes that the assignment is not arbitrary: the generators that build the base τ¹ are the generators whose E₁ incarnations are the *temporal* forces (gravity and weak), while the generators that span the fiber T² are those whose E₁ incarnations are the *spatial* forces (EM, strong, Higgs). The generator-carrier correspondence is a categorical fact proved in Books I–II; its identification as temporal versus spatial is an E₁-level structural reading formalised as the Temporal-Spatial Decomposition (*III.R40*). All ten No Knobs couplings are then classified as temporal–temporal, spatial–spatial, or temporal–spatial interactions, and the electroweak coupling κ(A,B) acquires a precise reading as the mechanism that makes light cones globally consistent.

## What this chapter contributes

Section 1 establishes the generator-carrier correspondence as a τ-effective structural fact (*III.R40*). The fibered product τ³ = τ¹ ×_f T² decomposes the five generators into two groups: the **temporal pair** (α, π) on base τ¹, hosting Gravity (D-sector) and Weak (A-sector); and the **spatial triad** (γ, η, ω) on fiber T², hosting EM (B-sector), Strong (C-sector), and Higgs/mass (B∩C-sector). The Parity Bridge (*III.T07*) forces π = Weak; the confinement coupling forces η = Strong; electroweak mixing forces γ = EM; the remaining slot gives α = Gravity.

Sections 2 and 3 characterise the two groups physically. The temporal pair: gravity (α, D-sector, κ(D;1) = 1 − ι_τ) provides temporal flow — symmetric, time-reversal invariant; weak (π, A-sector, κ(A;1) = ι_τ) provides the temporal arrow — the unique balanced sector (pol = 1) that violates CP and therefore T symmetry. The two self-couplings exhaust the depth-1 budget: κ(A;1) + κ(D;1) = ι_τ + (1 − ι_τ) = 1. Together: directed temporal flow. The spatial triad: Strong (η, C-sector) creates spatial points — irreducible loci, confinement at nuclear scale; EM (γ, B-sector) provides distances and directions between those points; Higgs (ω, B∩C-sector) provides spatial volumes — mass is dense spatial occupancy. Together: complete spatial geometry (points + distances + volumes). No fourth spatial primitive is needed; no fourth spatial force exists.

Section 4 reinterprets the electroweak coupling as Light Cone Consistency (*III.R41*). κ(A,B) = ι_τ²(1−ι_τ) couples the temporal arrow (A-sector on τ¹) to spatial direction (B-sector on T²). At each worldline point, weak provides the local temporal arrow, EM provides the local spatial direction, and κ(A,B) aligns them — forcing the local light cone to be consistently oriented. The Coherent Force (*III.T36*) ensures this alignment is preserved under transition maps across all patches, producing globally consistent light cones. The Weinberg angle sin²θ_W should, in this framework, be derivable as a function of ι_τ; this derivation is a prediction for Book IV.

Section 5 classifies all ten couplings through the temporal-spatial lens. Three patterns emerge: the temporal–temporal coupling κ(A,D) = ι_τ(1−ι_τ) equals the product of the two temporal self-couplings (the temporal pair is multiplicatively closed); the spatial–spatial coupling κ(B,C) = ι_τ³(1+ι_τ)⁻¹ is the Higgs/mass mechanism (volume = space between points along distances); the four temporal–spatial couplings (κ(A,B), κ(A,C), κ(B,D), κ(C,D)) are the mechanisms by which time and space interact — light cone alignment, beta decay at spatial sites, gravitational lensing, and mass curving time. The export contract boundary from Chapter 55 (*III.R39*) now has a natural reading: Book IV receives the spatial fiber; Book V receives the temporal base; κ(A,B) is the structural bridge between them.
