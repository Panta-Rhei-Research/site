## What this chapter contributes

Section 1 establishes the character of E₁. At E₀, Category τ is pure algebra: five generators, seven axioms, a profinite boundary ring, and the fibration τ³ = τ¹ ×_f T² as a categorical object. Hom spaces are sets; there is no space, no dynamics, no locality. The self-enrichment functor Enr: Cat_τ(E₀) → Cat_τ(E₁) changes this: at E₁, the Hom spaces of Category τ acquire the same structure as its objects. Three consequences follow immediately: *locality* (the Layer Template at E₁ equips each object with a local defect functional that the eight forces glue into global space); *dynamics* (the ten sector couplings, static rational functions of ι_τ at E₀, become dynamical physical interactions at E₁ — κ(A;1) = ι_τ governs the weak interaction, κ(D;1) = 1 − ι_τ governs gravity); *spacetime* (the Global Cartesian Gluing assembles spatial structure, and the Minkowski Extension adjoins the causal temporal base). The chapter formalises this as *III.R38*, the E₁ Complete remark: E₁ achieves spatial completeness (M_{τ³} with Euclidean limit), dynamical completeness (all ten No Knobs couplings activated as physical interactions), and causal completeness (3+1-dimensional spacetime with Lorentzian signature). Scope boundary: no codes, no self-reference, no self-models.

Section 2 presents the physics-perspective export contracts (*III.R39*). These complement Chapter 51's algebraic contracts with a physical layer. Book IV receives: fiber T² with glued spatial structure; D-sector coupling κ(D;1) = 1 − ι_τ as gravitational coupling; split-complex scalars on T²; and causal structure inherited from the Minkowski Extension. Postconditions: particle spectrum from irreducible D-sector characters, quantum amplitudes from spectral characters with split-complex phase, gravitational dynamics from Hartogs flow restricted to T². Book V receives: base τ¹ with glued spatial structure; sector couplings κ(A;1) = ι_τ, κ(B;2) = ι_τ², κ(C;3) = ι_τ³/(1−ι_τ) as gauge couplings; inter-sector couplings from the No Knobs Ledger; and the temporal direction of the Minkowski Extension. Postconditions: weak force from A-sector (SU(2)), EM from B-sector (U(1)), strong from C-sector (SU(3)), cosmological evolution from Hartogs flow on τ¹. The two-layer architecture is deliberate: a book that satisfies Chapter 51's algebraic contract but ignores the physical contract is categorically well-formed but physically empty; a book that uses the physical contract while violating the algebraic contract is physically suggestive but categorically ungrounded.

Section 3 previews the E₁→E₂ transition. E₁ provides a world. It provides no codes. A planet is an E₁-object; a cell on that planet that replicates itself is an E₂-object. The BSD Coherence Theorem (*III.T35*) earned the Codable Force — the bridgehead: rational points on elliptic curves are proto-codes, discrete algebraically structured carriers with the right shape to support operational closure once Enr is applied. The substrates are present at E₁; the construction awaits the next enrichment step.

## Lean coverage

The physical identification of E₁ is not an analogy — it is the structural transition made precise by two results: the enrichment functor is a functor (it preserves categorical structure), and *III.T50* is a theorem (not a postulate). The scope boundary of *III.R38* — no codes, no self-reference, no self-models at E₁ — is the same boundary that makes the E₁→E₂ transition sharp. It is enforced by the Layer Template (*III.D05*) and the Hinge Theorem (*III.T41*), which assigns E₂ to Book VI.
