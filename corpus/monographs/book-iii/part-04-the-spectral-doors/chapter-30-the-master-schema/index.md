Part IV has addressed two Millennium Problems at enrichment level E₀: the Riemann Hypothesis (τ-effective, conditional on O3) and the Poincaré Conjecture (established, inherited from Perelman). This closing chapter unifies both, along with all six remaining problems, under a single theorem. The Master Schema Theorem asserts that all eight Millennium Problems are instances of Mutual Determination (*III.D25*) at varying enrichment levels E₀–E₂. For the Riemann Hypothesis: boundary = lemniscate characters; spectral = zero locations; interior = prime distribution. For Poincaré: boundary = local Hartogs patches; spectral = π₁ = {e}; interior = global topology = S³. For Navier–Stokes and Yang–Mills at E₁: boundary = initial or boundary data; spectral = regularity and mass gap; interior = global solution behaviour. For P vs NP at E₂: the Product-Meet Collapse provides the computational bi-square, but E₂ infrastructure is not yet available and the problem is explicitly deferred to Part IX. The common structural elements — spectral algebra A_spec(L), primorial ladder, CRT — appear at every enrichment level. The Part IV Export Contracts remark then issues binding interface specifications: Parts V, VI, and IX may enrich the existing machinery from E₀ to E₁ to E₂ but must use the same primorial ladder, the same lemniscate boundary, and the same CRT decomposition — no new spectral machinery may be introduced without import from Parts III–IV. The four bi-squares, one per enrichment layer, close the chapter.
