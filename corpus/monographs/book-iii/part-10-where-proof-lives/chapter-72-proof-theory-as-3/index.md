Chapters 66–71 operated at E₂, building and auditing the bridge between two virtual machines. This chapter ascends one level. The distinction between E₂ and E₃ is structural rather than a matter of sophistication: a proof-checker is an E₂ object — it takes a proof string, applies type-checking rules, and outputs accept or reject. A proof-theorist is an E₃ object — they take the checker itself as the object of study, prove cut-elimination theorems, establish conservativity results, and diagnose the boundaries the checker cannot cross. The decisive difference is where the decoder lives. At E₂ the decoder is internal to the object; at E₃ the decoder models the observation process itself. This distinction was already foreshadowed in Chapter 68 (*III.T44*), which showed that consistency, completeness, and the halting predicate each require standing outside the code space to evaluate. The chapter uses the Layer Template to formalise proof theory's position at E₃, populates all four template slots, and then identifies the specific sector of E₃ in which Book III itself operates — the ω-coupling sector of the 4+1 decomposition, the sector of categorical reasoning about categorical reasoning.

## What this chapter contributes

*III.D73* (Proof Theory as E₃) populates the Layer Template at E₃ for the domain of proof theory: carrier = formal systems viewed as mathematical objects, predicate = provability about provability (metatheory soundness relative to object theory), decoder = metatheoretic interpretation (translating syntactic metatheorems into structural diagnoses such as the host-level property concept), invariant = metatheoretic consistency (stability of conclusions under changes of metatheory). The E₂→E₃ handoff is identified as the locus of the incompleteness phenomena: error-correction capacity, the E₂ invariant, becomes the object of study at E₃. *III.D74* (Diagrammatic Sector of E₃) identifies the ω-coupling sector at the terminal enrichment level — categorical reasoning about categorical reasoning — as the sector in which Book III operates. The Hinge Theorem (*III.T41*) is shown to be an E₃ statement in this sector: it concerns the architecture of the entire enrichment ladder, a statement that can only be made from outside the ladder.
