The Bridge Ledger tabulated each bridge's status across eight Millennium Problems. This chapter performs the final accounting: it collects what is unconditionally proved, states what remains conjectural and precisely why, records the one established case and the one broken bridge, and closes the Orthodox Bridge arc. The result is condensed into a single theorem — the Honest Claim (*III.T47*) — with three clauses. Every τ-internal theorem is unconditional, depending only on seven axioms, five generators, and the enrichment tower, with no adjustable parameters (the No Knobs Theorem *III.T42* guarantees this). Every bridge to a classical Millennium Problem is either established, broken, or conjectural. In every conjectural case the open question reduces to a single identification clause I_k: does the bridge functor F preserve the structure exploited by the τ-internal theorem when mapping to the classical domain? The chapter compiles the full τ-effective inventory — eleven theorems, none of which invokes the bridge functor or references ZFC — and presents the Bridge Settlement Criteria (*III.R34*) specifying the three conditions that would close any given conjectural gap. The argument then turns structural: the honest position is also the strong position, because no other framework places all eight problems in a single enrichment structure, and the identification gap for each problem is narrow, testable at finite primorial depth, and requires no new axioms.

## What this chapter contributes

*III.T47* (Honest Claim Theorem) is the programme's principal accountability statement. Three clauses: (i) τ-internal results are unconditional — the Hinge Theorem (*III.T41*) and No Knobs (*III.T42*) certify this; (ii) bridges are established, broken, or conjectural — the Bridge Ledger (*III.T46*) certifies this; (iii) the open question in every conjectural case is whether F preserves the relevant structure, formulated as I_k. *III.R34* (Bridge Settlement Criteria) specifies the three conditions to close I_k: exhibit a natural transformation η_k: F ∘ T_k ⇒ P_k ∘ F on the relevant subcategory, verify η_k is an isomorphism on objects, and check tower-compatibility at every primorial depth. These conditions require no new axioms. The eleven τ-effective theorems inventoried (Spectral Purity, BSD Coherence, Functoriality, Mass Gap, Hodge Addressability, NS Regularity, τ-P = τ-NP, Poincaré, No Barrier, Hinge, No Knobs) are explicitly confirmed as ZFC-free.

## Lean coverage

*III.T47* is *τ-effective* for its unconditional clause and inherits *conjectural* scope for the bridge clauses. *III.R34* is *conjectural*. The τ-effective inventory carries scope labels from originating chapters; all eleven theorems have Lean verification obligations inherited from Parts I–VIII.

## Where this leads

This chapter closes the Orthodox Bridge arc. Chapters 72–75 ascend to E₃ to explain why the bridge has this structure — why incompleteness phenomena arise at the E₂–E₃ boundary, why four paradoxes trace its perimeter, and why E₃ saturates. The Honest Claim remains the fixed reference point throughout: the E₃ perspective diagnoses the structure but does not alter the accounting of what is and is not proved.
