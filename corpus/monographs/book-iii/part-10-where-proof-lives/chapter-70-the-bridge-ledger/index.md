With the Bridge Axiom (*III.D71*) in hand and the three-layer template demonstrated on the Riemann Hypothesis (*III.T45*), this chapter applies the template systematically to all eight Millennium-scale problems and records the result in a single ledger. The ledger functions both as a validity check and as an accountability statement: it forces every claim into a structure where the τ-internal theorem (Layer 1), the classical formulation (Layer 2), and the bridge identification gap (Layer 3) are displayed side by side, with the primary forbidden-move obstruction named for each conjectural entry. The RH gap is the spectral-to-zeros identification with move 2 as primary obstruction; the Navier–Stokes gap is the fluid-data-to-Schwartz identification with move 4; the Yang–Mills gap is the τ-gauge-to-SU(3) identification with move 4; Hodge, BSD, and Langlands each carry a move-5 obstruction. The outcome partitions the eight problems into three categories — six conjectural bridges, one broken bridge, one established result — and the chapter argues that both exceptions confirm rather than undermine the three-layer pattern. The P vs. NP bridge break is diagnosed as a category error: the τ-internal computational universe and the ZFC-abstract Turing machine universe are structurally incomparable, with the bridge degenerating at moves 1, 3, and 4 simultaneously. The Poincaré independence is recorded honestly: two proofs confirming the same geometric fact from entirely disjoint frameworks.

## What this chapter contributes

*III.T46* (Bridge Ledger) is the central theorem: the complete three-column table for all eight Millennium Problems, formally partitioned into six conjectural, one broken, and one established. For each conjectural entry, the primary forbidden-move obstruction is named. *III.R32* (P vs. NP Bridge Break) formalises the diagnosis: the bridge degenerates at moves M₁, M₃, and M₄ simultaneously, and the τ-internal result τ-P = τ-NP (*III.T33*) is a category-error misread as a classical statement — it neither proves nor refutes P ≠ NP. *III.R33* (Poincaré Established) records the only ledger entry where no bridge functor is required: Perelman's 2003 proof and the τ-internal simply-connected theorem (*III.T24*) are independent confirmations of the same fact.

## Lean coverage

*III.T46* is *conjectural* in scope (the bridge functor is not yet constructed). *III.R32* is *τ-effective* — the diagnostic remark derives from the Move-Bridge Correspondence (*III.T43*), which is already proved. *III.R33* is *established*. Each Layer 1 result cited in the ledger carries its own scope label from the originating chapter.

## Where this leads

Chapter 71 (*III.T47*, The Honest Claim) condenses the ledger into a single three-clause theorem separating unconditional τ-internal results from conjectural and established bridge cases, and introduces the Bridge Settlement Criteria (*III.R34*) specifying what closing any conjectural gap would require. The ledger's partition — six open identification gaps, one diagnosed bridge break, one established bridge — is the direct source of the programme's position heading into the E₃ arc of Chapters 72–75.
