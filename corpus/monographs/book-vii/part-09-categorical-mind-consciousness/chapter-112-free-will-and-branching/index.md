The self-recognition loop of Chapter 111 established a subject that can attribute intentions and actions as its own. This chapter asks how that subject relates to alternatives. The action category Act_C is introduced for a decision context C: objects are feasible situation-states, morphisms are feasible action-transitions, and branching is ordinary categorical multiplicity — multiple outgoing morphisms from a given situation. The mind-topos enters through an intention interface ℐ : 𝓜 × Goals → Act_C that selects or ranks morphisms on the basis of internal evaluations (beliefs, preferences, commitments), and the self-recognition loop provides the identification morphism that anchors the selected action to the self-object 𝒮. Freedom is thus not the absence of determination but determination by the right kind of cause: the agent's own evaluative structure. The apparent conflict between determinism and free will is shown to be a level confusion: determinism applies to the physical trajectory in τ³ (a single actual path), free will applies to the branching structure of Act_C (the multiplicity of feasible alternatives). These are distinct mathematical objects, and the existence of a unique actual trajectory does not collapse the category of possibilities. The chapter closes with a preview of the One Free Choice — the foundational commitment to engage with all four registers at all — developed in Part XI.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** none introduced independently; Act_C is defined as a category of feasible actions in context C.
- **Key results:** *VII.T43 — Free Will as Branching* (τ-effective): an E₃ agent exercises free will when Act_C has branch points, the mind-topos provides reason-guided selection via ℐ, and self-attribution anchors the action to 𝒮. *VII.P26 — Compatibilism Dissolution* (τ-effective): determinism and free will operate at different structural levels and are logically independent.
- **Dependencies:** *VII.T39* (Chapter 106), *VII.T42* (Chapter 111); four-register model from Book VII Part I.
