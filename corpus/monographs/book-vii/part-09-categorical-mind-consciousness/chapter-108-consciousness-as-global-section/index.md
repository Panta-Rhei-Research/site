The mind-topos of Chapter 106 contains local sections — percepts, affects, motor readiness, working-memory traces — assigned to sub-regions of the carrier's integration domain Ω_int. This chapter asks what it means for all those local sections to belong to a single experiential field, and answers with a sheaf-theoretic account: a conscious episode is a global section ψ ∈ 𝓜(Ω_int) that glues compatible local data into one unified state. Unity is not an added property requiring a central theatre; it is a consequence of the gluing axiom. The classical binding problem — who binds distinct feature representations into a unified percept — dissolves once binding is identified with the compatibility condition that permits gluing. The chapter also classifies failure modes (hemispatial neglect, split-brain, anaesthesia, dissociation) as distinct breakdowns of the gluing condition, and positions the account relative to Global Workspace Theory, Integrated Information Theory, and Predictive Processing as a common mathematical language for their empirical claims.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** none introduced independently; this chapter refines the mind-topos of VII.D82 into a sheaf 𝓜(–) over the carrier domain with restriction maps and the compatibility/gluing conditions.
- **Key results:** *VII.T41 — Consciousness as Global Section* (τ-effective): a conscious episode is a global section of the mind sheaf; unity, content, and access all follow from the gluing axiom. *VII.L14 — Binding as Gluing* (τ-effective): feature binding is equivalent to the compatibility condition for gluing local sections.
- **Dependencies:** *VII.D82 — Mind as Internal Topos* (Chapter 106); sheaf theory from Book VI.
