Mind is not purely cognitive — it is suffused with feeling. This chapter develops the categorical account of emotions by treating them as morphisms in 𝓜 that arise when content crosses between registers of the metaphysics register model. The four registers (Reg_E empirical, Reg_P practical, Reg_D diagrammatic, Reg_C commitment) are not sealed compartments: content regularly moves across their boundaries, and emotions are the felt quality of that transit — the signal that two evaluative frameworks are simultaneously engaged and that something is at stake across their boundary. The Emotions as Register-Crossings Theorem identifies six canonical crossing families: E→P (fear), P→E (guilt), D→C (awe), C→D (doubt), E→C (grief), P→C (resolve). Complex emotions arise from simultaneous or composite crossings. Distinct from but prior to these episodic emotions is affect: a pre-symbolic valence/arousal field on the carrier domain that orients the system toward or away from register transitions before any explicit cognitive appraisal occurs. Affect operates at the subsymbolic E₀ stratum of 𝓜 and constitutes the felt pressure that makes register-crossings experienced rather than merely processed. The account aligns with cognitive appraisal theory (Lazarus, Scherer) while refining it: appraisals determine which crossing is occurring, while register-crossing theory identifies the structural locus. Emotional intelligence is re-expressed as fluent navigation of register-crossings — perception, understanding, regulation, and utilisation — and the chapter closes by grounding the embodiment of emotion in the dual-aspect architecture: mind is defined over the carrier domain, so transitions in 𝓜 are expressed as transitions in Ω_body.
