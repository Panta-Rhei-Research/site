Where does the mind stop and the world begin? Within the categorical framework, the question dissolves into a structural one about morphism boundaries. Because mind is morphism structure (Chapter 106) and morphisms are not confined to the skull, the extended mind thesis is not a surprising addition but a structural prediction of the framework. This chapter formalises the prediction via the Extended Mind as Carrier Extension Proposition: the carrier domain Ω_body admits extension to Ω_ext = Ω_body ∪ Ω_tool when an external substrate Ω_tool satisfies four integration conditions — reliability (consistently available and typically invoked), trust (retrieved information is endorsed without routine scrutiny), accessibility (retrieval does not require extended search), and prior endorsement (stored content was consciously endorsed at time of storage). When these conditions hold, the extended mind-topos 𝓜_ext over Ω_ext inherits the full topos structure of 𝓜, with the sheaf gluing condition serving as the precise mechanism through which internal and external sections compose into one coherent cognitive process. Clark and Chalmers' parity principle is thereby not endorsed as a stipulation but derived as a consequence of substrate-independence. The integration conditions also dissolve the "cognitive bloat" objection: the internet fails them for most users, while Otto's notebook satisfies all four. The chapter extends the analysis to tools and notational systems, digital devices, and social extension via transactive memory and institutional knowledge.
