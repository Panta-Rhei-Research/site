Having experiences is one thing; representing oneself as having them is another. This chapter develops the second layer: self-awareness as a reflexive loop inside the mind-topos. Two morphisms are required beyond the self-object 𝒮 already present from Chapter 107 — a representation morphism ρ : 𝒮 → R_𝒮 producing an internal model of the self, and an identification morphism ι : R_𝒮 → 𝒮 mapping that representation back as mine. Self-recognition holds when the composite ι ∘ ρ ≃ Id_𝒮. The central theorem shows that this loop condition is equivalent to the SelfDesc-of-SelfDesc property at level E₃ from Book VI (Theorem VI.T30), and equivalently to the awareness-of operator 𝒜 having a fixed point: 𝒜(𝒮) ≅ 𝒮. This fixed point terminates the higher-order regress that besets naive higher-order theories of consciousness — iterating 𝒜 beyond one application yields nothing structurally new. The chapter then reinterprets mirror self-recognition experiments as probes of the identification morphism ι, with modality dependence (dogs fail visual mark tests but pass olfactory protocols) following directly from the structural account. Metacognition — confidence monitoring, theory of mind — is identified as the E₃ capacity to operate on the morphisms, not merely the objects, of 𝓜.
