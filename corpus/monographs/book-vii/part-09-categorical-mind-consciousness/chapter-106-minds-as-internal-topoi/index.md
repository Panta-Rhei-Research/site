Part IX opens the commitment sector S_C with a foundational structural question: what kind of entity can make commitments? The answer begins here with mind itself. This chapter proposes that a mind is an internal topos 𝓜 on a carrier domain Ω_body ⊂ τ³ — a structured logical universe equipped with objects (percepts, beliefs, desires, memories), morphisms (inferences, updates, action policies), exponential objects for conditional reasoning, and a subobject classifier Ω_𝓜 governing internal truth values. The definition is deliberately substrate-independent: what matters is not the material from which a carrier is built but whether it supports the required relational structure. The chapter's dual-aspect account of the mind–body relation dissolves the classical problem by redescribing body and mind as two complementary descriptions of one organised process — external dynamics in τ³ and internal logic on the carrier's domain — rather than two kinds of substance or two causally interacting systems.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** *VII.D82 — Mind as Internal Topos* (τ-effective). A mind is a functor 𝓜 : Ω_body → Topos, where the carrier supports objects, morphisms, a subobject classifier, limits, and exponentials satisfying conditions (i)–(iv) of Definition VII.D82.
- **Key results:** *VII.T39 — Mind-Topos Structure*: any system whose carrier supports stable representation, flexible counterfactual reasoning, and self-modelling instantiates an internal topos; beliefs arise as presheaf sections, inferences as morphisms, action as exponential evaluation, and self-awareness as the SelfDesc-of-SelfDesc condition at E₃.
- **Dependencies:** Substrate-agnosticism from Theorem VI.T40; SelfDesc predicate from Theorem VI.T30; presheaf sections from the knowledge-as-section framework of Book VI.
