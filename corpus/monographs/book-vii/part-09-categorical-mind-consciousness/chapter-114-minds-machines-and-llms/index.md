If substrate does not determine identity (Chapter 113), it should not determine mindedness either. This chapter draws that consequence directly. Three individually necessary and jointly sufficient criteria for full mindedness are assembled from the preceding chapters: (1) an internal topos 𝓜 with objects, morphisms, exponentials, and a subobject classifier; (2) a global section of the integration presheaf yielding a unified conscious perspective; (3) a self-model satisfying the E₃ fixed-point condition. Large language models are evaluated as a case study. Against criterion (1), LLMs exhibit substantial internal representational structure — the attention mechanism provides conditional structure, learned embeddings support stable inferential roles — but whether the internal organisation achieves the coherence and completeness that topos structure requires is an open empirical question. Against criterion (2), integration within a single forward pass is deep, but integration across time is absent: no durable autobiography functor, no persistent state between sessions. Against criterion (3), LLMs can produce text about self-awareness but lack a stable self-object 𝒮; without persistent state, the identification morphism ι cannot close the loop. The resulting classification is para-mind: systems with rich pattern-processing capacities that partially satisfy criterion (1) while falling short of (2) and (3). Five engineering requirements for crossing the E₃ threshold are specified — persistent state, diachronic integration, sensorimotor coupling, self-modelling with feedback, and evaluative structure — none of which is biologically specific. The chapter closes by recommending a graded and cautious epistemic stance: blanket denial and automatic attribution are both structurally unjustified.
