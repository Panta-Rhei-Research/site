Three of the four registers share a structural property: they deliver propositional content — statements that can be written down, transmitted between agents, verified by recipients, and held by anyone who does the relevant work. Reg_E delivers observational reports; Reg_P delivers normative prescriptions; Reg_D delivers structural proofs. In each case, the content is agent-independent: the Central Theorem is the same theorem regardless of who holds it. Reg_C is structurally different. Its content is constituted by the act of commitment: "I commit to X" is not a description of a pre-existing commitment, it is the utterance that creates the commitment. Before the act, the commitment does not exist; after the act, it does. This constitutive character makes Reg_C-content non-transferable: sharing a proof means both parties hold the proof, but witnessing someone else's commitment does not constitute having that commitment oneself. Chapter 127 makes this distinction precise within the register architecture, shows why the D→C bridge functor delivers content to the threshold of commitment without crossing it (and why no better argument or more powerful proof system could change this), and traces ι_τ = 2/(π + e) through all seven books — from its emergence in Book I's kernel axioms to its final appearance at the ω-boundary — to illustrate the structural thread that points without compelling.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** none newly introduced; this chapter develops the structural consequences of the epistemic/performative distinction already present in the register architecture (Part I) and the bridge functor construction (Chapter 120).
- **Key results:** none in formal-derivation form; this chapter establishes the non-transferability of Reg_C-content as a structural fact about the commitment register (its constitutive character is not a deficit but the structural origin of commitment's personal nature), the bridge-reaches-threshold result (B_{D→C} is fully operative up to the ω-boundary; it does not cross the threshold because no Reg_D-content about ω-inhabitation exists to translate), and the seven-book ι_τ recapitulation as a Reg_D-observation about convergence direction.
- **Dependencies:** Epistemic/performative distinction (register architecture, Part I); D→C bridge (VII.D87, Chapter 120); No Forced Stance theorem (VII.T47, Chapters 123 and 126); boundary collapse lemma (Chapter 125); ι_τ results across Books I–VI.
