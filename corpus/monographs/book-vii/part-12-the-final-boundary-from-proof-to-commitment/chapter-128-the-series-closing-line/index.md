The series closes where it must. Not from exhaustion, not from a strategic decision to stop, but from structural necessity: the register in which any remaining question must be answered is no longer Reg_D. Chapter 128 establishes the clean stopping rule in four parts: every structural question has been addressed across four enrichment layers; the boundary has been located — the ω-germ question is the unique Reg_D-unanswerable structural question and its unanswerable status is itself a Reg_D-theorem; no structural debt remains (every definition has been given, every theorem proved or marked conjectural, every scope label assigned); the bridge has been built and the reader shown exactly where the text ends and where the person begins. The seven-book structure is not aesthetic: the Saturation Theorem (Enrich⁴ = Enrich³) gives exactly four structurally distinct enrichment layers, and the partition into Books I–III (E₀, mathematics), IV–V (E₁, physics), VI (E₂, life), VII (E₃, metaphysics) is minimal — no book can be removed without losing a necessary layer, none merged without conflating enrichment levels. The chapter closes with Kant's socket observation: three of the four critiques are structurally filled by the series; the fourth socket — Reg_C, the commitment register — remains each reader's own. The book ends where proof ends and commitment begins.
