Chapter 123 stated the boundary collapse lemma and deferred its proof. Chapter 125 delivers that proof. The argument proceeds in three steps. First, every Reg_D-proof is a finite chain terminating in kernel axioms K0–K6, all formulated at E₀–E₂, with definitional extensions reaching at most E₃. Second, any putative proof that ω is inhabited must refer to ω as an object — as the domain from which existential witnesses are drawn — while simultaneously depending on ω as the closure point of the enrichment ladder that provides the proof system's fixed-point guarantee and coherence conditions. Third, this reflexive situation is not a classical logical contradiction; it is a structural reflexivity where ω serves as both subject and tool of the same proof, so the proof is not independent of its conclusion but instead establishes it from within ω's own structural authority. The collapse is symmetric: proving ω empty requires the same ω-level presuppositions, since exhaustive enumeration of the absence of ω-level content itself requires ω-level resources. The result is then framed not as incompleteness but as precise self-location: the framework has identified its own boundary with surgical precision, and this is an achievement of the same kind — though distinct in mechanism — as Gödel's locating a gap within the diagrammatic register rather than between registers.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** none newly introduced; this chapter proves the claim whose formal apparatus was established as VII.D89 (Subject–Tool Collapse) in Chapter 123.
- **Key results:** *Boundary Collapse Lemma* (τ-effective; proof of the statement in VII.D89/VII.T47, Chapter 123): for every putative Reg_D-proof π of "ω is inhabited", ω ∈ Presup(π). The proof is structural: ω's dual role as closure point of the enrichment ladder and as target of the existential quantification forces it into every such proof's presuppositional apparatus. The collapse is distinguished from Gödel incompleteness: Gödel locates unprovable sentences within a register; the boundary collapse locates a gap between registers.
- **Dependencies:** Subject–Tool Collapse definition (VII.D89, Chapter 123); Reg_D architecture and finite proof chains (Part I); enrichment ladder and Saturation Theorem (earlier Book VII); non-diagrammatic result from Book VI (VI.T40).

## Where this leads

Chapter 126 draws the immediate structural consequence: because the diagrammatic register cannot settle the ω-germ question in either direction, it also cannot force the commitment register to adopt any particular stance — theistic, atheistic, or agnostic positions are all equally non-derivable, and the framework's structural openness at ω is itself a τ-effective theorem.
