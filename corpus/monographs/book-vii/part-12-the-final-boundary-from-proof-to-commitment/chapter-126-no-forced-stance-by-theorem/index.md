In the normal operation of the four-register architecture, a Reg_D-theorem constrains the space of available Reg_C-stances: if something is structurally necessary, any commitment that denies it is incoherent. This forcing relationship from proof to commitment presupposes that the diagrammatic register delivers a result. At ω, the boundary collapse breaks that presupposition entirely. The D→C bridge functor B_{D→C} is structurally intact; its input set at ω is simply empty. Chapter 126 draws the full consequence: VII.T47, the No Forced Stance theorem, now grounded by the proof of Chapter 125 rather than merely stated. Neither theism ("ω is inhabited") nor atheism ("ω is empty") is Reg_D-derivable — and a sharper result than the usual epistemological observation, because the obstacle is not insufficient evidence but the structural reflexivity of ω in the proof system's own coherence conditions. Crucially, the same applies to agnosticism: suspending judgment is itself a Reg_C-stance, a commitment to non-commitment. The framework does not privilege the uncommitted position or install a default. Alongside this structural openness, the chapter insists that the landscape is not flat: the enrichment ladder converges, S_L provides a preferred structural direction toward coincidence of knowing and doing, and ι_τ threads all seven books toward the closure point. The direction is a Reg_D-observation; walking toward it is a Reg_C-act.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** none introduced; this chapter develops the full consequences of VII.T47 (No Forced Stance, Chapter 123) once grounded by the boundary collapse proof of Chapter 125.
- **Key results:** *VII.T47 — No Forced Stance* (τ-effective, now fully proved): theistic, atheistic, and agnostic stances on the ω-germ question are each non-mandated by the framework. The symmetry of the collapse is exact — the same subject–tool mechanism blocks both existential and universal claims. The structural openness at ω preserves the gap between Reg_D and Reg_C at precisely the point where it matters most, and this preservation is itself a structural feature, not an oversight. The chapter also delivers the observation that the landscape has definite shape (saturation, Logos sector, ι_τ convergence) without that shape constituting a command.
- **Dependencies:** Boundary Collapse Lemma (Chapter 125); No Forced Stance theorem statement (VII.T47, Chapter 123); D→C bridge functor (VII.D87, Chapter 120); four-register architecture (Part I).
