The diagrammatic sector S_D opens not with formal proof but with something more primitive: the pre-conceptual recognition of pattern. This chapter establishes that aesthetic sensitivity is subsymbolic — operative before any stable stock of public concepts is available — and provides a formal account of what that means. Three lines of evidence motivate the claim. Developmentally, neonates attend to face-like stimuli and symmetric patterns within hours of birth, before concept formation. Comparatively, non-human animals exhibit structured aesthetic preferences: bowerbirds construct elaborate displays, songbirds select mates by vocal complexity, and primates respond differentially to rhythmic regularity. Phenomenologically, adult humans frequently register "rightness" or "fit" before they can articulate what they are responding to — aesthetic judgment precedes aesthetic reason. A motif is defined as a structured pattern recognizable across changes of viewpoint, medium, or realization. The chapter's core contribution is *VII.D46 — Pre-Symbolic Resonance*: a motif is resonant when it is transportable under admissible transformations, meaning the pattern survives restriction to subregions and transport across perspective changes without requiring re-interpretation. Aesthetic experience is the phenomenological correlate of this transportability — the felt recognition that a pattern "works" from wherever it is approached. The ordering consequence is structural: logic presupposes language, language presupposes pattern recognition, and pattern recognition presupposes this pre-conceptual aesthetic engagement. Part IV therefore begins where the diagrammatic register itself begins.

## What this chapter contributes

Given a motif presheaf ℳ : Persp^op → Set and an admissible transformation group G_adm, a motif m ∈ ℳ(U) is resonant if g*(m|_V) ≅ m|_{g(V)} for all subregions V and all g ∈ G_adm. Pre-symbolic resonance is the phenomenological correlate of this transportability.
- **Key results:** none in formal-derivation form; this chapter establishes the genetic and structural priority of aesthetic pattern recognition over symbolic representation, introduces the tension spectrum (zero, moderate, high), and positions aesthetics as the opening register of S_D.
