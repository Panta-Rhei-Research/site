The Platonism-nominalism debate asks whether mathematical entities are substances in a separate realm or useful fictions. Both camps share a presupposition this chapter removes: substance ontology. The Abstract Object as Structural Position definition (*VII.D36*) specifies that abstract objects in τ are structural positions characterized by three conditions: positional identity determined entirely by morphism profile (AO1), sharpened by rigidity from "up to isomorphism" to strict identity; no intrinsic nature beyond the morphism profile (AO2); and access through the diagrammatic register Reg_D rather than through intellectual intuition directed at a separate realm (AO3). The definition extends Resnik's and Shapiro's mathematical structuralism to ontology in general, adding two refinements: rigidity provides strict rather than isomorphic uniqueness, and register stratification explains why the same kernel position appears different depending on how it is accessed. The Platonism-Nominalism Dissolution remark (*VII.R10*) diagnoses the failure of both traditional positions as presupposition failure: neither mathematical substances (against Platonism) nor dispensable fictions (against nominalism), but structural positions with the same ontic standing as any other admissible τ-construction. The discovery-invention question dissolves in parallel: mathematical investigation is traversal of the address space — the positions were always there (NF addresses are determined by axioms), but the traversal is a genuine cognitive achievement. The "unreasonable effectiveness" of mathematics in physics is explained: both are projections of the same kernel through different registers.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** *VII.D36 — Abstract Object as Structural Position* (τ-effective): three conditions AO1–AO3 identifying abstract objects with morphism-profile-determined positions accessed through Reg_D.
- **Key results:** *VII.R10 — Platonism-Nominalism Dissolution*: philosophical remark showing that both traditional positions presuppose substance ontology; the well-formed replacement question ("Are there admissible τ-constructions accessible through Reg_D?") is trivially affirmative.
- **Notation introduced:** none new; Reg_D (diagrammatic register) used as established in Part I.
