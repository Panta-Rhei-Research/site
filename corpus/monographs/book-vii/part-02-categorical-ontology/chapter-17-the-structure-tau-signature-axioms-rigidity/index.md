What is the structure that Relational Primacy names as the ontic substrate? This chapter exhibits Category τ for philosophical readers, not as a technical recapitulation of Book I but as a reading of what each element means and excludes. The five generators — α (complementarity), π (projection), γ (crossing), η (mediating recurrence), ω (terminal coherence) — are shown to follow from the lemniscate topology 𝕃 = S¹ ∨ S¹: two lobes, one crossing point, and the initial and terminal morphisms required by categorical coherence yield exactly five independent generators, no more and no fewer. The seven axioms K0–K6 are rendered as exclusion principles: each eliminates a class of possible structures. Global coherence and uniqueness — terminality of ω and rigidity Aut(τ) = {id} — are theorems derivable from K0–K6 rather than separate postulates, a compression that is philosophically significant. Rigidity converts τ from one member of a family of τ-like structures into the unique structure: there is nothing to choose, and every element is identified exactly by its structural position.
