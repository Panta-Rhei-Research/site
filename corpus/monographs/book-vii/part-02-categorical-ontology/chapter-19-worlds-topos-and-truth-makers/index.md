Truth within τ is not a bivalent property but a structured one. The presheaf topos over τ carries a subobject classifier Ω whose elements form a Heyting algebra, providing a space of truth values that includes local, partial, and conditional truth alongside the classical extremes. This chapter develops three interlocking accounts. First, possible worlds are reconstructed as internal domains of the presheaf topos (*VII.R06*) — objects satisfying a local consistency condition, connected by accessibility morphisms, with the actual world as the terminal domain of global sections. Second, truth-makers are classified into four structural types (*VII.D27*): inclusion monomorphisms (TM1), global sections of presheaves (TM2), commuting diagrams (TM3), and structural invariants (TM4). Third, the Coherence-Correspondence Unification Theorem (*VII.T11*) proves that the sheaf condition on a Grothendieck site (τ, J_τ) simultaneously encodes local coherence and global existence — the two conditions correspond respectively to the coherence and correspondence theories of truth, which the sheaf condition requires jointly. Truth is therefore local-to-global rather than a brute correspondence or a self-enclosed coherence, and this account varies consistently by register across Reg_E, Reg_P, Reg_D, and Reg_C.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** *VII.D27 — Truth-Maker in τ* (τ-effective): four types TM1–TM4 classifying the structural witnesses that make propositions true.
- **Key results:** *VII.T11 — Coherence-Correspondence Unification*: the sheaf equalizer condition simultaneously requires local-to-global coherence (coherence theory) and unique global existence (correspondence theory); proof by restatement of sheaf definition in philosophical vocabulary. *VII.R06 — Possible Worlds as Internal Domains*: structural reconstruction of modal semantics within the presheaf topos, avoiding Lewisian concrete pluralism.
- **Notation introduced:** Ω (subobject classifier); (τ, J_τ) (Grothendieck site on τ); TM1–TM4 (truth-maker types).
- **Dependencies:** Chapter 18 (internal ontology, NF-addressability); sheaf theory and Grothendieck topologies (Book II); four registers (Part I).
