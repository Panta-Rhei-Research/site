The lemniscate 𝕃 = S¹ ∨ S¹ is not a passive container wall but an active interface: a constitutive structure through which two domains separate, interact, and mutually determine each other. This chapter develops bulk-boundary duality from the ontological reading of the Central Theorem. The Bulk-Boundary Duality definition (*VII.D30*) states that a τ-finite carrier X satisfies bulk-boundary duality when the inclusion ι: ∂X ↪ X induces an isomorphism of function algebras 𝒪(X) ≅ A_spec(∂X) — no information about the interior is lost in passing to the boundary. The paradigm instance is 𝒪(τ³) ≅ A_spec(𝕃). The duality has both epistemic and ontic readings: epistemically, complete boundary knowledge yields complete interior knowledge; ontically, the entity is its boundary structure, leaving no Kantian thing-in-itself lurking behind the phenomena. The chapter extends the principle to interface philosophy — four principles (constitutive, faithful, minimal, active) governing any two coherence domains that interact — and applies it to physics (event horizons), biology (cell membranes), the self-world distinction (mutual constitution via the crossing point γ), and the dissolution of the classical problem of other minds through structural isomorphism rather than unreliable inference.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** *VII.D30 — Bulk-Boundary Duality* (τ-effective): isomorphism condition ι*: 𝒪(X) → A_spec(∂X) for τ-finite carriers, with the Central Theorem as paradigm instance.
- **Key results:** none in formal-derivation form; this chapter establishes bulk-boundary duality as an ontological principle and develops its philosophical consequences (interface philosophy, elimination of the thing-in-itself, perception as boundary-reading).
- **Notation introduced:** ι* (pullback along the boundary inclusion); interface philosophy (as named framework).
- **Dependencies:** Book II (Central Theorem 𝒪(τ³) ≅ A_spec(𝕃)); Chapter 21 (τ³ Philosophical Framing, VII.D29); Chapter 16 (Relational Primacy, VII.D23).
