Causation, space, and time are among the oldest categories in empirical ontology, and all three have resisted principled structural grounding. This chapter provides that grounding in three parallel moves. Causation is reconstructed as constrained composition (*VII.D32*): a morphism f: A → B causes g: A → C when g = h ∘ f with h admissible — the causal relation is the factorization structure of the kernel's morphism composition, removing the demand for a mysterious "secret connexion." The definition handles transitivity (via associativity of composition), asymmetry (via non-reversibility of admissible factorizations), and dissolves pre-emption, overdetermination, and absence causation without ad hoc additions. Space is reconstructed as the relational structure encoded in the five generators and their composition relations: three independent projection directions (π onto τ¹, and γ, η onto the two circles of T²) yield three spatial dimensions without postulating a background manifold. Time is reconstructed as address persistence through morphism sequences, with the Temporal Ordering Proposition (*VII.P06*) proving that the persistence relation induces a partial order on NF addresses — partial, not total, corresponding structurally to spacelike separation. The result is morphism-structured temporality: neither the static block (because morphisms have directed source and target) nor strong presentism (because past addresses remain NF-addressable).

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** *VII.D32 — Causation as Constrained Composition* (τ-effective): g = h ∘ f with h admissible as the categorical definition of the causal relation; four derived properties (transitivity, asymmetry, context-sensitivity, no action at a distance).
- **Key results:** *VII.P06 — Temporal Ordering from Persistence*: partial order on NF addresses derived from address persistence through composable morphism sequences; proof by reflexivity, antisymmetry via NF-uniqueness, and transitivity.
- **Notation introduced:** ≼_Φ (persistence relation along morphism sequence Φ); addr(f_i) (address of i-th morphism in sequence).
- **Dependencies:** Chapter 23 (admissibility constraints, operator framework); Chapter 18 (NF-addressability); Chapter 20 (derived geometry, spatial structure from generators); Book IV–V (physical interpretations of causation and temporal ordering).
