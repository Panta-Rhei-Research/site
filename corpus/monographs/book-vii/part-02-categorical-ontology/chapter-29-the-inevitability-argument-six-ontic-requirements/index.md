The preceding chapters have grounded ontology in τ, but the question of why τ specifically has not been addressed directly. This chapter develops an inevitability argument: six independent ontic requirements that any adequate foundation of reality must satisfy, with the conjectural claim that their intersection singles out τ. The Six Ontic Requirements definition (*VII.D37*) specifies: (OR1) identity-faithful representation via the full Yoneda Lemma; (OR2) finite signature — finitely many generators and axioms; (OR3) diagonal-free self-reference avoiding Russell, Burali-Forti, and Cantor paradoxes structurally rather than by restricted comprehension; (OR4) address-centric access — every admissible construction has a unique NF address; (OR5) holomorphic continuation — local data determine global structure; (OR6) spectral completeness — internal self-diagnosis of operators. Each requirement is motivated independently, without presupposing the others. The Inevitability Convergence "Theorem" (*VII.T14*) is labeled conjectural throughout: the structural evidence is substantial, but a complete proof that no other structure satisfies all six requirements simultaneously has not been established. The Independent Necessity Proposition (*VII.P08*) — that removing any single requirement admits non-τ solutions — carries the same conjectural label, since the counterexamples are illustrative rather than exhaustive. This is the most speculative chapter in Part II; the remaining chapters return to τ-effective scope.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** *VII.D37 — Six Ontic Requirements* *(conjectural)*: OR1–OR6 specifying individually necessary conditions on any candidate foundation of reality.
- **Key results:** *VII.T14 — Inevitability Convergence* *(conjectural)*: asserts that the intersection C_Yon ∩ C_fin ∩ C_diag ∩ C_NF ∩ C_hol ∩ C_spec = {τ} up to equivalence; not proven in full generality. *VII.P08 — Each Requirement Independently Necessary* *(conjectural)*: counterexample sketches for each requirement showing non-τ solutions if any one is dropped.
- **Notation introduced:** OR1–OR6 (six ontic requirement labels); C_Yon, C_fin, etc. (constraint classes).
- **Dependencies:** Books I–VI (structural evidence for τ satisfying all six requirements); Chapter 17 (finite signature of τ); Chapter 18 (NF-addressability, OR4); Chapter 23 (holomorphic continuation, OR5).
