Traditional metaphysics begins with substances that bear properties and enter into relations. Category τ inverts this explanatory order: morphisms are primitive, and objects are derived — an object is the node characterized by its morphism profile. The Relational Primacy Principle (*VII.D23*) formalizes this inversion in three conditions: morphisms precede relata (RP1), identity is determined by relational position via the Yoneda Lemma (RP2), and there is no haecceity beyond relational position (RP3). The Yoneda Lemma thereby becomes an ontological theorem of individuation, not merely a technical convenience. Category τ functions as ontic substrate — the structure within which "real," "law," and "true" receive their meanings — rather than as a model applied to an independently given world. Historical precursors from Leibniz through Whitehead to ontic structural realism are surveyed, and the categorical sharpening that converts philosophical stance into a provable reconstruction principle is identified. The chapter opens Part II by stating the inversion at its sharpest and defending it against the three most natural objections.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** *VII.D23 — Relational Primacy Principle* (τ-effective). Three-condition definition: (RP1) morphisms first; (RP2) identity via Yoneda reconstruction; (RP3) no haecceity beyond relational position.
- **Key results:** *VII.R03 — Historical Precursors to Relational Ontology*: contextual remark surveying Leibniz, Whitehead, ontic structural realism, and Hegel as proto-forms of the categorical thesis, noting what each lacked.
