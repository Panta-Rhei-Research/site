The capstone chapter of Part II synthesizes the ontological apparatus into a single positive position and closes with the structural anchor of the entire series. Non-Dualistic Platonism (*VII.D40*) is defined by three conditions: single ontology — there is one relational structure τ, not two separate realms (NDP1); epistemic stratification — the apparent difference between physical and mathematical entities is a difference in readout functor, not in ontological status (NDP2); and no realm of forms — Platonic forms are NF addresses in the kernel's address space, not entities in a separate realm (NDP3). The qualifier "non-dualistic" is essential: classical Platonism is dualistic and faces the participation problem; the single-ontology thesis dissolves that problem entirely. What is preserved from Platonism is mind-independence and objectivity, grounded in the rigidity of τ. The Discovery-Invention Dissolution remark (*VII.R12*) identifies mathematical investigation as traversal of the address space — neither pure discovery (as if from outside) nor pure invention (as if NF positions did not already exist), but a genuine cognitive achievement of navigating a pre-determined structure. The ω-Uniqueness Principle (*VII.D41*) states three conditions: terminality (every admissible construction has a unique morphism to ω), non-competition (no locally infinite construction satisfies terminality, because infinity introduces morphism ambiguity), and global uniqueness (ω is unique on the nose by rigidity). The ω-Uniqueness Theorem (*VII.T16*) proves all three parts. Whether ω bears any relation to traditional concepts of a divine principle is deferred to Reg_C and Parts X–XI; the structural facts are earned.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** *VII.D40 — Non-Dualistic Platonism* *(conjectural as a philosophical stance; the structural elements are τ-effective)*: three conditions NDP1–NDP3 specifying single ontology with epistemic stratification and NF addresses as forms. *VII.D41 — ω-Uniqueness Principle* (τ-effective): terminality, non-competition of locally infinite constructions, and global uniqueness of ω.
- **Key results:** *VII.T16 — ω-Uniqueness* (τ-effective): three-part proof — terminal objects are unique up to unique isomorphism (standard); locally infinite constructions cannot be terminal because they admit multiple morphisms from some source; NF address of ω is uniquely reachable from every other address by a single morphism. *VII.R12 — Discovery-Invention Dissolution*: philosophical remark collapsing the discovery-invention dichotomy into "traversal" of the kernel's address space.
- **Notation introduced:** NDP1–NDP3 (Non-Dualistic Platonism conditions); ω-uniqueness as named principle.
- **Dependencies:** Chapter 16 (Relational Primacy, forms as relational positions); Chapter 17 (rigidity and terminality of ω); Chapter 18 (NF-addressability, forms as NF positions); Chapter 28 (structural positions, VII.D36, previewing this chapter's synthesis).
