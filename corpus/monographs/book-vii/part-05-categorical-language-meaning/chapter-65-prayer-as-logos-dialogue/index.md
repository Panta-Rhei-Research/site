Part V closes at the limit of language: the point where the symbolic medium acknowledges its own insufficiency and yields to an orientation that no finite symbol alphabet can fully express. The limit case is prayer—contemplative directed address found across unrelated traditions (Christian oratio, Islamic salat and du'a, Jewish tefillah, Buddhist bhavana, Hindu dhyana). This chapter formalizes the structural act that these practices share, without reducing any tradition to another. An ω-addressed communication is a morphism p : s → ω in the commitment register Reg_C, where s is the speaker's position and ω is the terminal coherence point established by the ω-uniqueness theorem. Three structural features characterize it: directionality (the morphism has a definite target, not merely an emission into ambient symbolic space), asymmetry (the speaker acknowledges that ω is not a peer; ordinary pragmatic reciprocity is not presupposed), and register crossing (the act originates in Reg_D—language—and terminates in Reg_C—existential orientation). The structural effect on the speaker is threefold: orientation (the commitment register is re-oriented toward a referent that exceeds the speaker's own cognitive horizon), integration (all four registers are brought into alignment—empirical honesty, practical intention, linguistic articulation, existential commitment), and silence (sustained practice tends toward a non-linguistic orientation, because the subsymbolic content exceeds what any symbol alphabet can express; the precision–expressiveness trade-off is resolved by abandoning the symbolic medium entirely). The convergence of contemplative practice across independently developed traditions is a structural datum explained by shared kernel architecture: different readout functors applied to the same ω-directed morphism. The chapter operates at two scope levels: the structural analysis of the act (directed address, asymmetry, register crossing) is τ-effective; the identification of ω with a personal divine principle is conjectural, belonging to the commitment register and not adjudicable by diagrammatic means.
