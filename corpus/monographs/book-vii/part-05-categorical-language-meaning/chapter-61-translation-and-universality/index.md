Translation is a functor between linguistic categories that preserves
kernel content. This chapter develops the structural theory of
translation: what is preserved, what is lost, and why universal
bridgeability holds for kernel invariants but not for carrier-level
accretions. The untranslatable is not a deficiency of translation but
a structural fact about local sections that do not extend globally.
A translation schema formalizes the decode–encode pathway through
the subsymbolic kernel.
