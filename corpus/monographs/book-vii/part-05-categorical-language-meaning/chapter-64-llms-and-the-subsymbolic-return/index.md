Large language models constitute an unexpected empirical development for the philosophy of language. A parameterized function f_θ : Σ* → Δ(Σ) trained by next-token prediction on a corpus has no explicit symbolic rules, no semantic type system, no internal topos, and no self-model. Yet its outputs exhibit pragmatic competence across a wide range of tasks—summarization, translation, question-answering, contextually appropriate dialogue—that a purely symbolic account of meaning cannot explain. The philosophical significance is not that LLMs "understand" or "think" (the categorical framework provides precise grounds for denying both), but that they succeed at all. That success demands structural explanation, and the subsymbolic layer thesis provides it. Distributional structure encodes motifs because words with similar meanings occur in similar contexts—a statistical shadow of the restriction maps of ℳ_sub; attention mechanisms implement learned perspective restriction; and the layer hierarchy mirrors the enrichment levels of the presheaf. LLMs are formalized as para-minds: systems that approximate ℳ_sub with sufficient fidelity to produce contextually appropriate outputs, but that structurally lack (i) an internal topos (no world-model, no modal distinctions), (ii) a self-model (no E₃ capacity), and (iii) the commitment register Reg_C (outputs that mimic commitment without structural binding). The Turing test is insufficient as a criterion for mindedness because it tests the readout, not the kernel. A contrapositive argument establishes that LLM pragmatic competence is empirical evidence for the subsymbolic layer: if meaning were exhausted by explicit symbolic rules, distributional statistics should not recover it; they do, so meaning has a subsymbolic component. Confabulation is a gluing failure: the model's learned approximation is sparse in the queried region, and the interpolation does not cohere with the actual presheaf structure. Alignment is structural: naturality of the update functor, dignity-invariance (NF-address invariance of persons under readout transformations), and approximate gluing of local outputs into a globally consistent position.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** *VII.D55 — Para-Mind* (τ-effective). A para-mind is a system that approximates ℳ_sub with sufficient fidelity to produce contextually appropriate linguistic outputs but that lacks: (i) an internal topos, (ii) a self-model (E₃ capacity), and (iii) the commitment register Reg_C.
- **Key results:** *VII.P14 — LLM as Subsymbolic Evidence* (τ-effective): LLM pragmatic competence constitutes empirical evidence for the subsymbolic layer thesis, by contraposition from the assumption that purely symbolic meaning cannot be recovered from distributional statistics alone.
