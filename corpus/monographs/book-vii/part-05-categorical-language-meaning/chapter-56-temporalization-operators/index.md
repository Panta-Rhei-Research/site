the relevant chapter identified temporalization as
language's most consequential addition to the subsymbolic layer.
This chapter formalizes the structure of symbolic time. Tense,
aspect, and mood are not surface features of grammar but functorial
operations on the category of propositions—endofunctors that
relocate, re-shape, and modalize propositional content. The
formalization connects linguistic temporalization to the
phenomenological account of temporal experience developed in
Part III.
