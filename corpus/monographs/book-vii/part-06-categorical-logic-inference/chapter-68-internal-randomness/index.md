Randomness, in τ, is not imported from outside. It arises from
the internal structural complexity of the kernel's constructions.
A process appears random when its description cannot be compressed
below its own length—when it is, in the technical sense,
incompressible. This chapter formalizes internal randomness as a
τ-concept, connects it to Kolmogorov complexity, and shows that
quantum indeterminacy (Book IV) is a special case: the apparent
randomness of quantum measurement outcomes is not external noise
injected into a deterministic substrate but the incompressibility of
the T² fiber's internal structure as seen from the observer's
meso-scale perspective.
