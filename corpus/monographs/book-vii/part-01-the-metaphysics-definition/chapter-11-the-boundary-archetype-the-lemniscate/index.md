The first archetype is extracted: the *boundary archetype*,
whose underlying j-closed fixed point is the lemniscate
𝕃 = S¹ ∨ S¹. As the boundary of the central
object τ³, the lemniscate is the minimal pattern that
exhibits the structural invariant of *threshold-crossing*—the
transition from one coherence domain to another through a controlled
self-intersection. The figure-eight topology encodes the fundamental
duality: two loops sharing a single crossing point, creating a gate
through which every structural transition must pass. The Boundary
Archetype Minimality proposition establishes that no simpler j-closed
pattern carries the threshold-crossing invariant. Cross-cultural
recurrence of boundary imagery is explained as independent readout of
the same kernel invariant, not as cultural diffusion.
