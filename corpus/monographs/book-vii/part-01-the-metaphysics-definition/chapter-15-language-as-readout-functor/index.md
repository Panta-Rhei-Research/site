The j-closed archetypes of Chapters 11–13 are structural features of the kernel's internal organisation. This chapter asks how they become communicable: how does a minimal j-closed fixed point become a theorem, a legal statute, a prayer, a myth, or a gesture? The answer is the readout functor — a functor R: K_τ → Expr from the coherence kernel to a category of expressions. A readout functor is typed when it factors through one of the four register readout functors (R = R_ext ∘ Reg_X); it is untyped when it projects directly from the kernel without factoring through a single register, which is why poetry resists reduction to a single mode of engagement. No readout functor is privileged: different natural languages, formal systems, artistic traditions, and ritual practices are all different readout functors from the same kernel, which accounts structurally for cross-cultural recurrence of archetypal motifs. The two canonical shadow families (VM/ZFC-PA mathematical and epistemic-pragmatic philosophical) are analysed as worked examples of typed and multi-register readouts. The Readout Functor Faithfulness Theorem gives the quality criterion: a readout functor is faithful on archetypes if and only if it preserves all j-closure distinctions, so distinct kernel patterns produce distinct expressions.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** *VII.D22 — Readout Functor* (τ-effective): a functor R: K_τ → Expr to a category of communicable contents; typed when it factors through Reg_X ∘ R_ext for a single X; untyped when it projects directly from the kernel.
- **Key results:** *VII.T10 — Readout Functor Faithfulness* (τ-effective): R is faithful on archetypes if and only if for all j-closed subobjects A₁, A₂, R(A₁) = R(A₂) implies A₁ = A₂; equivalently, distinct j-closed patterns produce distinct expressions. Consequences: faithful readouts distinguish what the kernel distinguishes; unfaithful readouts conflate structurally distinct patterns; faithfulness is a quality criterion, not a cultural ranking.
- **Notation introduced:** R: K_τ → Expr for a general readout functor; R_ext for the externalisation functor from a register codomain to Expr; R_math for the VM/ZFC-PA shadow; R_phil for the epistemic-pragmatic shadow. The cross-cultural archetype-readout table (τ, Greek, Hebrew, East Asian).
- **Dependencies:** Three canonical archetypes (Chapters 11–13); register readout functors (Chapter 3); shadow families (Chapter 6, VII.D12–14); j-closure machinery (Chapter 10).
