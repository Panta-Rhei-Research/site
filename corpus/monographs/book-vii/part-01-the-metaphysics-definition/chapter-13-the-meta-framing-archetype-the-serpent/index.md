The third archetype operates at a level above the other two: the boundary archetype acts on objects, the mitigation archetype acts on states, and the meta-framing archetype acts on framing functors themselves. The meta-framing operation is a natural transformation F ↦ F' that preserves the objects of C while shifting the codomain from D to D' — it alters context rather than content. The serpent in Genesis 3 is the canonical prototype: it does not deny the command but reframes it from command-as-given to command-as-questionable. Trickster figures across traditions — Loki, Coyote, Hermes, Anansi — share this structural signature without cross-cultural contact. The archetype is morally neutral: the same pattern serves Socratic irony and gaslighting alike. The guardrails are structural: legitimate meta-framing preserves j-closure; the liar paradox and the serpent's register conflation do not. Its philosophical consequence is that reflexivity cannot be eliminated from any E₃ observer.
