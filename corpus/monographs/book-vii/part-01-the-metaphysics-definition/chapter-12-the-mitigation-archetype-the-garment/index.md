Every boundary-crossing leaves the system in a state of raw exposure: its prior coherence conditions no longer hold, and a new stabilisation is required. The mitigation archetype formalizes the minimal structural response to this condition. Where the boundary archetype is activated at the threshold, the mitigation archetype is activated only after crossing — its post-boundary response is part of the definition. The covering morphism μ: B → B̄ provides a new coherence envelope adapted to the post-crossing state B, reducing the defect measured by the exposure functor without undoing the crossing (boundary morphisms are non-invertible). Three conditions specify the archetype: post-boundary activation (M1), covering effectiveness (M2), and minimality (M3). The Genesis 3:21 narrative — garments of skin after the opening of eyes — is analysed as a structural prototype: verse 3:21 is protection and accommodation, distinct from the consequences of verses 3:16–19. The j-closure of the mitigation archetype is proved, making repair a kernel invariant rather than an optional supplement: as long as j-closed boundary crossings exist, the mitigation pattern necessarily persists.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** *VII.D19 — Mitigation Archetype* (τ-effective): the minimal j-closed subobject M ↪ Ω satisfying post-boundary activation (M1), covering — the composite B → B̄ → Exp(B̄) has strictly smaller defect than ε: B → Exp(B) — (M2), and minimality (M3). The covering morphism μ: B → B̄ is the garment operator.
- **Key results:** *VII.T49 — j-Closure of the Mitigation Archetype* (τ-effective): j(M) = M; proved by combining the j-closure of the boundary archetype (VII.P05) with the functoriality of the exposure functor and the minimality of M — no refinement can eliminate the need for repair so long as boundary crossings persist.
- **Notation introduced:** M for the mitigation archetype; μ: B → B̄ for the covering morphism; ε: B → Exp(B) for the exposure functor; the boundary-mitigation duality table.
- **Dependencies:** Boundary Archetype and Minimality (Chapter 11, VII.D18, VII.P05); j-closure machinery (Chapter 10, VII.D16); Lawvere–Tierney axioms.
