Kant's discipline of incompleteness, abstracted in Chapter 91, generalises into a grammar for all ethical reasoning. Not every claim made in ethical discourse carries equal structural weight, and conflating claims of different weights generates the apparent paradoxes that have occupied ethical theory for centuries. The Earned/Unearned Ethics Framework (VII.D74) defines an earned move as one satisfying three conditions simultaneously: register-typed (formulated in the appropriate register — obligations in Reg_P, structural patterns in Reg_D, empirical facts in Reg_E); structurally derived (the derivation chain is traceable to the axiomatic base of τ or to certified empirical data); and cross-register consistent (the claim does not contradict earned results in other registers). The probative weight of a claim is the degree to which it satisfies these conditions; an unearned claim has zero probative weight regardless of its psychological force or cultural authority. The three principal failure modes are authority import (importing from Reg_C or external authority without structural mediation), intuition import (treating pre-symbolic Reg_D resonance as if it were a Reg_D proof), and convention import (treating social consensus, a Reg_E fact, as Reg_P warrant). The Pseudo-Paradox from Type Violation proposition (VII.P22) shows that when an earned claim with positive weight conflicts with an unearned claim with zero weight, the result is not a genuine dilemma but a prompt: either earn the zero-weight claim or withdraw it. The chapter applies this analysis to the trolley problem (the "saving five is better" intuition is psychologically powerful but structurally unearned, while the dignity constraint is structurally derived and carries full weight), to the naturalistic fallacy (any Reg_E→Reg_P import without a structural functor), and to divine command theory (the Euthyphro dilemma as a symptom of Reg_C→Reg_P type violation). The First Bombshell returns: most philosophical attempts at ethical proof fail not at the proof stage but at the language stage, because the conventional ethical vocabulary is mostly unearned until given precise categorical definitions.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** *VII.D74 — Earned/Unearned Ethics Framework* (τ-effective). Three conditions (register-typed, structurally derived, cross-register consistent) define an earned move; probative weight is zero for any claim failing at least one condition.
- **Key results:** *VII.P22 — Pseudo-Paradox from Type Violation* (τ-effective): a conflict between a positive-weight earned claim and a zero-weight unearned claim is not a genuine ethical dilemma but a type violation; resolution requires earning the unearned claim or withdrawing it.
