Chapter 88 established that the CI is the minimal j-closed fixed point. This chapter asks why such a fixed point exists at all. The answer unfolds across three stages of a proof programme with explicitly differentiated scope labels. Stage K — the Kernel Theorem (VII.T36, τ-effective) — shows that three properties of τ at its third self-enrichment level E₃ force the existence of a j_dig-closed operator graph: (a) self-enrichment at E₃ provides internal hom objects rich enough to support typed maxims and universalization; (b) the bipolar lemniscate boundary L = S¹ ∨ S¹ generates at least two independent perspectives, making universalization a non-vacuous test; (c) the Yoneda embedding ensures faithfulness, so two maxims agreeing on all perspectives are identical and degenerate graphs are excluded. The result is canonical — no arbitrary choices, because each component is defined by a universal construction and the rigidity of τ propagates. Stage S — the Semantic Object Construction (VII.T37, τ-effective) — shows that typed maxims, universalization domains (sieves in C), personhood predicates (decidable at E₃ because local finiteness of NF addresses makes invariance conditions decidable), and obligation morphisms (dignity-preserving morphisms in A_dig) are all internally constructible at E₃. The ethical domain is built, not imported. Stage CI — the Fixed-Point Uniqueness Conjecture (VII.P21, conjectural) — claims that the minimal j-closed fixed point is unique up to natural isomorphism. The gap is technical: it requires showing that the intersection of j-closed operator graphs is again j-closed (lattice completeness of F), which has not been formally verified. The First Bombshell Lemma (VII.L13, τ-effective) distils the key insight: earning the language to formulate the CI proof is itself a foundational result — the ethical vocabulary (agent, maxim, dignity, obligation) is neither primitive nor empirically derived but generated by τ's self-enrichment, dissolving the is–ought gap without crossing it.
