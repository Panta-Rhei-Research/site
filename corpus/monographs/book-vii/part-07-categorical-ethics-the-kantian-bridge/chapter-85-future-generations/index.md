Chapter 84 extended dignity spatially, across species. This chapter extends it temporally, to persons who do not yet exist. The extension is structural: the site of perspectives (P_T, J_T) is augmented with future temporal charts covering present agents, near-future agents (identity determined by ongoing causal processes), and far-future agents (existence contingent on present decisions, specific identity not yet fixed). Crucially, the inclusion of future charts does not require knowing who future agents will be — only that future perspectives are modeled as charts so that admissibility constraints range over them. Any entity that will have a well-defined NF address has prospective dignity, because the temporal label "future" is contingent and label-independence forbids using it to discount ethical weight. The Intergenerational Obligations remark (VII.R31) applies all four ethical tests to the temporally extended site: temporal gluing (local enactments from present and future perspectives must agree on the temporal boundary), prospective respect (D(M(X_f)) ≅ D(X_f) for future agent-states), temporal coherence (present and future obligations compatible), and temporal monodromy (the loop present → future → present must have trivial holonomy). Irreversibility sharpens both Tests 2 and 4: destroying the conditions under which future agents would possess their invariants is a preemptive erasure of identity-invariants, and the holonomy of the temporal loop through the resulting conditions is not merely nontrivial but permanent. The chapter concludes that sustainability is a coherence constraint, not a preference — admissible policies must glue across the temporally extended site.
