The four-register architecture assigns to Reg_C the mode "what I am willing to live as true." This chapter gives the commitment register its full structural account, distinguishing it from the epistemic registers by register type rather than epistemic quality. Reg_E describes, Reg_P prescribes, Reg_D certifies; Reg_C constitutes through performance. A Reg_C-claim is not a proposition to be evaluated against states of affairs or proofs — its truth-maker is the agent's ongoing enactment of the commitment. Without admissibility constraints, this would collapse into arbitrary assertion. The Commitment Register Admissibility definition (VII.D72) imposes four conditions: consistency with established Reg_E, Reg_P, and Reg_D results (the commitment must not contradict earned outcomes); internal coherence (the agent's commitment set must remain consistent); performative coherence (the commitment is actually lived, not merely stated); and transparency (the agent acknowledges the claim as a Reg_C-claim rather than presenting it as evidence, obligation, or proof — "register smuggling" is inadmissible). The C Register Independence Theorem (VII.T38) proves four structural properties: no conjunction of epistemic claims entails a specific Reg_C-claim (the normalisers differ in kind — performative vs. propositional); no Reg_C-claim is equivalent to epistemic content; Reg_C cannot be eliminated without structural incompleteness (the 4+1 sector decomposition at E₃ necessarily generates all four registers); and admissible Reg_C-claims are compatible with the epistemic registers by the consistency condition. The chapter treats faith without theological vocabulary: it is an admissible Reg_C-commitment that is not a defective form of knowledge, not irrational (it must satisfy the four admissibility conditions), not universally binding (it binds the committing agent, not all agents), and not structurally forced in content (only the slot is structurally forced, not what fills it).

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** *VII.D72 — Commitment Register Admissibility* (τ-effective). Four conditions — consistency, internal coherence, performative coherence, transparency — that an admissible Reg_C-claim must satisfy; register smuggling is structurally inadmissible.
- **Key results:** *VII.T38 — C Register Independence* (τ-effective): Reg_C is non-derivable from, non-reducible to, and non-eliminable relative to the epistemic registers, yet compatible with them through the consistency condition.
- **Dependencies:** Four-register architecture (Reg_E, Reg_P, Reg_D, Reg_C); 4+1 sector decomposition at E₃; witness bundles and normalisers from sector witnesses chapter.
