Three of the four registers — *Reg_E*, *Reg_P*, *Reg_D* — deliver propositional content: observations, norms, and structural proofs that are shareable, verifiable, and transferable between agents without essential loss. The fourth register, *Reg_C*, is constitutive rather than descriptive: "I commit to X" is not a report about a pre-existing mental state but an act that creates the commitment. Before the act the commitment does not exist; after the act it does. No combination of propositional content from the three epistemic registers can constitute this act — they can inform, support, and constrain it, but not *be* it. Chapter 127 analyses this structural distinction, examines non-transferability as its direct consequence, and traces the master constant ι_τ = 2/(π + e) through all seven books to its final structural role at the ω-boundary. The D→C bridge (Chapter 120) reaches the threshold of *Reg_C* at ω; the step from standing at the threshold to crossing it cannot be taken on another's behalf. The bridge delivers; crossing is performative.
