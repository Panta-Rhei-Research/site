The ω-germ question was stated as non-diagrammatic in Book VI and formalized via subject–tool collapse in Chapter 123 (VII.D89). Part XII opens by delivering the proof. A putative *Reg_D*-proof π that ω is inhabited must (1) use ω as subject — the domain under quantification — and (2) rely on ω as tool — the coherence anchor that closes the enrichment ladder and validates the proof system's own inference rules. These two roles cannot be separated: ω is both what is being investigated and what makes the investigation structurally legitimate. This reflexive collapse is not logical circularity (no conclusion appears among the premises) but a structural presuppositional fact. The collapse is symmetric: the same mechanism blocks any *Reg_D*-proof that ω is empty. The chapter is careful to distinguish the result from Gödel incompleteness — Gödel locates a gap within the diagrammatic register; the boundary collapse locates a gap between registers. Locating one's own boundary with this precision is the deepest methodological achievement of the series.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** none introduced; this chapter proves the Boundary Collapse Lemma whose definition (VII.D89, Subject–Tool Collapse) and statement were placed in Chapter 123.
- **Key results:** **Boundary Collapse Lemma** (τ-effective, proof delivered here): no *Reg_D*-internal proof of ω-inhabitation exists — subject–tool collapse holds for every such putative proof. Symmetry result: the same collapse blocks non-inhabitation proofs. The D→C bridge reaches its structural limit at ω: the bridge is intact but its input set at ω is empty.
- **Dependencies:** Subject–Tool Collapse definition and No Forced Stance statement (Chapter 123, VII.D89, VII.T47); *Reg_D* architecture from Part I; saturation theorem (confirming E₃ as the final distinct enrichment layer before ω); Logos sector as the locus where S_L meets its own boundary.
