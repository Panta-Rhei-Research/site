This chapter closes the empirical sector S_E of Book VII. The sector opened with a single question — What do I observe? — and required twenty-five chapters, nineteen definitions, eight theorems, five propositions, and fifteen remarks to answer it rigorously. The answer can be stated in one sentence: what an E₃ observer observes is a global section of the presheaf ℱ : Obs^op → Set over the cover determined by the carrier's sensorimotor boundary, read through the empirical register Reg_E. Six structural commitments are made explicit: observation is structured (section-construction over a cover), perspectival (cover-dependent on position and body), faithful (readout functor Reg_E does not distort), corrigible (error is correctable by cover refinement), shared (multiple observers can glue sections intersubjectively), and embodied (the cover is constrained by the carrier boundary). The Empirical Sector Summary remark catalogs all 51 registry entries across Parts II and III — definitions D23 through D45, theorems T11 through T18, propositions P06 through P10, and remarks R03 through R17 — recording what each chapter contributed. The chapter then argues structurally why S_E is complete: the empirical register has definite scope (what admits sectional description over experience-covers), and both ontology and epistemology within that scope have been exhausted. What remains belongs to other registers: S_D asks what pattern is discernible, S_P asks what ought to be done, S_C asks what the observer commits to.
