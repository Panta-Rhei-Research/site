Chapter 33 established that knowledge is a global section and that justification is one face of the sheaf condition. This chapter develops justification as its own structural object. The decisive move is replacing the chain metaphor — in which each belief is supported by a prior belief, generating regress, circularity, or arbitrary foundation — with the overlap metaphor: a knowledge claim K is justified if and only if K|_{Uᵢ ∩ Uⱼ} = K|_{Uⱼ ∩ Uᵢ} for all pairs of observation contexts in the cover. This condition is finitely checkable on finite covers, which immediately dissolves Agrippa's trilemma: the trilemma exhausts the possibilities for chains, but justification is not a chain. Underdetermination (Quine-Duhem) is reformulated as the existence of multiple global sections over a coarse cover; the Underdetermination as Cover Failure proposition shows that a sheaf — not merely a presheaf — over a sufficiently fine topology distinguishes competing theories. Habermas's force of the better argument receives a structural reading: a better argument is one whose section survives refinement to a finer cover, and rational discourse is progressive cover refinement toward the maximal sieve.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** *VII.D43 — Justification as Gluing* (τ-effective). A knowledge claim is justified if and only if its local data agree on all pairwise overlaps of the observation cover; justification is a symmetric, locally checkable relation, not a directed chain.
- **Key results:** *VII.P09 — Underdetermination as Cover Failure*: two distinct global sections coexist over a cover if and only if the cover is too coarse to distinguish them; the Quine-Duhem thesis is recast as a diagnosis rather than a permanent predicament.
- **Dependencies:** *VII.D42* (Knowledge as Section, Chapter 33); Chapter 27 (worlds-topos presheaf framework).
