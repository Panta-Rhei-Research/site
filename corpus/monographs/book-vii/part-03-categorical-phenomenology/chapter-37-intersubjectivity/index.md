The preceding chapters treated the single E₃ observer. Knowledge, justification, perception, and temporal experience were all developed from within a single perspective. This chapter extends the framework to the multi-observer setting. Each observer Oₖ carries a personal observation cover {Uᵢ^(k)}; the multi-perspectival cover is their union. Contexts belonging to different observers overlap wherever both have data on the same situation — typically through shared behavioral surface. A knowledge claim is objective if and only if it is a global section over this multi-perspectival cover: not the view from nowhere but the view from everywhere that glues. Three misconceptions of objectivity are diagnosed: objectivity is not perspective-elimination (there is no cover without perspectives), not consensus (consensus is necessary but not sufficient), and not observer-independence (objectivity is cover-relative, not observer-relative). Husserl's appresentation from the Fifth Cartesian Meditation is reformulated as a structural operation: I restrict my section s₁ to the behavioral overlap between my cover and yours, yielding a prediction about your experiential state — a gluing hypothesis tested by subsequent observation. The Intersubjectivity Conditions remark identifies three structural preconditions for successful intersubjective gluing: shared register, comparable cover granularity, and genuine overlapping contexts. Register mismatch — physicist observing in Reg_E, poet in Reg_C — is a principled explanation for why some disagreements cannot be resolved by more data.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** none introduced; this chapter develops consequences of *VII.D42* (Knowledge as Section) applied to the multi-observer setting.
- **Key results:** *VII.P10 — Perspectival Gluing Grounds Objectivity*: a knowledge claim is objective if and only if it is a global section over the multi-perspectival cover; objectivity is coherent integration of perspectives, not their elimination. *VII.R14 — Intersubjectivity Conditions*: successful intersubjective gluing requires shared register, comparable cover granularity, and genuine overlapping contexts.
