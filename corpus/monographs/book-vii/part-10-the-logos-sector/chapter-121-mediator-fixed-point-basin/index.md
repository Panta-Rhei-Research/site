If Reg_D and Reg_C coincide at S_L, do the other two registers — the empirical (Reg_E) and the practical (Reg_P) — also converge there? The answer is yes, and the mechanism is the joint conservativity of the four sector functors on the coherence kernel: a content with D–C coincident readouts has a fibre in K_τ that is a singleton up to isomorphism, and that forces the E- and P-readouts to agree with the D–C readout by the same conservativity condition. The result is striking: at S_L, all four registers — "What do I observe?", "What should I do?", "What can I prove?", "What am I willing to live as true?" — receive the same answer. This is convergence, not collapse: the registers retain their independent definitions and failure modes everywhere else; what converges is the answer, not the question. Chapter 121 also defines the mediator fixed-point basin formally and shows that S_L is its own basin of attraction, then traces the master constant ι_τ = 2/(π + e) through all five register-factorizations to explain its ubiquity across the series.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** *VII.D88 — Mediator Fixed-Point Basin* (τ-effective). The register-crossing endofunctor Φ = B_{C→D} ∘ B_{P→C} ∘ B_{E→P} ∘ B_{D→E} ∘ B_{C→D} ∘ B_{D→C} is defined on K_τ, and the basin B(S_L) is the subcategory of contents converging to S_L under iteration of Φ. The result that B(S_L) = S_L — basin equals sector — follows from the uniqueness of the coincidence locus (VII.T45) and the fact that convergence in the finite-sector covering is achieved in a single Φ-cycle.
- **Key results:** *VII.P29 — Four-Register Convergence at S_L*: for any φ ∈ S_L, Reg_E(φ) ≅ Reg_P(φ) ≅ Reg_D(φ) ≅ Reg_C(φ) naturally in φ. The five-register factorization of ι_τ (D, C, E, P, plus the organizing role across seven books) shows that the master constant's ubiquity is a structural shadow of its membership in S_L, not a coincidence.
- **Dependencies:** Bridge functor and equivalence (VII.D87, VII.T46, Chapter 120); four-register model with joint conservativity (Part I); mediator-sector template from Book III Part II.
