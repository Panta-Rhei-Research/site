The Logos sector is not merely the set-theoretic overlap of two registers — it is the unique location in the 4+1 decomposition where all four readout functors converge. Chapter 121 proves that the D–C coincidence defining S_L forces *Reg_E* and *Reg_P* to agree there as well (Four-Register Convergence, VII.P29), using the joint conservativity of the four register functors on the coherence kernel 𝒦_τ. The chapter then introduces the register-crossing endofunctor Φ — the composition of all six pairwise bridge functors — and shows that S_L is its unique fixed-point basin: B(S_L) = S_L. Contents that can survive iterated translation across all four registers without information loss are precisely those already in S_L; the basin is exactly the attractor. Finally, the master constant ι_τ = 2/(π + e) factors through S_L in all four registers simultaneously, explaining its structural ubiquity across seven books, and the chapter connects the E₃ instantiation to the abstract mediator-sector theorem of Book III.
