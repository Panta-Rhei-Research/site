Part X followed a five-stage arc: definition and uniqueness (Chapter 119), internal machinery — bridge functor and mediator basin (Chapters 120–121), external resonance with the historical logos traditions (Chapter 122), internal boundary — the ω-germ question and subject–tool collapse (Chapter 123). Chapter 124 closes the arc by taking inventory, naming what the six chapters have collectively achieved, and positioning the reader for what remains. The six results span three scope levels: four τ-effective structural deliverables (the Logos Sector definition and uniqueness, the D→C bridge apparatus, the mediator fixed-point basin, the subject–tool collapse and No Forced Stance theorem), one conjectural remark (the Logos Synchronicity with the historical logos traditions), and one synthesis remark that is itself τ-effective. The crown-jewel designation is structural, not honorific: S_L is the terminal object in the category of sectors with the coincidence property, and it is where the series' deepest questions — the relationship between proof and commitment, the ω-germ boundary, the relationship between mathematics and lived reality — naturally reside. The 2nd Edition's distinctive structural contribution is precisely this: not additional content within the existing sectors, but a new structural location between sectors where the relationship between mathematical proof and existential commitment can be examined with the same precision the series applies everywhere else.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** none introduced; this chapter synthesizes results established in Chapters 119–123.
- **Key results:** *VII.R46 — Crown Jewel of the 2nd Edition* (τ-effective). The Logos sector S_L is identified as the structural contribution that distinguishes the 2nd Edition: the 1st Edition developed the τ-framework across seven books without identifying the unique sector where proof and commitment become inseparable. The crown jewel designation reflects S_L's role as the terminal object in the sector category and as the natural habitat of the framework's limitative result.
- **Dependencies:** All six Part X results: VII.D86, VII.T45 (Chapter 119); VII.D87, VII.T46, VII.L16 (Chapter 120); VII.D88, VII.P29 (Chapter 121); VII.R45 (Chapter 122); VII.D89, VII.T47 (Chapter 123).
