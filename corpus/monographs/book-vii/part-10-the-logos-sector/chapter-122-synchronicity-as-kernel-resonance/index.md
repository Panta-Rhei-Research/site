Yet Heraclitus, the Stoics, Philo of Alexandria, and the Johannine Prologue all converge on structurally similar territory: a rational principle that is simultaneously structural law and lived practice, reason-that-acts rather than reason-that-describes. Chapter 122 applies the synchronicity framework (kernel-invariant correlation across independent readout functors) to this resonance. The structural concept of synchronicity is τ-effective; the specific claim that the historical *logos* traditions are independent readout functors of the same kernel invariant that defines S_L is interpretive and carries the conjectural scope label. The chapter claims nothing theological, explains nothing historical, makes no priority claims, and does not invoke hidden causation. It notes a correlation and diagnoses its structural source with the honesty the framework requires.
