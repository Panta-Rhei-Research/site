Religion cuts across all nine sphere levels: intimate dyads, local communities, civilisational zones, and the species as a whole can all be structured by religious practice. This closing chapter of Part VIII treats religion not primarily as a set of propositions about supernatural entities but as the social infrastructure through which communities construct collective access to the commitment register Reg_C. The argument proceeds in four stages. First, Durkheim's sacred-profane distinction is reformulated as a boundary invariant: the sacred is the section of the social sheaf that remains fixed under all profane transformations — the automorphisms of everyday social life — and its invariance is a structural feature of collective life, not a supernatural one. Eliade's hierophany is then a controlled crossing of that boundary, managed by what the chapter formalises in its second stage: ritual as a social gluing protocol (*VII.D81 — τ-effective*). A ritual is a gluing protocol with four components — stereotypy (fixed patterns ensuring local sections are compatible on overlaps), synchrony (temporal coordination creating the overlap conditions), collective scope (individual local sections extending to a glued group section), and transformation (the glued section carrying collective properties absent from any single local section). Durkheim's collective effervescence is the phenomenological correlate of successful gluing. Third, the four structural mechanisms of collective Reg_C-access are analysed: shared narrative, moral code, ritual calendar, and community boundary. Fourth, the Religion as Readout Functor remark (*VII.R40 — conjectural*) addresses pluralism: different traditions are different readout functors R_i: ker(τ) → 𝒞_i mapping shared structural invariants into tradition-specific symbolic carriers. Faithfulness has structural criteria — dignity preservation, coherence, and boundary structure — making religious pluralism neither relativism nor exclusivism but functorial pluralism.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** *VII.D81 — Ritual as Social Gluing* (τ-effective). A ritual is a social gluing protocol with stereotypy, synchrony, collective scope, and transformation; ritual failure is gluing failure.
- **Key results:** *VII.R40 — Religion as Readout Functor* (conjectural): different religious traditions are readout functors R_i: ker(τ) → 𝒞_i; shared invariants (dignity, coherence, boundary structure) are the kernel content; doctrinal differences are carrier differences; faithfulness has structural criteria.
- **Dependencies:** *VII.D76* (social ontology), commitment register Reg_C, four-register framework, from Chapters 94–104 and Part VII.
