Sloterdijk's *Sphären* trilogy makes the observation that scale matters: the coherence available within an intimate dyad differs structurally from what a nation can sustain, and both differ from the fractured plurality of modern foam-like life. This chapter makes that observation precise. Starting from the social base space Ω_soc equipped with the recognition-generated Grothendieck topology of Chapter 94, it defines a sphere as a triple consisting of an open set, a recognition-density function, and an internal topos of social facts restricted to that open set. Three qualitatively distinct scales follow directly — bubbles (small carrier set, high density), globes (larger set with institutionally mediated density), and foams (many partially overlapping spheres without a single coherent global section). Atmosphere — the pre-symbolic sense of dwelling that a sphere affords — is reformulated as the phenomenological correlate of how smoothly local sections glue within a sphere: when gluing succeeds, atmosphere is stable; when sections fail to cohere at overlaps, atmosphere degrades into vigilance and friction. The Sloterdijk–τ correspondence (*VII.R35*) draws a structural parallel between this three-level hierarchy and the τ³ fibration, noting that modernity's shift from globe to foam corresponds categorically to increasing obstruction of global sections in high-dimensional social topologies.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** *VII.D77 — Sphere as Shared Topos* (τ-effective). A sphere is a triple (U, ρ, ε_U) where U is a recognition-generated open set, ρ is a recognition-density function on U, and ε_U is the internal topos of sheaves on the restricted site.
- **Key results:** *VII.R35 — Sloterdijk–τ Correspondence*: the bubble–globe–foam hierarchy is structurally parallel to the τ³ = τ¹ ×_f T² fibration, with the fiber T² carrying local structure and the lemniscate boundary governing the local/global transition.
- **Dependencies:** *VII.D76* (social ontology, recognition topology) from Chapter 94.
