Political philosophy asks how collective order emerges from
multiplicity. This chapter develops the categorical account: power
as morphism structure in the social category, sovereignty as boundary
control, and legitimacy as recognition coherence. The Power as
Morphism Structure (VII.D80) formalizes political asymmetry, and the
Legitimacy as Recognition Coherence proposition (VII.P25) derives
the structural conditions under which authority is legitimate.
Justice emerges from the categorical imperative, the dignity
constraint, and the fairness protocols of earlier chapters.
