Chapters 62–66 developed the five modes mode by mode. This chapter assembles the results into a unified account: the master trade-off. It proves that the fork is not optional, shows that the 69 classified constructions cluster into five thematic patterns, extends the comparison to the physical world, and establishes that the gain–cost structure cannot be cherry-picked.

## What this chapter contributes

The deepest single consequence of the fork is the **Infinity Trade-Off** (*established*, *II.D69*): the two paths are mutually exclusive. Property U — unique global infinity, a single canonical ω with ρ(ω) = ω, spectral access via ω-germs, no cardinality hierarchy — is the τ path. Property A — Archimedean local density, between any two distinct points uncountably many others, ε-δ limits providing infinite refinement at every point — is the orthodox path. The **Structural Incompatibility theorem** (*established*, *II.T43*) proves they cannot coexist in any single framework: accepting U requires K5 to block the diagonal argument, while accepting A requires the diagonal argument to construct uncountable sets. This is not a limitation of current proof techniques; it is structural. You must choose one.

The **Combined Gain–Cost Ledger** (*established*, *II.D93*) tallies the full audit: 69 classified constructions across Books I–II, comprising 17 same or parallel (Modes A+B), 16 refused (Mode C), 23 gained (Mode D), and 13 earned (Mode E), for 36 total gains against 16 costs. Including Mode A/B constructions with additional τ-specific structure, the gain count rises to approximately 49. The 3:1 ratio is not evidence that τ is better; it reflects the structural fact that categoricity, though expensive in refusals, generates many downstream consequences because each gain is a theorem that follows from categoricity and the forced sign.

Five **thematic patterns** organise the 69 constructions: the Earned/Imposed Duality (every major structure appears twice — once axiomatized, once derived); the Constructivity Upgrade (K6 forces countability, which forces finite witnesses, which forces constructivity at every stage); the Inversion Cascade (the entire classical dependency order reversed, each arrow a proved theorem); Sign Determines Everything (the master switch traces every difference to j² = +1, itself forced by prime polarity *I.T05* → *I.T10*); and the Protection Architecture (Mode C refusals are not random losses but coherent barriers guarding Mode D gains).

The fork extends to physics. The **Physics Quadrant Matrix** (*established*, *II.D73*) classifies frameworks by PDE type (elliptic/hyperbolic) and metric type (Archimedean/non-Archimedean): QFT occupies the Hyperbolic-Archimedean quadrant, GR the Elliptic-Archimedean quadrant, and Category τ the Hyperbolic-Non-Archimedean (fourth) quadrant. The **Unification Obstruction** (*established*, *II.D74*) states that no Archimedean framework can simultaneously support hyperbolic propagation and elliptic regularity — all major unification programs remain in the Archimedean column and face this obstruction. The **Fourth Quadrant Resolution theorem** (*established*, *II.T46*) proves that in the fourth quadrant the obstruction dissolves: wave characteristics exist, Archimedean infinities are absent, stage-finite Euclidean geometry replaces curvature singularities, and the bipolar decomposition supplies quantum phases.

Finally, the **Trade-Off Atomicity theorem** (*established*, *II.T56*) proves the ledger is a package deal: accepting K0–K6 and j² = +1 simultaneously forces all 16 Mode C costs and simultaneously enables all 23 Mode D gains and all 13 Mode E earned results. Each cost traces to K5, K6, or j² = +1; each gain traces through categoricity (*I.T08*) or the forced sign (*I.T10*). No partial selection is structurally possible.
