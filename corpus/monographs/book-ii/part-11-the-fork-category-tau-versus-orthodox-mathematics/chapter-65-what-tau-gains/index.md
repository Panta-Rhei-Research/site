Chapter 64 recorded what τ structurally refuses — the sixteen Mode C constructions that its axioms block. This chapter catalogues the opposite: what τ structurally gains. Mode D objects have no orthodox counterpart. They are not improved versions of existing theorems; they are new mathematical facts that arise precisely because τ's axioms differ from ZFC and PA. The 23 gains across Books I–II divide into three categories of impossibility: metamathematical (blocked by the Löwenheim–Skolem theorem or incompleteness), structural (dependent on j² = +1, bipolar idempotents, or the primorial tower), and logical (reversals of orthodox theorems or axioms).

## What this chapter contributes

The **Mode D Catalog** (*established*, *II.D82*) enumerates all 23 gains with their source theorems and the specific reason each is impossible in ZFC/PA. Five gains are highlighted as structurally deepest. **Categoricity** (*established*, *I.T08*) — exactly one model up to isomorphism — is the strongest property a foundational system can possess, and the Löwenheim–Skolem theorem proves it is impossible for any first-order theory with infinite models. K6 achieves it by sealing the universe, making the axiom system effectively second-order. The **Central Theorem** (*established*, *II.T40*) O(τ³) ≅ A_spec(𝕃) asserts exact holographic duality between the holomorphic function algebra and the spectral character algebra on the boundary; no orthodox analogue exists, and the closest approximation — AdS/CFT — is a conjecture, not a theorem. The **Liouville Dodge** (*established*, *II.T41*) circumvents Liouville's theorem not by weakening hypotheses but by changing PDE type: standing waves provide non-constant bounded holomorphic functions because the wave equation has no maximum principle. **Safe self-enrichment** (*established*, Book II Part VIII) avoids the Russell-type obstacles that require Grothendieck universes in orthodox category theory, because K5 blocks the self-referential constructions that generate paradoxes.

The extended case study is gain 17: **the Parallel Postulate proved** (*established*, *II.T15–II.T18*). A hyperbolic substrate (j² = +1, wave equation) should produce Minkowski-like geometry with light cones and destroyed parallels. Instead τ-geometry is internally Euclidean, with all ten Tarski axioms satisfied. The resolution is **Stage-Finite Euclidean Geometry** (*established*, *II.D71*): at each primorial depth k, the stage-k space τ³_k is a finite set of points; the wave equation governs inter-stage propagation (boundary data into the interior), but within each stage the geometry is flat, discrete, and Euclidean. **Parallel Preservation** (*established*, *II.T45*) proves the Postulate holds at every stage and is preserved in the profinite limit. The Minkowski comparison (*established*, *II.R26*) makes the contrast precise: light cones require continuous characteristics in a manifold; τ³_k is discrete and has no continuous curves.

All 23 gains carry the scope label *established*: each is proved from K-axioms and backed by registered definitions and theorems. No Mode D claim in this chapter is conjectural.
