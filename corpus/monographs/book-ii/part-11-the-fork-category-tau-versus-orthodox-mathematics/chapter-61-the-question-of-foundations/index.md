Fifty-nine chapters of Books I and II built a complete mathematical framework from seven axioms on five generators, culminating in the Central Theorem O(τ³) ≅ A_spec(𝕃). Part XI turns from construction to comparison: in what precise sense does Category τ differ from orthodox mathematics built on ZFC and PA, and what are the structural consequences of those differences? This chapter opens the audit by establishing the vocabulary that will organize the seven chapters that follow.

## What this chapter contributes

The chapter's primary contribution is a two-part classification apparatus. The **Five Comparison Modes** (*established*, *II.D89*) assign every τ-construction one of five labels: Mode A (Same), Mode B (Parallel), Mode C (Refused), Mode D (Gained), or Mode E (Earned). The modes are exhaustive and mutually exclusive — every construction in Books I–II falls into exactly one. The **Six Comparison Axes** (*established*, *II.D90*) then evaluate each construction along six dimensions: Ontic Status, Axiom Dependency, Constructivity, Uniqueness, Scope Gain, and Scope Cost. Axes 1–4 yield factual answers; Axes 5–6 compile the evaluative ledger.

Before deploying that apparatus, the chapter establishes why it is needed. Three properties of τ have no ZFC counterpart and cannot be imported into it: categoricity (*I.T08*), which the Löwenheim–Skolem theorem makes impossible for any first-order theory with infinite models; unique infinity (*K2*), which stands against ZFC's unbounded hierarchy of cardinals ℵ₀, ℵ₁, ℵ₂, …; and the forced sign j² = +1 (*I.T10*), which produces an incompatible function theory. These are not matters of convention. They establish that τ and orthodox mathematics are not variants of a shared framework but genuinely different arenas — each able to see structures the other cannot.

A mode-distribution table summarises the full survey before Part XI begins: across Books I–II, 36 constructions are gained or earned (Modes D+E), 17 are same or parallel (Modes A+B), and 16 are refused (Mode C). The 16 costs are real — this accounting is honest about them — and they will be treated with the same precision as the gains.

## Where this leads

Chapter 62 (The Master Switch) identifies the single algebraic sign j² = +1 as the structural source of every difference the audit will document. Chapters 63–66 then apply the five modes in sequence: what survives (Modes A and B), what τ refuses (Mode C), what τ gains (Mode D), and what τ earns (Mode E). Chapter 67 (The Master Trade-Off) assembles the full gain–cost ledger and proves the structural incompatibility theorem (*II.T43*). Chapter 68 asks whether the fork is worth it. The comparison vocabulary introduced here — five modes, six axes, four scope labels (established / τ-effective / conjectural / metaphorical) — is the shared language for all seven chapters.
