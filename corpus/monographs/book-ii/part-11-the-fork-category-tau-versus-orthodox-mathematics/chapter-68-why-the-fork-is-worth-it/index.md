the relevant chapter assembled the master trade-off:
49 gains against 16 costs,
structurally inseparable,
with the incompatibility theorem
proving that the fork is not optional.

This chapter asks the final question of Books I–II:
**is the fork worth it?**

The answer depends on purpose.
For pure mathematics as currently practiced,
orthodox foundations are expressively richer:
uncountable sets, ε-δ analysis,
smooth structures, conformal geometry.
For the purpose of describing physical reality,
τ foundations are structurally superior:
narrower but uniquely determined,
with no free parameters
and no room for ambiguity.

The chapter previews the structural guarantees
that the fork provides for Books III–V
and closes the narrative arc of Books I–II.
