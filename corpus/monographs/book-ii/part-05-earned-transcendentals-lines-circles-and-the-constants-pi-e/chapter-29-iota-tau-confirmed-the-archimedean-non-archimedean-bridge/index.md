Book I introduced the master constant ι_τ = 2/(π + e) ≈ 0.341304 as a structural mediator between the B and C channels (I.D24, Book I), but at that stage neither π nor e was earned — the formula was a structural placeholder awaiting numerical content. Chapters 26 and 27 earned π and e from τ-internal data, and Chapter 28 established the bipolar unit j. This chapter closes the Part V calibration arc. *II.T25* (*ι_τ Geometric Confirmation*) supplies: (i) the numerical value ι_τ = 2/(5.85987…) = 0.341304…; (ii) the harmonic-mean interpretation — ι_τ is the half of the harmonic mean of 2/π and 2/e, coupling the solenoidal period and the growth eigenvalue symmetrically; (iii) the structural derivation — ι_τ couples the angular calibration (π, B/C channels) with the radial growth calibration (e, D channel), no external import needed; (iv) probable transcendence over ℚ, which would follow from the conjectural algebraic independence of π and e. The *Archimedean-Non-Archimedean Bridge* (*II.D34*) formalizes ι_τ's conversion role: ultrametric depth δ (measuring D-depth of CRT agreement) translates to Euclidean precision approximately ι_τ · 2^{−δ}; the Euclidean bound 4/P_k tightens super-exponentially relative to the ultrametric bound 2^{−k} because log₂ P_k ~ k ln k / ln 2 by the prime number theorem. *II.R09* (*Refinement = Resolution*) and *II.P06* (*Refinement-Resolution Duality*) make this precise: the same primorial stage k that represents one unit of ultrametric refinement delivers approximately k ln k bits of Euclidean precision. The chapter closes with the **calibration dictionary** for Part V: π (angular, B/C), e (growth, D), j (polarity, B/C swap), ι_τ (bridge coupling). All four constants arise from tower-computable rational sequences; ℝ is invoked only as the Archimedean target, not as an axiom. This dictionary is the load-bearing engine for holomorphic analysis in Parts VI–IX.
