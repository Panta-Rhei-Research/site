Book I earned the split-complex algebra H_τ, the bipolar idempotents *e_± = (1 ± j)/2*, the spectral characters *χ_±*, and the master constant *ι_τ = 2/(π + e)*. All of these are available. None of them have been used. Parts I and II of Book II operate entirely on a structural-algebraic and combinatorial-topological foundation: ABCD coordinates (*I.D17*), CRT reduction maps (*I.T18*), profinite completion, ω-germ naturality (*I.D47*), the cylinder basis (*II.T04*), and the ultrametric (*II.D13*). The split-complex codomain H_τ has not entered a single definition, lemma, or proof across nine chapters. This chapter pauses to record that fact explicitly and to explain why it is architecturally necessary, not an oversight. The restraint is the inverted dependency chain in action: holomorphy and continuity come before the codomain algebra, because the codomain algebra requires transcendental calibration that has not yet been earned.

## What this chapter contributes

Remark *II.R05* (Topological Skeleton): the formal registry observation that Parts I–II build a purely combinatorial-topological foundation — the skeleton on which split-complex holomorphic structure will later be erected. The chapter also provides an explicit inventory of the five Book I results held in reserve: the split-complex unit j with j² = +1 (*I.D20*, *I.T10*), bipolar idempotents *e_±* (*I.D21*), split-complex codomain H_τ (*I.D20*), spectral characters *χ_±* (*I.D22*, *I.D23*), and master constant *ι_τ* (*I.D24*). Each is available; each is deliberately deferred. The reason: the master constant *ι_τ = 2/(π + e)* requires π and e as numerical values, which are not earned until Part V. Without that calibration, the bipolar channel decomposition cannot distinguish the two channels quantitatively, so H_τ cannot function as intended.

## Lean coverage

Commentary in existing modules; no new formalization needed. This chapter contains no theorems requiring proof.

## Where this leads

The five deferred items become load-bearing in Part VI: the BndLift_n construction for boundary-to-interior lifting requires the bipolar idempotent decomposition f = e_+ · f_+ + e_- · f_-, and without the idempotent split the two channels cannot be lifted independently. The ordering — topology (Parts I–III), geometry (Part IV), transcendentals (Part V), then split-complex deployment (Part VI) — is forced by the dependency structure. Part III (Chapters 13–18) still uses none of H_τ; Part IV adds geometry from the ultrametric alone; only Part V, which earns π and e as computable τ-native limits, provides the numerical content that makes ι_τ = 2/(π + e) a genuine calibration constant rather than a formal symbol. Chapter 12 is the explicit marker of that forced waiting, ensuring that readers arriving at Part VI understand that H_τ's deployment is not delayed for stylistic reasons but because its prerequisites were genuinely not available earlier.
