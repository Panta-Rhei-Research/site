Where the electromagnetic and weak sectors occupy the χ₊-dominant and balanced regions of the E₁ spectral page, the C-sector — the strong force — occupies a structurally different position: it is χ₋-dominant, driven by the η-generator, and its vacuum is topologically non-trivial. We derive the C-sector from first principles, showing that SU(3) color is not postulated but follows from the three-fold branch structure of the fiber T², that the three color charges {R, G, B} are branch indices, and that the eight gluons are the SU(3) connection on the τ³ fiber bundle. The vacuum self-coupling κ(C;3) = ιτ³/(1 − ιτ) emerges as a rational function of ιτ — consistent with the No Knobs Theorem (III.T42), which forbids free parameters — and the (1 − ιτ) denominator is identified as the temporal complement, prefiguring the confinement mechanism developed in Chapter 30. Gluon self-interaction, arising from three- and four-point vertices in the non-abelian field strength, is a direct consequence of SU(3)'s non-commutativity; the absence of a color-singlet gluon is topologically necessary.
