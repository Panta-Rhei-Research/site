The cosmological constant problem — the 10¹²⁰ discrepancy between the QFT vacuum energy prediction ρ_vac^{QFT} ∼ M_Pl⁴ ≈ 10⁷³ GeV⁴ and the observed ρ_vac^{obs} ≈ 10⁻⁴⁷ GeV⁴ — is often called the worst prediction in physics. In Category τ, there is no catastrophe because vacuum energy is not computed by summing independent mode contributions. The vacuum is the minimum of the defect functional Δ(f, n) evaluated at the vacuum mode f₀; its energy is a single geometric quantity determined by the boundary topology of 𝕃, and the compact T² fiber guarantees discreteness and the absence of UV divergence. The No Knobs Theorem (III.T42) prevents any free parameter from tuning the result, and sector preservation (III.T05) prevents the inter-sector leakage that mode summation implicitly assumes. QFT mode summation is a valid readout approximation for scattering processes — locally, T² looks flat, and independent-mode approximation works — but it is not a valid computation of global vacuum energy. The 10¹²⁰ discrepancy is a category error: comparing a readout quantity (QFT mode sum) with a structural quantity (τ³ defect minimum) as if they computed the same thing. The precise numerical value of ρ_vac in terms of ιτ awaits the D-sector treatment of Book V; the structural finiteness result is complete here.
