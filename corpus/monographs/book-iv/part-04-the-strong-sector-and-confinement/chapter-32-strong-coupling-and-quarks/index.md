The strong coupling constant αₛ is the largest gauge coupling of the Standard Model, and its magnitude at the Z-boson mass — αₛ(M_Z) ≈ 0.118 — is conventionally obtained by running from high-energy measurements via the QCD beta function. In Category τ, no running is involved: κ(C;3) = ιτ³/(1 − ιτ) is a boundary fixed-point invariant, a rational function of ιτ = 2/(π + e) fixed once by the No Knobs Theorem (III.T42). Numerically, 2κ(C;3) ≈ 0.1208 matches the PDG 2024 value of αₛ(M_Z) = 0.1180 ± 0.0009 to 2.4%, a deviation consistent with the expected readout correction at that scale. The chapter then turns to the quark content of the C-sector: quarks are confined character modes on T², analogous to phonons in a crystal, with fractional charges {+2/3, −1/3} arising from the three-fold address structure of the η-holonomy covering. Six flavors in three generations follow from π₁(τ³), nucleon mass is 99% T² mode energy rather than quark rest mass, gluon exchange is holonomy transport, and asymptotic freedom is reinterpreted as local flatness of T² at short distances — not a changing coupling but a property of the readout geometry.
