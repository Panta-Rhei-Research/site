Before the neutron can decay, we need the carrier mode through which electromagnetic information will travel. The photon is introduced here not as a "particle of light" but as the **null transport mode** for U(1) phase on τ³: it carries zero T² breathing amplitude, which is exactly why its mass is zero — a theorem, not a postulate. Because mass in Category τ is resistance to the solenoidal expansion of T², any mode with vanishing breathing amplitude has vanishing mass and propagates on null geodesics of τ¹ at speed c, the structurally determined maximum. The chapter derives the photon's masslessness, its null propagation speed, its spin-1 character (two polarization states from the two transverse modes of T², with no longitudinal mode because m_γ = 0), and its identity as the gauge boson of the B-sector (EM) carrying U(1) holonomy between charged defect configurations. The classical electromagnetic field is shown to be an emergent, many-photon effective description rather than an ontologically primitive entity.

## What this chapter contributes
