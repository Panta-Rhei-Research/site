β⁻ decay displayed all five sectors in a single process; this chapter systematizes the observation. The four sectors plus one crossing — D (gravity, generator α), A (weak, generator π), B (EM, generator γ), C (strong, generator η), ω (Higgs, crossing γ ∩ η) — are derived from the lemniscate structure 𝕃 = S¹ ∨ S¹ at the boundary of τ³, and their gauge groups SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1) are determined by the topology of T² and the character decomposition on 𝕃. The chapter's central result is that all leading-order sector couplings are rational functions of a single constant ι_τ = 2/(π + e) ≈ 0.341304: κ(D; 1) = 1 − ι_τ, κ(A; 1) = ι_τ, κ(B) ∼ ι_τ², κ(C; 3) = ι_τ³/(1 − ι_τ). These exponents reflect the number of τ³ circles involved — zero, one, two, three — and give a structural account of the hierarchy of force strengths. The sectors are not independent forces; they are aspects of one E₁ template, always unified, only appearing independent as an artefact of projection onto the microcosm or macrocosm. The temporal complement κ(A; 1) + κ(D; 1) = 1 reflects the shared τ¹ base direction of gravity and the weak force.
