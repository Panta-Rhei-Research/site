Every introductory physics course begins with protons, neutrons, and electrons as co-primitive building blocks. Category τ reverses this order: only the neutron is primitive, and this chapter makes that derivation explicit. The **proton** is defined as the residual bulk mode of the neutron after β⁻ decay — p := n − χ_e − χ_ν̄ — retaining most of the T² tension and acquiring charge Q_p = +e by conservation alone. The **electron** is the co-rotor surface mode that emerges when the neutron's T² saturation breaks: lighter by a factor of ~1839 because surface breathing amplitude is geometrically smaller than bulk breathing amplitude, and carrying charge Q_e = −e by the same conservation argument. The **antineutrino** is the τ¹ longitudinal mode expelled at the Diophantine resonance, carrying away the time-phase deficit with right-handed helicity forced by the τ¹ arrow of time. The chapter's culminating theorem is the ontic sequence: vacuum → neutron →^{β⁻} proton + electron + antineutrino — a derivation from one primitive, not an assembly of three. Two Book III results underlie the derived particles: the mass-gap guarantee (III.T27) confines quarks inside the proton as the lowest-energy charged mode, and NF-discreteness (III.T28) ensures the electron's quantum numbers are finitely addressable.
