With the τ³ arena set and the photon established as the null carrier, the question becomes: what is the first massive excitation above the vacuum? This chapter answers that the **neutron** emerges as the unique minimal stable ontic particle — not postulated, but derived as the minimizer of the holomorphic tension functional on T². The neutron is a bi-rotating configuration in which both the poloidal and toroidal angular velocities are locked at speed c with a phase ratio ω_A/ω_B ∼ ι_τ = 2/(π + e); because ι_τ is transcendental the path is pseudo-dense on T², giving the neutron its featureless solid-ball appearance in 3D bulk. Three structural facts explain its primacy: charge neutrality (zero net U(1) holonomy), lowest baryonic energy (guaranteed by the mass-gap result of Book III, III.T27), and self-containment (no partner charge required). The chapter also derives the bi-rotation energy identity E = mc², establishes existence and uniqueness of the stable mode, and introduces the proton–neutron mass splitting as a two-sector formula in ι_τ accurate to 33 ppm.
