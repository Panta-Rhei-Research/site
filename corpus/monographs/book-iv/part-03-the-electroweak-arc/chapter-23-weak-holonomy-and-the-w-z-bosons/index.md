In the τ³ framework, the W⁺ and W⁻ are not particles traveling through space — they are the junction-crossing process itself: intertwiners mapping modes between the two lobes L₊ and L₋ of 𝕃, charged (Q = ±e) because lobe-crossing changes the U(1) holonomy. The Z⁰ is the complementary junction oscillation without lobe-crossing: a neutral mode that touches ω and returns, coupled to both SU(2) and U(1) channels and therefore heavier than the W. The W mass M_W = gv/2 ≈ 80.4 GeV and Z mass M_Z = gv/(2cosθ_W) ≈ 91.2 GeV arise from the characteristic tension of the ω-crossing — the junction energy scale v set by τ³ geometry — so no Mexican-hat potential is required. The mass-gap guarantee (Book III, III.T27) ensures that the junction mode energy has a spectral floor, making these masses well-defined.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** *IV.D41 — W Boson* (τ-effective). W± := intertwiner mode on 𝕃 junction; a morphism connecting modes on different lobes, mapping L₊ ↔ L₋. *IV.D42 — Z Boson*. Z⁰ := neutral oscillation mode at the 𝕃 junction; probes ω without crossing. *IV.D43 — Physical Electroweak Bosons*. Four gauge bosons: W⁺, W⁻ (mass 80.4 GeV), Z⁰ (mass 91.2 GeV), γ (massless).
- **Key results:** *IV.T13 — W Mass from Junction Tension*: M_W = gv/2 ≈ 80.4 GeV from holomorphic junction tension. *IV.T14 — W Charge from Lobe Crossing*: Q(W±) = ±e because crossing changes U(1) holonomy. *IV.T15 — W/Z Mass from ω-Crossing* (τ-effective): M_W = gv/2 (charged crossing), M_Z = gv/(2cosθ_W) (neutral oscillation), photon massless because it does not couple to the junction mode. Geometric projection, not symmetry breaking. *IV.T15b — Electroweak Boson Mixing*: physical eigenstates W±, Z, γ as linear combinations of SU(2) and U(1) gauge fields via Weinberg angle θ_W. Real/virtual W distinction is energetic (E ≷ M_W), not ontological.
- **Notation introduced:** v ≈ 246 GeV (electroweak junction scale); g ≈ 0.65 (SU(2) coupling); θ_W (Weinberg angle, derived in Chapter 25).
