The weak force operates at the singular junction point ω of 𝕃 = S¹ ∨ S¹, and this geometric fact explains three otherwise-baffling features. Parity violation: the junction carries an intrinsic orientation from the CR-structure of τ³, so only left-handed modes can pass through — a geometric necessity, as the Parity Bridge Theorem (Book III, III.T07) guarantees only the A-sector bootstraps the E₁ → E₂ transition. SU(2) gauge structure: SU(2)_L = Aut(𝕃, ω), with doublets (u, d) and (ν_e, e⁻) on opposite lobes. Relative weakness: the junction is geometrically narrow, producing effective low-energy coupling G_F = √2 g²/(8M_W²) ≈ 1.17 × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻². The A-sector intrinsic coupling at rank 1 is κ(A;1) = ι_τ ≈ 0.341304.
