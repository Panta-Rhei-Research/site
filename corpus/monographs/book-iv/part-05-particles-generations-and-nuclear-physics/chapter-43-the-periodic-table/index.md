Mendeleev organised the elements by atomic weight and chemical properties; the modern table organises by electron configuration. The τ-periodic table goes deeper: it organises by T² saturation level of both nucleus and electron cloud simultaneously, revealing the periodic table not as an empirical catalogue but as a structural consequence of T² quantisation on τ³ = τ¹ ×_f T². The Aufbau principle—the 1s, 2s, 2p, 3s, … orbital filling order—is not an empirical recipe but a theorem about T² breathing mode energies, with the Madelung (n+l) rule emerging from the T² energy landscape. Each shell holds 2n² electrons from spin × orbital degeneracy, reflecting T² mode multiplicity. Noble gases occur at electron magic numbers 2, 10, 18, 36, 54, 86 where outer subshells close; these differ from the nuclear magic numbers because atomic spin-orbit coupling is weak relative to the strong nuclear case. Helium is unique in being doubly magic by both nuclear and atomic criteria. The τ-table adds three further dimensions to the standard layout: neutron number N (isotope), stability (half-life), nucleosynthesis origin, and cosmic abundance—turning it into a map of cosmic chemical history. The island of stability near Z = 114, N = 184 is predicted as the next doubly-magic T² shell closure.
