From helium-4 to iron-56, two organising principles govern the nuclear landscape: magic numbers and the binding energy curve. Magic numbers (2, 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, 126) mark complete T² shell closures; they arise from nucleon filling of T² modes in the presence of strong spin-orbit coupling, which the τ³ framework identifies as a geometric fibre-twist interaction rather than an empirical add-on. The Mayer–Jensen level sequence derives from this coupling, producing the observed magic-number sequence with all five doubly-magic nuclei (⁴He, ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, ²⁰⁸Pb). The mass-5 and mass-8 gaps—no stable nuclei at A = 5 or A = 8—follow from the α-particle's complete first T² shell: adding one nucleon finds no stable orbit, and two α-particles lack the residual attraction to bind without the Hoyle resonance gateway. The binding energy curve peaks at ⁵⁶Fe (B/A = 8.790 MeV), the T² saturation optimum where surface-energy gain exactly balances Coulomb cost; below iron, fusion releases energy; above iron, fission does. Fe-56's 224 voxel-donut realisation and its 50% shell filling (explaining ferromagnetism) close the chapter.
