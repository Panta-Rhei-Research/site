Individual quarks never appear in isolation—the C-sector confinement principle forces them into colour-neutral composites whose properties can be classified and, in the τ³ framework, derived. This chapter treats the resulting bound states: mesons (q q̄ pairs, integer spin) and baryons (qqq triples, half-integer spin) as the two colour-neutral families consistent with C-sector confinement. The pion, the lightest q q̄ breathing mode on T², mediates the residual strong force via a Yukawa potential. Nuclear structure then follows: the Bethe–Weizsäcker semi-empirical mass formula receives a term-by-term τ³ interpretation—volume from T² saturation, surface from T² boundary energy, Coulomb from B-sector repulsion, asymmetry from Fermi statistics on T² modes, pairing from spin pairing on T². The binding energy curve peaks at ⁵⁶Fe (B/A ≈ 8.8 MeV), the point of maximum T² saturation efficiency; the valley of stability and the magic-number sequence 2, 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, 126 emerge from T² shell closures. The mass-5 and mass-8 gaps, and the primordial abundance pattern from Big Bang nucleosynthesis, complete the survey.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** *Hadron Classification* (τ-effective). Baryons (qqq, half-integer spin) and mesons (q q̄, integer spin) as the two classes of colour-neutral C-sector composites.
- **Definitions / Axioms:** *Nuclear Structure* (τ-effective). A nucleus with Z protons and N neutrons; radius R ≈ r₀ A^(1/3) with r₀ ≈ 1.2 fm; central density ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³ from T² saturation.
- **Definitions / Axioms:** *Binding Energy Per Nucleon* (τ-effective). B/A = [Zmₚ + Nmₙ − M(A,Z)]c²/A.
- **Definitions / Axioms:** *Valley of Stability*. Stable nuclides in the (N, Z) plane; β-stability line N/Z ≈ 1 + 0.015 A^(2/3).
- **Definitions / Axioms:** *Nuclear Drip Lines*. Boundaries at Sₙ = 0 (neutron) and Sₚ = 0 (proton); approximately 3 000 bound species, ~300 stable.
- **Key results:** *Semi-Empirical Mass Formula* (τ-effective): B(A,Z) = a_V A − a_S A^(2/3) − a_C Z²/A^(1/3) − a_A (N−Z)²/A + δ; each term given a T³ geometric interpretation.
- **Key results:** *Binding Energy Curve Shape* (τ-effective): rises sharply to He-4, continues to ⁵⁶Fe maximum at 8.8 MeV, slowly falls to uranium; below Fe fusion releases energy, above Fe fission does.
- **Key results:** *Magic Numbers* (τ-effective): 2, 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, 126 mark complete T² nuclear shell closures; doubly magic nuclei (⁴He, ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, ²⁰⁸Pb) are exceptionally stable.
- **Key results:** *Mass-5 and Mass-8 Gaps* (τ-effective): no stable A = 5 or A = 8 nuclei because the α-particle fills the first T² shell completely, leaving no stable orbit for an additional nucleon or a second α without the triple-α resonance.
- **Key results:** *BBN Abundances*: ~75% H, ~25% He by mass within the first 20 minutes; gaps prevent heavier element production.
- **Dependencies:** Book III III.T27 (Yang–Mills/Mass Gap) for confinement; Book III III.T28 (Hodge/Addressability) for discrete spectrum; Chapter 34 (Sector Atlas) for C-sector framework.
