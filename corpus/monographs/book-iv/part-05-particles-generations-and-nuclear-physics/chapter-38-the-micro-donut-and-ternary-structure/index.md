The neutron is the first "micro-donut"—a saturated T² configuration whose bi-rotating character path traces a pseudo-dense trajectory across the toroidal fiber. A character path on T² = S¹ × S¹ is a trajectory (Θ₂(t), Θ₃(t)) with both angular velocities at speed c. When the phase ratio ω_A/ω_B = ι_τ = 2/(π + e) is irrational—indeed transcendental—the path's closure equals the entire torus, giving pseudo-dense coverage and explaining why the neutron appears as a featureless solid ball in 3D projection. The bi-rotation energy identity E_bi-rot = ½mc² + ½mc² = mc² recovers mass-energy equivalence kinematically, with torus radii cancelling. Saturation—the condition that no additional excitation fits without restructuring—implies stability. The SU(3) three-fold covering of the C-sector then forces hadron compositeness: baryons hold three quark colour modes (one per branch of the η-covering), mesons hold two (colour–anticolour). Baryon number counts topological winding. The donut ladder extends this architecture across 25 orders of magnitude, from nucleons at 10⁻¹⁵ m to neutron stars and black holes at 10¹⁰ m.
