Beyond iron, fusion no longer releases energy. Heavy elements—gold, platinum, uranium, and all actinides—form instead by neutron capture, which faces no Coulomb barrier. Two processes operate on very different timescales: the s-process in AGB stars captures neutrons over months to years, staying close to the valley of stability and building strontium, barium, and lead; the r-process in core-collapse supernovae and neutron-star mergers captures many neutrons in milliseconds, building far from stability and then cascading back via β-decay to form gold and uranium. The gravitational-wave event GW170817 (August 2017) provided direct spectroscopic confirmation of r-process production in a neutron-star merger. Unstable nuclei created by both processes transform via three decay channels, each interpreted in τ³ as a restoration of T² saturation balance: α-decay ejects a saturated T² sub-unit; β-decay rearranges the T² mode flavour (nucleon species) while conserving A; γ-decay de-excites without transmutation. Abundance peaks at magic neutron numbers N = 50, 82, 126 in the cosmic abundance spectrum provide direct observational evidence for T² shell structure.
