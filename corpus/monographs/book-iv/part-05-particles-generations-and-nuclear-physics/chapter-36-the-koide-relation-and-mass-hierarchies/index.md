The three generations of fermions differ by mass—and the mass ratios are not random. The Koide formula, known since 1982 and unexplained in the Standard Model, states that (mₑ + mμ + mτ)/(√mₑ + √mμ + √mτ)² = 2/3 to five significant figures. This chapter derives that ratio as an exact algebraic theorem: any σ-equivariant 3×3 mass matrix of the form [[a, b, 0], [b, c, b], [0, b, a]] automatically satisfies Q = 2/3 for its eigenvalues. The σ-symmetry itself traces to the lemniscate 𝕃 = S¹ ∨ S¹, whose two-lobe structure constrains the democratic coupling. The lepton masses are then expressed as integer-power ι_τ scalings: m_μ/mₑ ≈ ι_τ⁻⁵ (with a NNLO correction of 1/W₃(4)² = 1/25), and mτ follows from the Koide constraint to 61 ppm. Quark mass hierarchies—m_t/m_u ≈ 79 000 for up-type, m_b/m_d ≈ 890 for down-type—and the complementarity between CKM and PMNS mixing angles round out the chapter.
