The α-particle—helium-4 nucleus—appears throughout nuclear physics as a radioactive decay product, a projectile in scattering experiments, a cluster within heavier nuclei, and a component of cosmic rays. Its ubiquity has a single underlying cause: it is the smallest nucleus with all four spin-isospin slots filled (2 protons ↑↓ and 2 neutrons ↑↓), making it the minimal doubly saturated T² unit with binding energy B/A = 7.07 MeV. No residual T² mode is available for further interaction, leaving it categorically inert. α-decay from heavy nuclei is quantum tunnelling through the Coulomb barrier—Gamow's 1928 exponential transmission formula—with the tunnelling itself interpreted as holomorphic continuation of the α mode through the barrier. α-clustering in nuclei such as ¹²C = 3α and ¹⁶O = 4α reflects nucleons' preference for saturated T² sub-units. The Hoyle resonance at 7.65 MeV in ¹²C, without which stellar carbon production would be negligible, is the T² mode gateway for triple-α fusion. Rutherford's 1911 scattering formula, derived from α-particle probes, confirmed the nuclear atom.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** *α-Particle Ubiquity Principle* (τ-effective). The α-particle's omnipresence in nuclear physics is equivalent to its doubly closed T² shells.
- **Definitions / Axioms:** *α-Decay* (established). Heavy nucleus sheds ⁴He: ᴬ_Z X → ᴬ⁻⁴_(Z−2)Y + ⁴He.
- **Definitions / Axioms:** *α-Clustering* (τ-effective). α-conjugate nuclei (A = 4n, Z = 2n) form cluster sub-structures corresponding to n saturated T² units.
- **Key results:** *α as Minimal Saturated T² Unit* (τ-effective): ⁴He is doubly magic (first T² shell closed for both protons and neutrons); no residual mode available for interaction; B/A = 7.07 MeV.
- **Key results:** *Gamow Tunnelling* (established): transmission T ≈ exp(−2πZ₁Z₂e²/ℏv) through Coulomb barrier; factor of 2 in decay energy produces 10²⁰ variation in half-life (Geiger–Nuttall law).
- **Key results:** *τ³ Clustering Principle* (τ-effective): nucleons prefer saturated T² sub-units; the α-particle is the natural quantum of nuclear matter.
- **Key results:** *Hoyle Resonance* (established): 3α → ¹²C* (7.65 MeV, J^P = 0⁺) → ¹²C + γ; without this resonance, stellar carbon production would be negligible.
- **Key results:** *Rutherford Scattering Formula* (established): dσ/dΩ = (Z₁Z₂e²/4E)² / sin⁴(θ/2); deviations reveal finite nuclear size.
