The Standard Model contains three generations of fermions—(e, νₑ, u, d), (μ, νμ, c, s), (τ, ντ, t, b)—but provides no explanation for why exactly three exist. In Category τ, three is a topological necessity: the fibration τ³ = τ¹ ×_f T² has fundamental group π₁(τ³) ≅ ℤ³ with exactly three independent generators, each defining a winding class that corresponds to one generation. The Laplacian spectrum of T² with radii ratio ι_τ = 2/(π + e) supports exactly three primitive modes below the composite-mode threshold at λ ≈ 12.58/R²; no fourth light generation fits within the framework. Mass hierarchies across generations scale as powers of ι_τ⁻² ≈ 8.6, with third-generation particles occupying the lemniscate crossing sector where both lobes of 𝕃 = S¹ ∨ S¹ contribute—explaining why the top quark is anomalously heavy. The chapter closes by deriving the CKM and PMNS matrices from generation overlap on T², and predicting Majorana neutrinos from the self-dual τ¹ base circle.
