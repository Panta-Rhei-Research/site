Standard quantum mechanics introduces measurement and time evolution as independent axioms; in τ³ both are consequences of one geometry. Measurement is character mode selection: a general state Ψ = Σ c_{m,n} χ_{m,n} on T² is projected onto a character subspace when a subsystem's modes become correlated with environmental degrees of freedom—orthogonal projection in H_τ, not mysterious collapse. The Born rule P(m₀, n₀) = |c_{m₀,n₀}|² then follows from the Pythagorean theorem: since the characters form an orthonormal basis and ‖Ψ‖ = 1, the squared coefficient is exactly the squared projection length, normalized by Parseval. Time evolution along the τ¹ worldline is generated by a Hamiltonian operator Ĥ = iκ ∂/∂t; the requirement that evolution preserve the CR-structure and the fibration measure makes it necessarily unitary, and Stone's theorem then gives the Schrödinger equation iκ ∂_t Ψ = Ĥ Ψ. Consistency of measurement across sectors relies on Book III's Langlands/Prism guarantee (III.T36), ensuring that spectral decomposition commutes with sector transport.
