When matter is cooled below a critical temperature, viscosity vanishes, resistance drops to zero, and vorticity is forced into quantized filaments. In τ³ none of these phenomena require new physics or separate postulates. Superfluidity and superconductivity are consequences of vorticity quantization on T²: when the defect ensemble collapses into a single macroscopic mode, there is no second mode to generate inter-mode friction, so viscosity vanishes identically. The quantization of circulation Γ_γ = nh/m follows from the single-valuedness of the wavefunction around the non-contractible cycles, whose constraint is H¹(T²; ℤ) ≅ ℤ². The chapter contains the part's deepest derivation: Cooper pairing is not postulated but derived from the electroweak-mixed normalization on T², making BCS theory a corollary of the fiber's two coupled gauge sectors.
