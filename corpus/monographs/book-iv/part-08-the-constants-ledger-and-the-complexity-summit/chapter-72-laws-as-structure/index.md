Throughout Parts I–VII, every particle, force, coupling constant, and mass ratio was derived from the fiber T² of τ³ = τ¹ ×_f T² without imposing a single equation of motion or symmetry principle from outside. This is not an accident of the approach — it is its central claim. Physical laws are not external rules governing matter; they are the internal categorical structure of τ³ itself. This chapter makes that claim precise across four domains: conservation laws are naturality conditions on functors; symmetries are endofunctors of τ³; Feynman diagrams are morphisms, not visual aids; and τ³ is self-enriched, meaning its morphism spaces are themselves τ³ objects. The last point yields the sharpest consequence: laws and matter have exactly the same ontological type.
