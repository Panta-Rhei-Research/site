Part V turns Category τ toward the sky.

The previous four Parts earned the temporal substrate
(Part I), the gravitational connection
(Part II), the thermodynamic inversion
(Part III), and the collective dynamics
of fluids and plasmas (Part IV).
All of these operate at scales
where laboratory confirmation is (in principle) direct.
Part V confronts the framework
with astrophysical and cosmological observations
where confirmation is indirect
and the orthodox consensus invokes
two invisible ingredients:
dark matter and dark energy.

The arc of Part V is a scaling ladder.
**Chapter 34** dissolves the "classical illusion":
the impression that Newtonian mechanics
is a fundamental large-scale law
rather than a coarse-grained readout
of the τ-Einstein equation.
**Chapter 35** derives Kepler's laws
as theorems of the rotational flux constraint
and uses the solar system
as a precision calibration laboratory.
**Chapter 36** redefines the galaxy
as a relational object in τ³,
and **Chapter 37** explains
the flat rotation curves of spiral galaxies
through the D-sector capacity gradient—without
dark matter halos.
**Chapter 38** ascends the compact-object ladder
(white dwarfs, neutron stars, black holes),
connecting each rung to the TOV builder
of Part II.
**Chapter 39** derives core-collapse supernovae
as topological channel openings
and closes the nucleosynthesis bridge
to the periodic table of Book IV.
The remaining six chapters (40–45)
complete the observational programme:
accretion and jets, gravitational waves,
the Event Horizon Telescope re-read,
the bullet cluster,
the Sector Exhaustion Theorem
(no dark matter, no dark energy),
and the Hubble tension.

The central structural claim is simple:
every astrophysical structure—star,
galaxy, cluster, void—is
a readout of H_∂[ω],
not an independent entity.
The five sectors of Category τ
exhaust the coupling budget.
There is no sixth sector,
no dark sector,
no room for invisible matter
or phantom energy.
