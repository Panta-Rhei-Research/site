Chapters 24 and 25 developed the macro fluid framework using the base-projected defect tuple — neutral, carrying no electric or color charge. This chapter adds charge to the macro picture. In Category τ, charge is not a primitive attribute attached to particles; it is a *boundary obstruction*: a topological defect in the *B*- or *C*-sector holonomy that cannot be removed by smooth deformation within the fiber T². Book IV derived this at the quantum level (the address-obstruction interpretation of QM). This chapter lifts the result to the macroscopic scale, proves the No-Isolated-Charges Theorem — every charged configuration on τ³ is neutralized at the boundary 𝕃 — and establishes the macro vocabulary for electromagnetic and chromodynamic flux. The theorem has two immediate corollaries: no isolated magnetic monopole can be constructed on τ³, and all color charge is confined to color-neutral composites.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Macro charge definition** (τ-effective): Q^macro(𝔡) = ∮_{τ¹} Hol_B(𝔡|_{t×T²}) dt — the base-integrated holonomy obstruction. A charge-neutral configuration gives Q^macro = 0.
- **No-Isolated-Charges Theorem** (τ-effective): the total boundary charge Q_∂^total = ∮_𝕃 Hol_{B+C}(𝔡) dσ = 0 for every τ-admissible configuration. The proof applies the Kirchhoff conservation law at the crossing point of the lemniscate 𝕃: the net *B*-sector flux entering the crossing equals the net flux leaving, forcing q₊ + q₋ = 0.
- **Electric charge neutrality** (τ-effective): every positive charge has an equal and opposite partner — not by empirical observation but by the lemniscate topology of the boundary.
- **No magnetic monopoles** (τ-effective): a monopole would require nonzero holonomy around every enclosing loop; all loops on τ³ are homotopic to combinations of the two lobe generators of π₁(𝕃), which sum to zero.
- **Color confinement at macro scale** (τ-effective): the *C*-sector coupling κ(C;3) = *ι*_τ³/(1−*ι*_τ) at depth 3 forces all color defects into singlets; the global *C*-holonomy on 𝕃 is trivial.
- **Why the universe is electrically neutral**: not an unexplained initial condition but a structural consequence of τ-admissibility.

## Lean coverage

- τ-effective throughout. All three conclusions of the No-Isolated-Charges Theorem follow from the holonomy algebra structure and τ-admissibility conditions, without any appeal to experiment.
- Weak charge (*A*-sector, SU(2)) plays a subdominant role at the macroscopic scales of this chapter — relevant at fiber level for weak decays, not at the macro collective-dynamics scale.

## Where this leads

The charge framework established here is prerequisite for the next three chapters. Chapter 27 (τ-Plasma) takes the *B*-sector boundary obstructions and asks what happens when they are mobile and numerous enough to constitute a plasma. Chapter 28 (τ-MHD) couples those charged carriers to the macro fluid equations of Chapter 24 through the induction equation. The No-Isolated-Charges Theorem underlies the forced quasi-neutrality that is the starting point of all plasma theory in Part IV.
