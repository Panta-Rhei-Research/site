Book IV, Chapter 53, established the fundamental principle: every phase transition is an inequality crossing in the defect-tuple space (μ, ν, κ, θ). No external order parameter is needed; the defect tuple *is* the order parameter. That chapter classified the fiber-level regimes — superfluids, superconductors, crystals, glass, quasicrystals — by their defect-tuple signatures. This chapter lifts the principle to the macroscopic scale. Phase transitions at the macro level — stellar phase boundaries, neutron star crusts, white dwarf crystallization, the glass transition in astrophysical melts — are the same structural phenomenon as their fiber-level counterparts: inequality crossings in the base-projected defect-tuple space. The chapter closes Part IV by completing the collective-dynamics vocabulary prerequisite for the astrophysical applications of Part V. Phase transition boundaries are smooth rather than singular because the regularity guarantee of *III.T25* prevents blow-up precisely at crossing points.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Crossing preservation** (τ-effective): the base projection pr_base preserves inequality crossings — if the fiber-level defect tuple crosses an inequality boundary, the macro defect tuple crosses the corresponding base-projected boundary. Averaging can smooth first-order fiber transitions into gradual macro crossovers; this is the structural origin of sharp vs. broad astrophysical phase boundaries.
- **Macro τ-crystal** (τ-effective): μ^macro = 0 (rigid), ν^macro = 0, κ^macro large (high compression rigidity), θ^macro ≠ 0 (long-range topological order). The topological charge θ encodes lattice symmetry; dislocation density appears as δθ.
- **Macro τ-glass** (τ-effective): same as crystal except θ^macro = 0 — frozen but with no long-range order. The glass transition is a kinetic arrest in defect-tuple space at μ → 0, not a thermodynamic singularity. Aging is monotonic drift toward lower μ at fixed θ = 0; annealing is a controlled excursion back into the liquid region followed by a return crossing at a more favorable coordinate.
- **Critical surface geometry** (τ-effective): each inequality function f_i defines a codimension-1 surface in the 4-dimensional defect-tuple space. Triple points are codimension-2 intersections; critical points are codimension-2 tangencies. This is the structural analogue of the Gibbs phase rule.
- **White dwarf crystallization** (τ-effective): a first-order transition with μ dropping to 0 and θ jumping to θ_lattice. The latent heat delay produces a measurable pile-up in the white dwarf luminosity function consistent with the Gaia color-magnitude diagram observation (Tremblay et al. 2019). Registry *V.D336*, *V.P190* (NS crust-core transition density, crust fraction from defect-tuple).
- **Neutron star crust phases** (τ-effective): outer crust to inner crust to core represent successive inequality crossings as pressure and density increase. The crust-core transition density is a base-projected crossing of the κ-inequality. *V.R471* flags the condensed-matter status as partial: individual phase sequence is τ-effective; quantitative transition densities are currently estimated.
