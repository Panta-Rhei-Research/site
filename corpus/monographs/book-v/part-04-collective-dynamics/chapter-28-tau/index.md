A plasma is a state of matter in which charge carriers — ions and electrons — are free to move independently. In Category τ, plasma is a *boundary-obstruction flow*: the charged defects (topological obstructions in the *B*-sector holonomy established in Chapter 26) propagate as a collective through the macro defect-transport equation of Chapter 24. The plasma framework derives the three foundational plasma phenomena — forced quasi-neutrality, plasma oscillations, and Debye shielding — not from empirical fits or hydrodynamic approximations but from the sector structure and lemniscate boundary topology. The second half of the chapter applies these structural results to astrophysical plasmas: the solar wind, stellar interiors, and accretion environments. The plasma vocabulary established here is prerequisite for MHD (Chapter 28) and Alfvén waves (Chapter 29).

## What this chapter contributes

- **τ-plasma definition** (τ-effective): a macro defect configuration with positive *B*-sector defect density n_B(𝔡) > 0, mobile carriers (μ_B > μ_crit), and Debye length λ_D ≪ system size L. All four states of matter — solid, liquid, gas, plasma — are configurations of the same defect tuple (μ, ν, κ, θ), distinguished by their inequality signatures.
- **Forced quasi-neutrality** (τ-effective): from the No-Isolated-Charges Theorem (Chapter 26), the total charge vanishes at the boundary. This forces quasi-neutrality in every macroscopic plasma volume at scales larger than λ_D — not as an empirical approximation but as a structural consequence.
- **Plasma oscillations** (τ-effective): the *B*-sector restoring force against charge separation drives oscillations at the plasma frequency ω_p, with ω_p² determined by the *B*-sector coupling κ(B;2) = *ι*_τ² and the carrier density.
- **Debye shielding** (τ-effective): the exponential decay of boundary characters at length scale λ_D emerges from the decay rate of *B*-sector holonomy perturbations away from a point charge — the shielding length is set by the ratio of thermal energy to electrostatic energy in *B*-sector terms.
- **Astrophysical applications** (τ-effective): solar wind modeled as a radially expanding boundary-obstruction flow; stellar interiors as high-density plasma regimes where pressure is dominated by the *B*-sector coupling; accretion environments as turbulent plasma cascades feeding the MHD regime.

## Lean coverage

- τ-effective throughout. The fundamental plasma phenomena follow directly from the sector structure and the lemniscate boundary topology, without appeal to experiment beyond dimensional anchors.
- The MHD limit — where the plasma can be treated as a single conducting fluid — is defined in this chapter and handed off to Chapter 28. The plasma chapter deliberately stops at the single-fluid threshold.

## Where this leads

This chapter provides the plasma foundation for the final three chapters of Part IV. Chapter 28 (τ-MHD) takes the conducting plasma in the MHD limit and derives the frozen-flux theorem, magnetic reconnection as a defect event, and MHD instabilities. Chapter 29 (τ-Alfvén) develops the photon-in-fluid mode that propagates through the magnetized plasma. Both chapters build their physics directly on the quasi-neutrality and Debye framework established here.
