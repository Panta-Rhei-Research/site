Chapter 28 derived the τ-MHD system and introduced Alfvén waves as coupled fluid–field oscillations. This chapter develops the Alfvén mode in full structural detail. The central insight: an Alfvén wave is a *photon-in-fluid* — the electromagnetic degree of freedom (the *B*-sector holonomy) propagating not through vacuum but through a conducting medium (the *D*- and *A*-sector fluid). The propagation speed is not c but v_A = B/√(μ₀ρ), because the fluid inertia loads the electromagnetic oscillation. In the τ-framework, this is a *mixed-sector mode*: a coupled oscillation of the *B*-sector (magnetic field perturbation) and the *D*-sector (fluid velocity perturbation). On the fiber T², the Alfvén mode corresponds to a coupled periodic orbit — a trajectory that oscillates between the *B* and *D* directions in the holonomy algebra. The chapter applies the Alfvén framework to stellar physics (coronal heating, stellar wind driving) and the interstellar medium (turbulent energy transport, cosmic ray scattering).

## What this chapter contributes

- **Mixed-sector mode definition** (τ-effective): a propagating perturbation on τ³ involving coherent oscillation between two or more sector components of H_∂[ω]. The Alfvén mode is the *B*–*D* mixed mode; the cross-coupling κ(B,D) = *ι*_τ²(1−*ι*_τ) controls the mode's existence and polarization.
- **Alfvén speed derivation** (τ-effective): v_A = B/√(μ₀ρ) follows from the balance between *B*-sector field tension and *D*-sector fluid inertia in the coupled-orbit equations on T². The factor μ₀ is the *B*-sector permeability readout.
- **Photon-in-fluid interpretation** (τ-effective): in vacuum, the *B*-sector oscillates freely at speed c (photon). In a conducting fluid, the *D*-sector inertia couples to the *B*-sector, slowing the oscillation to v_A < c. The Alfvén wave is the same topological object as the photon — a *B*-sector periodic orbit — loaded by the *D*-sector.
- **Dispersion and polarization** (τ-effective): torsional Alfvén modes (pure azimuthal oscillation) and compressional modes (mixed with acoustic) follow from the decomposition of the *B*–*D* coupled orbit into symmetric and antisymmetric components.
- **Coronal heating application** (τ-effective): Alfvén waves propagating upward from the photosphere carry energy flux ~ B²v_A/μ₀, sufficient to supply the observed coronal luminosity. The dissipation mechanism is topological relaxation (reconnection at current sheets) rather than resistive Ohmic heating.
- **Cosmic ray scattering** (τ-effective): cosmic rays scatter off Alfvén-wave magnetic field perturbations through pitch-angle diffusion — the scattering cross-section is set by the *B*-sector perturbation amplitude in the inertial range of MHD turbulence.
