The singularity is the theory's confession of its own breakdown. The information paradox — the apparent loss of quantum information behind the horizon — has driven four decades of unresolved debate. The horizon is not a sphere S² but a torus T² — the same torus that forms the fiber of τ³. There is no interior singularity, and there is no information paradox.
