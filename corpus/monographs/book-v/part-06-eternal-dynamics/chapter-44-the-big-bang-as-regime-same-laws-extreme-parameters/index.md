What is the Big Bang? Orthodox cosmology treats it as a singular event — a point of infinite density, infinite temperature, and zero volume — from which spacetime itself emerged. The singularity theorems of Penrose and Hawking guarantee that under mild energy conditions, a past-incomplete geodesic must exist: the universe "began" in a singularity. But a singularity is not a physical state. It is a signal that the theory has broken down. General relativity predicts its own failure. In Category τ, the Big Bang is neither a singularity nor a creation event. It is a **regime** — the pre-hadronic regime of the same τ-Einstein equation that governs the present epoch. The equation R^H = κ_τ · T does not change; only the boundary characters entering it take extreme values at early refinement depths. The Planck epoch is not an era of unknown physics but the first few α-ticks, where boundary characters approach their saturation values.
