A theory that cannot be falsified is not a physical theory. Category τ claims to derive cosmology from seven axioms on five generators — a claim with sharp empirical teeth. This chapter puts that claim on the line by assembling a **falsification pack**: a collection of pre-registered predictions organised by observational domain, each carrying its scope label (Structural / Parametric / Contact), a precision estimate, and a clear statement of what observational result would constitute a falsification. Seven domains are covered; the chapter is deliberately conservative about scope, flagging every conjectural element and every result that awaits future instrumentation. Honesty is the price of credibility.

## What this chapter contributes

The chapter organises the τ-effective and conjectural predictions from across Part VI into a structured register. The **scope protocol** (Structural / Parametric / Contact) distinguishes τ-effective structural claims from parametric predictions involving ι_τ, and from contact-level claims that require the full calibration cascade. The **CMB power spectrum** domain (predictions C1–C9) includes: acoustic peaks (Structural), no dark matter contribution (Structural — five sectors, no sixth), scalar spectral index n_s = 1 − 2/57 = 0.9649 at +13 ppm from Planck (Parametric), tensor-to-scalar ratio r = ι_τ⁴ ≈ 0.014 falsifiable at ~14σ by CMB-S4 (Parametric), Silk damping scale ℓ_D = ℓ₁ × κ_D/κ_B ≈ 1244.0 at +9 ppm (Parametric, upgraded from conjectural in Wave 36D), and baryon loading R_b(z_rec) = 0.615 (Parametric). The **BAO and SN Ia** domain tests the expansion history without dark matter. The **growth of structure and weak lensing** domain tests f·σ₈(z) against the τ-native gravitational coupling κ(D;1) = 1 − ι_τ. The **BBN** domain enters the Y_p, D/H, He-3/H, and Li-7/H predictions from Chapter 45 as contact-level tests. The **GW standard sirens** domain enters the Hubble constant prediction and the T² QNM ratio ι_τ⁻¹ ≈ 2.930 as a gravitational-wave topology discriminator. The **galaxy rotation curves** domain enters the flat-rotation-curve prediction from τ-native dark sector dissolution (no dark matter halo). The **falsification standard** section states the hierarchy: a Structural falsification (e.g., a sixth gauge force confirmed) would invalidate the axioms; a Contact falsification (e.g., BBN abundance tension) would indicate a calibration error, not necessarily an axiom failure.

## Lean coverage

Scope varies by domain: Structural and Parametric predictions are τ-effective; Contact predictions are conjectural. The chapter explicitly acknowledges that the calibration cascade from τ-native quantities to SI observables introduces residual uncertainty in all Contact-level predictions, and that CMB predictions C2 and C8 depend on Boltzmann transfer functions not yet derived from first principles in τ. The baryon asymmetry exponent coincidence — the e-fold window number N_e/dim(τ³) = 19 equals the CF-window exponent governing η_B — is noted as a structural constraint that may tighten both predictions simultaneously.
