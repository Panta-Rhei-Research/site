Is the universe infinite? Orthodox cosmology answers ambiguously: the observable universe is finite (bounded by the particle horizon), but the "full" universe may be infinite depending on spatial curvature. The ΛCDM model with Ω_k = 0 admits both finite and infinite topologies — the theory is silent on global structure. Category τ answers unambiguously: the universe is **finite, structured, and closed**. This is not a postulate but a theorem chain: the profinite structure of τ³, the finite count of stable defect configurations, and the monotone dynamics of the refinement operator together guarantee that all structure fits within a finite portion of τ³, with no infinite hierarchy, no fractal abyss, and no endless plain of new motifs.

## What this chapter contributes

The chapter establishes four linked τ-effective results. The **Finite Motif Theorem** proves that only finitely many distinct topological motifs exist in τ³: a motif is an equivalence class of defect tuple configurations that is stable (ρ-invariant) and irreducible; the defect tuple has four components each bounded in [0,1], and the stability condition restricts the allowed configurations to a finite set at each refinement depth. The **Saturation Radius Theorem** proves that beyond a characteristic scale R_sat — determined by the number of distinct motifs and the primorial lattice spacing — every region of τ³ is a repetition of an already-encountered motif. The **Absorbing Pattern Theorem** proves that the large-scale structure converges to an absorbing pattern P_∞: long-range structure is eventually determined by the local pattern, with no new topological information appearing at large scales. The **Global Finiteness Corollary** assembles these three results: the universe admits no infinite hierarchy of nested structures, no fractal self-similar complexity, and no open-ended motif production. The full structure of τ³ at any refinement depth is captured by finitely many pieces of local data. This result depends on the Global Cartesian Gluing Theorem (Book III, III.T50) for the coherence of the global assembly, and on the Sector Exhaustion Theorem (Part V) for the bounded count of topological type.

## Lean coverage

Scope τ-effective. The finiteness chain rests directly on the kernel axioms (K0–K6) and the profinite structure of τ³: the same boundedness argument that underlies the No-Singularity Theorem (Chapter 43) extends from the cosmological to the global-structure setting. The quantitative value of R_sat is contact-level (conjectural in SI units), but its existence and the finitude of the motif count are τ-effective. Boundary cases — whether BH excisions contribute motifs that prevent full saturation — are addressed by the No-Shrink Theorem: excision count is bounded and non-increasing under the Merger Normal Form.

## Where this leads

Global Finiteness sets the stage for the cosmological endstate (Chapter 52): if structure saturates within a finite region, the absorbing pattern that the endstate converges to is the finite terminal configuration. It also closes the density gradient narrative from Chapter 46: the BH-dominated cosmic web is the chart-level readout of the absorbing pattern. The Falsification Pack (Chapter 53) notes that an observational detection of genuine large-scale fractal structure or infinite hierarchical nesting at scales beyond current surveys would constitute a structural falsification of the Absorbing Pattern Theorem.
