How does the universe end? Orthodox cosmology offers three scenarios: heat death, Big Crunch, or Big Rip. All three extrapolate current expansion dynamics to infinity and end in catastrophe or stasis. Category τ rejects all three. The universe does not "end." The temporal boundary α_o — the accumulation point of the α-orbit at n → ∞ — is not a catastrophe but a **saturation**: the absorbing pattern P_∞ reaches its final state, and the defect entropy S_def converges to its irreducible BH floor while the reference entropy converges to the total entropy budget. The result is **eternal circulation** — a stable, self-sustaining pattern of boundary characters flowing on 𝕃, with no further generative activity but perpetual coherent motion.
