The temporal epoch opens at ignition depth n_ign, but what does the universe structurally look like at that moment? This chapter answers: hot, dense, and maximally coupled. At n_ign, no mode of the boundary holonomy algebra H_∂[ω] has yet been filtered by the primorial sieve — all spectral labels are active, the total spectral energy is at its maximum, and all five sectors are coupled at full strength through ω (the maximal coupling condition, V.D22). The τ-Einstein equation holds at all depths without modification; in the opening regime it operates in its strongest regime, with gravitational character G_ω at its peak and no initial singularity — the Big Bang is a well-defined algebraic state, not a breakdown of the theory. Cosmological inflation is identified structurally as early refinement progression: the refinement progression rate ℋ(n) = p_{n+1}/p_n = q_{n+1} (the next prime) shrinks α-ticks super-exponentially, and the chart-level readout of this shrinkage is what orthodox cosmology calls exponential expansion. No inflaton field is required. Flatness and the horizon problem do not arise: the fiber T² is flat by construction, and all spatial points at depth n are structurally simultaneous.
