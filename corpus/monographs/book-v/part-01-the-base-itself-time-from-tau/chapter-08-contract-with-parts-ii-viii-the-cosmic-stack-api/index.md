Part I is complete. Seven chapters have earned the base vocabulary of Book V — time as arc length on τ¹, three temporal epochs, high-energy initial conditions, photon ontology as null intertwiners, operational distance, redshift as refinement drift, the distance ladder as readout calibration, and the CMB and CνB as constraint surfaces — without invoking gravity, thermodynamics, or any cosmological model. This closing chapter converts those results into a formal interface contract: the *Cosmic Stack API*. The API lists seven fixed inputs (I1–I7) that Part I delivers unchanged to all subsequent Parts, five inherited constants from Book IV (ι_τ, sector couplings, calibration anchor, mass ratio, neutrino mass prediction), five boundary constraints propagated by Σ_CMB and Σ_{CνB}, and seven required outputs that Parts II–VIII must produce. The pasting of local sector constructions into a globally coherent structure is licensed by Global Cartesian Gluing (Book III, III.T50). The vocabulary table registers 21 definitions and theorems earned in Part I; 19 are τ-effective, 2 conjectural (readout curvature and dark energy artifact, deferred to Part V). The chapter also makes explicit what Part I has *not* done — no G, no τ-Einstein equation, no Friedmann equation, no dark sector exclusion, no black holes — so that the post-conditions for each subsequent Part are unambiguous.
