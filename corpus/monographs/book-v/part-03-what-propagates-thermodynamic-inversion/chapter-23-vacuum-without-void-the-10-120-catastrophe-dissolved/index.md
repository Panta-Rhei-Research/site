The vacuum catastrophe is the most extreme discrepancy in all of theoretical physics: quantum field theory predicts a vacuum energy density of roughly 10¹¹³ J/m³ by summing zero-point energies ½ℏω over all momentum modes up to the Planck cutoff, while cosmological observations of the accelerating expansion point to a value near 10⁻⁷ J/m³ — a ratio of 10¹²⁰. No orthodox mechanism cancels this sum without fine-tuning to one part in 10¹²⁰. This chapter dissolves the catastrophe. The dissolution is not a cancellation — not a delicate subtraction of one enormous number from another. It is a *dissolution of the premise*. In the τ-framework the vacuum energy is not a sum of mode energies at all. It is the energy of the coherent boundary state H∂[ω] in its ground configuration, a finite integral over the compact lemniscate boundary, with no momentum sum, no cutoff, and no catastrophe.

## What this chapter contributes

The chapter establishes the τ-vacuum formally and proves three theorems that together constitute the complete structural resolution.

Definition *def:ch25-tau-vacuum* identifies the τ-vacuum as the ground configuration ω₀ ∈ H∂[ω] satisfying three conditions simultaneously: the tangential Cauchy–Riemann operator annihilates it (∂̄_b ω₀ = 0 everywhere on τ³, meaning the state is holomorphic throughout); the defect entropy S_def[ω₀] = 0; and its energy equals the boundary energy E_bdry. This is not an empty space with fluctuations but a *definite state* — the coherent boundary state — with a definite, finite energy.

Theorem *thm:ch25-vacuum-boundary* proves that E_vac = E_bdry = ∮_ℒ |H∂[ω₀]|² dσ, where the integral runs over the lemniscate ℒ = S¹ ∨ S¹. Because ℒ is compact and the boundary holonomy is smooth, this integral is finite with no ultraviolet issue. The QFT mode sum diverges because it integrates over infinitely many momentum modes; the τ-vacuum energy involves no momentum integral and hence no cutoff question.

Theorem *thm:ch25-category-error* delivers the diagnostic core: the QFT vacuum energy density ρ_vac^QFT is not the energy of the vacuum state. It is a *refinement count* — the number of lattice modes at the finest refinement level of the character lattice Λ_CR^(n) on the fiber T², each weighted by its zero-point energy. This count corresponds to the refinement entropy S_ref, not to the physical energy. Proposition *prop:ch25-qft-refinement* makes the correspondence explicit: at cutoff level n the mode sum scales as p^(3n) ℏc/(2ℓ_ref), which recovers the standard QFT result when p^n is identified with the Planck cutoff. A corollary records the consequence for the τ-Einstein equation: the vacuum source term T_vac = E_bdry/V is finite and cutoff-independent; the 10¹²⁰ discrepancy does not arise because no mode sum appears in the source term.

Theorem *thm:ch25-vacuum-state* proves that the H∂[ω] ground state is the *unique* physical vacuum: existence follows from the Global Defect Exhaustion Theorem (Chapter 23 of Part III), which guarantees the α-orbit drives every initial configuration to S_def = 0; uniqueness follows from the kernel axioms K0–K6 determining the coherence kernel uniquely; minimality follows because any configuration with S_def > 0 has non-holomorphic insertions that contribute positively to the CR-tension integral. As a subsidiary result, the chapter shows the Casimir effect (*prop:ch25-casimir*) is reproduced as a boundary effect — the difference in boundary energies between constrained and unconstrained geometry — without invoking a mode sum, confirming that the Casimir effect does not distinguish mode summation from boundary energy.
