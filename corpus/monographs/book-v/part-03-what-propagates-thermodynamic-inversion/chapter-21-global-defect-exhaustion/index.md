Chapters 19 and 20 established the Categorical Second Law and the entropy splitting S = *S*_def + *S*_ref. Defect entropy decreases monotonically and the contraction factor (1 − *ι*_τ) ≈ 0.6587 controls the rate. But a monotonically decreasing sequence might never reach zero — it could approach an asymptote. This chapter proves that it does not. The key ingredient is simple and decisive: defect bundles are discrete objects, integer-counted excitations on a lattice. When the geometric contraction bound drops below one, there is no room for a fractional defect. The last defect is absorbed, *S*_def jumps to zero, and the coherence horizon — not merely asymptotic but a realized finite event — is crossed.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Finite initial defect count** (τ-effective): at the coarsest refinement level, |𝒟_0| ≤ |Λ_CR^(0)| < ∞. No renormalization needed — the lattice is finite from the start, in contrast to QFT's infinite mode sum.
- **Finite global defect budget** (τ-effective): ℬ_def ≤ |𝒟_0|/*ι*_τ. The universe's total irreversibility is bounded above by a number that depends only on the initial defect count and the master constant.
- **Global Defect Exhaustion Theorem** (τ-effective): there exists a finite n_coh such that |𝒟_n| = 0 for all n ≥ n_coh. The coherence horizon is bounded by ⌈ln|𝒟_0| / ln(1/(1−*ι*_τ))⌉ — at most 441 steps for a baryon-count initial condition, at most 661 steps for the vacuum-catastrophe scale.
- **Finite irreversibility** (τ-effective): every friction event, every dissipative process draws from the finite budget ℬ_def. After n_coh, no further irreversible process occurs.
- **Post-horizon vacuum circulation**: the α-orbit continues beyond n_coh but evolution is defect-free. Time does not stop; the arrow of time — irreversibility — does.
- **No Poincaré recurrence conflict**: recurrence in the post-horizon regime is recurrence of a zero-defect state. Higher-defect states no longer exist to recur into.

## Lean coverage

- τ-effective throughout. The exhaustion theorem depends on: the finite initial defect count, the geometric contraction bound from Chapter 19, and the integer threshold lemma (a non-negative integer sequence satisfying the contraction bound must reach zero in finitely many steps).
- All quantitative bounds are sub-1000 orbit steps across the entire physically relevant range of initial conditions.
- The defect half-life n_{1/2} ≈ 1.66 orbit steps is universal — it does not depend on defect type or sector.
- Orbit steps are not years. The calibration between orbit depth and physical time depends on regime; the *finiteness* of n_coh is regime-independent.

The coherence horizon is the structural endpoint that the rest of the monograph builds toward. Chapter 23 (dark energy) depends on the defect-to-refinement transition whose timing is set by n_coh.
