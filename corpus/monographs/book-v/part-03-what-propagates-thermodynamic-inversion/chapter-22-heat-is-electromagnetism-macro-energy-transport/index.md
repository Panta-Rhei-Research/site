Classical thermodynamics identifies three modes of energy transport — radiation, conduction, and convection — and treats them as if they have different physical origins: one a field phenomenon, one a microscopic collision chain, one a bulk flow. This chapter shows that all three are the same thing: electromagnetic boundary exchange in the *B*-sector (γ-generator, EM) of the boundary holonomy algebra. Conduction is not molecular billiards; it is near-field EM transport through the lattice. Convection is not kinetic mass transfer; it is EM-driven coherent displacement of the defect-functional profile. The fine-structure constant α ≈ (11/15)² *ι*_τ⁴ controls all three modes — it sets the coupling strength between the radiating configuration and the boundary character that carries the energy. The trichotomy is pedagogical, not physical.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Heat is electromagnetism theorem** (τ-effective): all macroscopic energy transport at enrichment layer E₁ is mediated by the *B*-sector of H_∂[ω]. A single boundary integral Q̇ = ∮ ⟨H_∂^B[ω], δ[ω]⟩ dσ unifies radiation, conduction, and convection.
- **Radiation** as far-field *B*-sector transport: radiative flux scales as *ι*_τ² · ρ_def² · c, reproducing the Stefan–Boltzmann law under the identification of defect density with temperature.
- **Conduction** as near-field *B*-sector transport: phonons are collective modes of the electromagnetic lattice potential; the Fourier thermal conductivity κ_th = (1/3)c_V v_s ℓ_mfp with all three factors determined by κ(B;2) = *ι*_τ².
- **Convection** as *B*-sector defect-functional displacement: pressure gradients are electromagnetic — kinetic and interaction pressure both arise from *B*-sector Coulomb repulsion.
- **α governs macroscopic thermodynamics** (τ-effective): the energy transport rate between any two macroscopic configurations is proportional to α, with the proportionality constant varying by regime but the α-dependence universal.
- **Two relaxation channels**: geometric relaxation (fast, fiber-scale diffusion, rate ~ α·c/ℓ) and topological relaxation (slow, base-scale, rate ~ (1−*ι*_τ) per orbit step). The hierarchy τ_geom ≪ τ_top explains why equilibration precedes defect exhaustion.
