With the PPAS algorithm established in Chapter 37, this chapter examines its large-scale consequences on the fitness landscape. Speciation is the splitting of one connected PPAS trajectory into two reproductively isolated ones: a distinction boundary forms between populations that can no longer exchange information through recombination. The three modes — allopatric (geographic barrier), sympatric (disruptive selection within the same range), and peripatric (founder effect in a peripheral population) — each describe a different mechanism by which the separation is initiated, but all produce the same formal outcome: two independent PPAS searches running on distinct regions of the fitness landscape. Adaptive radiation is the rapid filling of an empty fitness landscape when ecological opportunity is present; Darwin's finches and the post-K-Pg mammalian radiation are the canonical examples. Convergent evolution reveals a deeper fact: the fitness landscape has universal attractors — peaks that trajectories reach regardless of starting point — because physical law constrains design. Camera eyes evolved at least 40 times, powered flight 4 times, biological sonar independently in bats and toothed whales. The depth of convergence, from gross morphology to molecular sequence (Prestin in bat and dolphin cochleae), reflects the narrowness of the attractor. Extinction is path termination; mass extinction is catastrophic landscape reorganization that destroys existing basins and creates new ones, invariably followed by adaptive radiation into the vacated space. The five Phanerozoic mass extinctions each exhibit this pattern: the End-Permian event eliminated ~96% of marine species, and recovery took ~10 million years, but the subsequent radiation filled the reorganized landscape; the End-Cretaceous event cleared the large-bodied terrestrial niches, and the resulting mammalian radiation produced all major orders within ~10 million years. The universality of this post-extinction pattern confirms that the fitness landscape, reshaped by the extinction event, retains the capacity for PPAS exploration.
