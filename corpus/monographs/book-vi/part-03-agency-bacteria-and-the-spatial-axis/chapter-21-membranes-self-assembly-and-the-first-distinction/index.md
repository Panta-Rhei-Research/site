The τ-Distinction predicate D: X → 2_τ requires a physical object that separates self from non-self. That object is the cell membrane. Every living cell is bounded by a lipid bilayer approximately 5–8 nm thick; without it there is no inside and no outside, no carrier on which the distinction can be defined. This chapter sharpens the formal correspondence between the membrane and the lemniscate boundary L = S¹ ∨ S¹ of the τ³ domain, develops the thermodynamics of spontaneous bilayer assembly, maps the four macromolecular families to categorical functions, and recasts protein folding as Yang–Mills gauge dynamics.

The Membrane as Lemniscate Boundary definition (VI.D33) identifies the lipid bilayer with L through three correspondences: the two lobes (S¹ outer leaflet facing non-self, S¹ inner leaflet facing self) map to the two circles of L; the clopen condition of τ-Distinction is realized by the bilayer as the shared boundary of D⁻¹(+) (interior) and D⁻¹(−) (exterior); and selective permeability — channel proteins admitting specific ions while the lipid matrix excludes all others — is the material realization of the H_∂-equivariance condition. The leaflet asymmetry (phosphatidylcholine and sphingomyelin concentrated in the outer leaflet, phosphatidylserine and phosphatidylethanolamine in the inner) is maintained by ATP-dependent flippases; when a cell dies and the asymmetry collapses, the phosphatidylserine signal on the outer face is the molecular signature of distinction failure. Bilayer topology — two leaflets forming a closed boundary — is invariant across all three domains despite radically different lipid chemistries (ether-linked isoprenoids in Archaea, ester-linked fatty acids in Bacteria and Eukarya).

Self-Assembly as Boundary-Induced Distinction (VI.P12) establishes that the distinction self-assembles: place phospholipids in water and bilayers form spontaneously on millisecond timescales. No enzymes, no ATP, no template. The thermodynamic basis is the hydrophobic effect: ~3.4 kJ/mol per CH₂ group transferred from water to the nonpolar interior; for a typical C16-chain phospholipid, the total driving force is ~−109 kJ/mol, far exceeding thermal energy. The Membrane Thickness from ι_τ theorem (VI.T_Mem, τ-effective) derives the universal 7–8 nm bilayer thickness from the master constant: the optimal chain length is ⌊ι_τ⁻²⌋ = ⌊8.585⌋ = 8 carbons per half-chain, corresponding to 16–17 carbons per full chain; with the tilted-bilayer conformation factor and headgroup lengths, the result is ~7.5 nm, within 4% of the universal observed value. The CMC drops to ~10⁻¹⁰ M precisely for C16–C18 lipids — the ι_τ-optimal chain lengths.

The Molecular Architecture definition (VI.D34) maps the four macromolecular classes to four categorical forces: proteins (catalysis and structure) to Yang–Mills, nucleic acids (information storage) to BSD, lipids (compartmentalization) to Hodge, and carbohydrates (energy and signaling) to Riemann. The four classes are categorically complete: they exhaust the functional requirements of a self-maintaining carrier, and their incompatible molecular properties prevent any single class from fulfilling more than one primary function. Protein folding is then recast as gauge dynamics on the conformational manifold parametrized by backbone dihedral angles (φ_i, ψ_i). The native fold is the flat connection F_μν = 0; folding is gauge-field relaxation from high-curvature (unfolded) to flat (native) configuration; misfolding is kinetic trapping in a non-flat state; amyloid diseases (Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, prion) are gauge-failure pathologies in which the wrong flat connection propagates.
