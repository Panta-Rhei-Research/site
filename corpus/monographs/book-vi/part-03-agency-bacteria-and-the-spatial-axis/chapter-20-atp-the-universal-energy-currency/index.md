Every living cell runs on a single oscillation: ATP hydrolysis releases free energy (~30.5 kJ/mol under standard conditions, ~50–55 kJ/mol under cellular conditions); ADP phosphorylation stores it. A human cell performs this cycle ~10¹⁶ times per day, recycling roughly 40 kg of ATP across the whole body every 24 hours — nearly the body's mass. The chapter asks not only what life's energy currency is but why there is one currency, why this one, and whether the hydrolysis quantum is a structural consequence of the τ-framework or a historical contingency. The established biochemistry and the τ-effective additions are kept carefully separate throughout.

The ATP/ADP Oscillation definition (VI.D32) formalizes the energy cycle as a Poincaré-periodic pair of half-reactions on Ag_π: the hydrolysis half-reaction ε₊ releases ΔG_ATP, the phosphorylation half-reaction ε₋ stores ΔG_input, and the composition ε₋ ∘ ε₊ returns the system to its initial state, forming a closed orbit in (ATP, ADP, P_i) concentration space. The τ-framework then adds the ι_τ calibration (τ-effective): ΔG_ATP = ι_τ² × E_P-O × n_eff ≈ (0.3415)² × 420 kJ/mol × 0.62 ≈ 30.34 kJ/mol, within 0.5% of the observed standard-state value of 30.5 kJ/mol. This "Goldilocks energy" matches the cost of the most widespread cellular work — Na⁺/K⁺ ion pumping and actin–myosin contraction both require ~30 kJ/mol — almost exactly.

ATP synthase is the rotary machine that drives the phosphorylation half-reaction. The F₀F₁ complex is a nanometer-scale turbine: the c-ring (8–15 subunits depending on organism) rotates at ~100–130 rev/s driven by proton-motive force, one proton per c-subunit; the γ-stalk transmits this rotation to the F₁ catalytic head, which cycles three β-subunits through open, loose, and tight states, producing three ATP per 360° rotation. Single-molecule experiments by Noji et al. (1997) directly visualized the rotation. The machine is fully reversible: when ATP is high or the proton gradient is dissipated, it runs backward as a proton pump. Mammalian mitochondria with n_c = 8 achieve ~85–95% thermodynamic efficiency — comparable to the best electric motors.

The Universal Currency Uniqueness theorem (VI.T19, τ-effective) provides the structural answer to why one currency rather than many. The Life Loop Class closure condition constrains any energy oscillation on Ag_π to be a two-state cycle (charged/discharged) with a single energy quantum mediating the transition. A second independent oscillation mode would either decouple from the π-orbit (violating the coupling constraint from Navier–Stokes dissipation) or be isomorphic to the first. GTP, CTP, and UTP are not independent currencies: their pools are equilibrated with ATP via nucleoside diphosphate kinase. The chemiosmotic mechanism — proton gradient across a membrane, proton flow through ATP synthase — is universal across all three domains, confirming it is a structural requirement and not a historical accident. The logical chain membrane → gradient → ATP synthase → ATP → agency is developed explicitly.
