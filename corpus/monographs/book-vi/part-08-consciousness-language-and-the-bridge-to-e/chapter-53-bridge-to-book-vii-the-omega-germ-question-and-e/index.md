Book VI opens with the question "What is life?" and closes, fifty-three chapters later, with a proved answer and a formally located residue that the answer cannot reach. Every structural question about the ω-germ code has been answered: how it is encoded, how it replicates, how it converges under merger, how it is observationally accessible. One question has not been answered and is proved unanswerable within the framework: why does this particular code exist rather than another? The ω-Germ Question is not about mechanism — mechanism is settled — but about existential contingency. This chapter proves it lies outside diagrammatic resolution, delivers the six completed E₂ structures to Book VII as formal inputs, locates the principled science-faith boundary, and previews the five movements of Book VII's E₃ development.

A question is resolved diagrammatically in Category τ if its answer can be exhibited as a commutative diagram — a morphism, a factorization, a limit, a colimit. Every result in Book VI is of this kind. The ω-Germ Question is not: commutative diagrams relate existing objects and presuppose the existence of the objects they relate; no τ-morphism has "existence of a specific code" as its codomain; the initial object ∅ and the unique morphism ∅ → X assert that X receives from the initial object but do not explain why X is there to receive. The boundary is therefore structural, not epistemic: it is drawn by the architecture of the formalism itself, analogous to — but distinct from — Gödelian incompleteness (which is syntactic, about provability within a system; the ω-germ limit is semantic, about existential grounding). The six export contracts to Book VII are: (1) the Life predicate — Distinction + SelfDesc, closed formal package with seven hallmarks as theorems; (2) the 4+1 Life sectors — organizational grammar of biological and gravitational Life; (3) genotype and phenotype as τ-predicates — ω-germ code and normal form formally grounded, three identity regimes; (4) the ω-Germ Question — explicitly formulated open question with proof of non-diagrammatic status; (5) Life basins — galaxies anchored by SMBHs, basin merger driving code refinement toward ι_τ; (6) substrate-agnosticism — the Life predicates are substrate-independent by verification on both biological and gravitational carriers, and any philosophical argument depending on carbon is not an argument about Life. The science-faith boundary is not agnosticism ("we do not know") and not fideism ("believe without evidence") — it is a structural limit, located exactly at the ω-Germ Question, as sharp as the Layer Separation Lemma's NS-to-BH phase boundary. Book VII receives six completed structures and one formally stated open question, opens at consciousness, and proceeds through ethics, meaning, the good and the beautiful, and the philosophical resolution of the ω-Germ Question.
