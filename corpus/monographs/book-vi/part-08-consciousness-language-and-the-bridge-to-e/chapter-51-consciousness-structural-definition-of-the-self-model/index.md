The enrichment ladder (Chapter 50) assigns the step from E₂ to E₃ a new predicate — reflective SelfDesc — in which the system models its own modeling process rather than merely its distinction. Part VIII investigates this step. The present chapter asks not what consciousness feels like (the hard problem, deferred to Book VII) but what structural conditions a carrier's architecture must satisfy for the system to represent its own representational process. The answer is precise and limited: a system is structurally conscious at E₂ when its internal evaluator takes itself as argument — Eval^(2) = Eval ∘ Eval. Qualia and subjective experience are E₃ phenomena; this chapter provides only the structural definition and proves which Life sector can instantiate it. Scope is τ-effective throughout.

A bacterium running chemotaxis evaluates its chemical environment and adjusts its flagellar motor; it does not represent the fact that it is evaluating. Its SelfDesc is first-order: Eval_X models D_X, not Eval_X. The structural self-model arises when the evaluator takes itself as argument: Eval^(2)_X = Eval_X ∘ Eval_X, with reconstructive fidelity (the self-model tracks the evaluator at every refinement level) and dynamical coherence (when the evaluator is modified by learning, injury, or environment, the self-model tracks the modification). The topology of the consumer sector makes this possible: the fiber torus T² = S¹ × S¹ provides two independent winding directions, so the γ-winding can encode the state of the η-evaluator and vice versa — closing the reflective loop. Sectors operating on a single circle (persistence, agency, source, closure) have π₁ ≅ ℤ, which provides no independent dimension for self-representation. The bridge-head lemma (*VI.L07*) establishes that only the consumer sector's two-dimensional fiber trajectory supports Eval^(2); the Consciousness as Mixed-Sector Self-Modeling theorem follows by two steps. Three constructive consciousness criteria (CC1–CC3) extract operationally testable conditions equivalent to the full structural criterion: consumer-sector topology realized (CC1), Eval^(2) loop closed (CC2), structural self-model maintained (CC3). The Silicon Realization Lemma establishes that no substrate is structurally excluded — there is no structural obstruction to non-biological consciousness — while explicit remarks (*VI.R30*, *VI.R31*) note that empirical identification of consciousness in any specific system is outside the framework's scope, and that current AI architectures (feedforward networks, autoregressive models) typically lack the genuine mixed-sector feedback topology required by CC1.
