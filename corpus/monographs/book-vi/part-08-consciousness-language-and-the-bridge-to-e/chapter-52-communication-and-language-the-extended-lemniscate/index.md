Chapter 51 defined consciousness as the consumer sector's SelfDesc applied to itself — Eval^(2), the evaluator reading its own evaluative process. That definition is intrinsically solitary. This chapter traces what happens when the lemniscate boundary L = S¹ ∨ S¹ of one agent is extended to touch a second agent's boundary through a shared environmental region. Communication is any process transmitting a signal from one evaluator to another through that shared channel; language is the shared code that stabilizes and formalizes the extension. The chapter then synthesizes the computation theme running through the entire book and shows how social structures from bacterial biofilms to human civilizations are extended lemniscate networks of varying topological complexity.

Every living system carries a τ-Distinction: an interior lobe (self) and an exterior lobe (non-self) joined at the wedge point where the evaluator sits. When agent A's exterior lobe overlaps with agent B's exterior lobe through a shared signal channel S, the two individual lemniscates become linked — the extended lemniscate L_ext(A,B) = L_A ∪_S L_B. Communication is the morphism σ_{A→B} : Eval_A → Eval_B passing through S. Language formalizes this as a triple (Σ, E, D) — a finite signal alphabet (grammar), an encoding map (syntax), and a decoding map (semantics) — with faithfulness as the inter-agent closure of the SelfDesc loop: the decoded content matches the encoded content up to basin equivalence. Pragmatics is identified with basin dynamics: the same sentence is decoded to different attractors depending on the receiver's current SelfDesc landscape. The five computational forms running through Book VI — Life Loop Class (metabolic computation), PPAS on genomes (evolutionary computation), neural PPAS (learning computation), Eval^(2) (self-modeling computation), and shared codes (inter-agent computation) — are shown to be nested: each presupposes all preceding forms, and none is reducible to another. Social structures are extended lemniscate networks with varying bandwidth and code richness: bacterial biofilms (chemical quorum sensing), eusocial colonies (pheromone trails and collective distinction maintained by soldier castes), wolf packs (vocalization and posture), and human civilizations (language supporting displacement reference, productivity, and temporal reference, enabling cumulative culture across generations). Institutions — laws, universities, markets — are identified as stabilized attractors in the cultural landscape: shared SelfDesc codes that define a collective distinction, are read by evaluators, and are maintained through enforcement loops. Cultural evolution is the PPAS operating on shared codes, with horizontal inheritance accelerating the cycle time from decades to months.
