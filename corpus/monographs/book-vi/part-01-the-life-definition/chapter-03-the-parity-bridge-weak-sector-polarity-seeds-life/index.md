The passage from physics (E₁) to life (E₂) is not a smooth continuum — it requires a structural seed. This chapter identifies that seed precisely: the maximal parity violation of the weak sector. Among the four primitive holonomy sectors of H_∂ at enrichment level E₁, three — electromagnetic (γ), strong (π), gravitational (α) — admit only trivial polarity functionals into the split-complex idempotent codomain 2_τ. The polarity functional P_𝒮: End(𝒮) → 2_τ asks whether a sector carries an intrinsic orientation asymmetry — a preferred handedness — that could serve as the seed for the self/non-self distinction. For the EM, strong, and gravitational sectors the answer is no: their Lagrangians are parity-invariant, and every endomorphism maps to the same fixed point + in 2_τ. The weak sector (η) is the sole exception. Its V−A Lagrangian structure selects a single chirality at tree level, producing a nontrivial P_weak that maps endomorphisms to both + and − in 2_τ. The parity violation is not approximate but maximal: the W± and Z⁰ bosons couple exclusively to left-handed fermions. This asymmetry is the polarity source. The chapter then introduces three rigorously separated polarity terms — polarity source, polarity basin, and polarity stabilizer — to replace the single overloaded word "chirality" that marred the first edition. A low-noise carrier condition formalises when the seed can actually be amplified: the carrier's internal dynamics must not wash out the asymmetry before SelfDesc closure locks it in. Biological homochirality — the universal preference for L-amino acids and D-sugars — is identified as a conjectural phenomenological shadow of this structural bridge.
