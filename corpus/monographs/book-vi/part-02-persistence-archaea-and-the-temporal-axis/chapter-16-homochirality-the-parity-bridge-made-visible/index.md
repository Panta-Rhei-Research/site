Every protein in every organism on Earth is built from L-amino acids; every nucleic acid uses D-sugars. The enantiomeric excess is exactly 1, without documented exception, across approximately 3.8 billion years. Prebiotic chemistry produces racemic mixtures; the weak-force energy difference between enantiomers (ΔE_LR ~10⁻¹⁷–10⁻¹⁴ eV) is a factor of 10¹¹–10¹³ below thermal energy at biological temperatures; either choice is chemically equivalent in achiral environments. Explaining the universality and absolute nature of biological homochirality has been an open problem since Pasteur's discovery of molecular chirality in 1860. This chapter closes the structural gap by completing a derivation chain that upgrades the homochirality explanation from conjectural to τ-effective, while maintaining strict scope discipline about what the chain does and does not establish.

The Parity Bridge Theorem (VI.T01, Book VI ch. 01) established that among the four primitive holonomy sectors at E₁, only the weak sector admits a nontrivial polarity functional P_weak: End(S_weak) → 2_τ. The electromagnetic, strong, and gravitational sectors are parity-symmetric; their polarity functionals are trivial. The transition E₁ → E₂ factors uniquely through the weak sector. This chapter projects that structural result onto biology through a complete chain: the Polarity Propagation Functor (VI.D71) maps σ_A-admissibility through the Parity Bridge into the polarity functional; the Chirality Seed (VI.D72) is the definite-sign initial asymmetry provided by weak V−A coupling, coherent across all amino acids because it originates from gauge structure rather than molecular geometry; Propagation Preserves Chirality (VI.T41) shows the left-handed input yields definite polarity in 2_τ; Stereochemical Selection (VI.T42) shows SelfDesc closure amplifies ee(n) monotonically toward 1 with gain g > 1 per refinement; Monotone Convergence of ee (VI.P21) proves the limit is 1, protected against regression by Hodge stabilization (energetic barrier Δφ ≫ k_BT) and Poincaré lock-in (topological distinctness of L- and R-loops in π₁(L)); and Homochirality Universality (VI.T43) concludes that all persistence-sector carriers inherit the same chirality. Open problem VI.OP9 is thereby solved.

The scope discipline is precise (VI.R09, VI.R26). What is now τ-effective: the existence of a universal chirality preference among all E₂ carriers. What remains conjectural: whether the specific sign (L rather than R) is determined by the polarity functional's sign at the molecular level or involves a symmetry-breaking bifurcation; whether the four-force amplification mechanism (Yang–Mills seed, Riemann amplification, Hodge stabilization, Poincaré lock-in) is the correct mechanistic account; and whether extraterrestrial life would necessarily exhibit L-amino acid preference rather than R. The shadow metaphor is precise: observing homochirality confirms the Parity Bridge's existence but does not eliminate the possibility of other explanations for the sign.

The chapter belongs to Part II because homochirality is a persistence-sector phenomenon: the chiral choice, once made, is maintained by the same temporal-stability mechanism (VI.D25) that maintains the self/non-self distinction. Active metabolism continuously counteracts racemization; the temporal stability predicate is the formal account of this maintenance.
