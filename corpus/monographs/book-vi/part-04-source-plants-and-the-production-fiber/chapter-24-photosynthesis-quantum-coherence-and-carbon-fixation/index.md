Photosynthesis — 6 CO₂ + 6 H₂O + light → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6 O₂ — is the most important instantiation of the structure generation predicate. The inputs, carbon dioxide and water, are low-complexity inorganic species with Hodge capacity near zero; the output, glucose, carries a free-energy content of 2,870 kJ/mol and participates as the substrate for all downstream structural polymers. Land plants and marine phytoplankton together fix approximately 170 Gt C/yr, sustaining virtually every food web on Earth. This chapter examines how that fixation is accomplished at three scales: the molecular (carbon fixation as the canonical production act), the biochemical (the light reactions in photosystems I and II, and the Calvin cycle), and the quantum (coherent energy transfer in the FMO complex). The biochemistry throughout is established; the categorical interpretation of quantum coherence as a manifestation of the P vs NP force carries a conjectural scope label, which the chapter makes explicit.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** *VI.D38 — Carbon Fixation as Canonical Production* (τ-effective): photosynthetic carbon fixation satisfies all three conditions of the structure generation predicate — w_γ > 0 (net organic carbon produced), C(glucose) ≫ C(CO₂) (complexity increase measured by Hodge capacity), and autotrophy (no organic input required). Carbon fixation is canonical in the sense that every other source-sector act — cellulose synthesis, lignin deposition, secondary metabolism — depends on the organic carbon it supplies.
- **Key results:** *VI.P13 — Quantum Coherence in the FMO Complex* (conjectural): environment-assisted quantum transport (ENAQT) in the Fenna–Matthews–Olson complex is interpreted as a manifestation of the P vs NP force at E₂. This universality — observed across four photosynthetic complexes spanning more than 2 Gyr of evolutionary divergence, all with γ/Δ ∈ [0.8, 1.1] — is the testable prediction; the categorical identification with P vs NP is not experimentally falsifiable in its current formulation.
