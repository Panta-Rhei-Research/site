The ~380,000 described plant species share a single metabolic commitment: oxygenic photosynthesis using chloroplasts descended from endosymbiotic cyanobacteria. Chapter 23 gave the formal sector definition and identified plants as the source archetype; chapter 24 treated the production act at molecular scale. This chapter fills the archetype with biology — the whole organism, its body plan, and the planetary record of source-sector activity. The defining sector-theoretic feature is sessility: land plants have w_π(γ_plant) = 0, no agency component, and the plant's Life loop winds only on α (temporal persistence) and γ (structure generation). This is not a deficiency but the metabolic consequence of maximal γ-dominance — resources that a bacterium allocates to flagellar synthesis and rotation are in a plant redirected entirely to cellulose synthesis, lignin deposition, and leaf expansion. The chapter closes with the Great Oxidation Event (~2.4 Gya), which shows that source-sector production by cyanobacteria transformed Earth's atmosphere, ocean chemistry, and geological record before any plant existed — and thereby enabled the aerobic metabolic complexity on which every subsequent sector depends.

## What this chapter contributes

- **Definitions / Axioms:** *VI.R14 — Plants as Source Archetype* (τ-effective): the kingdom Plantae instantiates all three structure generation conditions in maximal form — photosynthetic carbon fixation (~120 Gt C/yr terrestrial NPP), complexity increase from CO₂ to cellulose and lignin (ΔC > 0 per Calvin cycle), and obligate autotrophy. *VI.R15 — Sessile = Pure Source* (τ-effective): a carrier is a pure source if its Life loop has w_α > 0, w_γ > 0, and w_π = w_η = 0; land plants are the closest biological approximation, with sessility setting w_π = 0 and the absence of decomposition activity keeping w_η negligible.
- **Key results:** No new theorems; VI.R14 and VI.R15 are remarks formalizing the sector-theoretic status of the plant body plan. The architecture survey — cell walls (cellulose, hemicellulose, lignin, pectin), chloroplasts (endosymbiotic origin ~1.5 Gya, retained 70S ribosomes and circular DNA), vascular tissue (xylem driven by transpiration tension; phloem by osmotic pressure) — serves as the biological instantiation table for VI.D37.
- **Notation introduced:** w_π = 0 (sessility condition); NPP (net primary production, ~120 Gt C/yr terrestrial, ~50 Gt C/yr marine); standing biomass figures (~450 Gt C for land plants, ~80% of all living biomass).
- **Dependencies:** VI.R14 depends on VI.D36/VI.D37/VI.T20 (ch. 23) and VI.D38 (ch. 24). The endosymbiotic origin of chloroplasts from cyanobacteria connects to the agency archetype (ch. 18). The Great Oxidation Event section connects forward to the closure archetype (ch. 28–29) by noting that pre-GOE carbon accumulated without recycling — source-sector production without closure-sector return.
